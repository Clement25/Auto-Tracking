# Top AI News (2026-06-12)

## [OpenAI Acquires Ona to Power Longer-Running Codex Coding Agents](https://openai.com/index/openai-to-acquire-ona/)
**Source:** OpenAI / Bloomberg / CNBC
**Summary:** OpenAI announced the acquisition of Ona (formerly Gitpod), a German cloud startup, to enable Codex to run multi-hour and multi-day agentic coding tasks without requiring the user to remain on a single device or active session. Ona's infrastructure gives Codex agents access to the tools, systems, and long-horizon context they need to complete complex work autonomously. This is significant because Codex already serves over 5 million weekly users—up 400% from earlier in 2026—and the deal positions OpenAI to compete directly with enterprise development platforms.

## [Great American AI Act Unveiled: Bipartisan Federal Framework Would Preempt State AI Laws for 3 Years](https://rollcall.com/2026/06/04/bipartisan-ai-draft-proposes-three-year-preemption-of-state-laws/)
**Source:** Roll Call / Nextgov / DLA Piper
**Summary:** Representatives Jay Obernolte (R-CA) and Lori Trahan (D-MA) released a 269-page discussion draft of the Great American Artificial Intelligence Act of 2026, which would establish the first comprehensive federal AI governance framework and freeze new state AI development laws for three years. The bill covers four major areas: Frontier AI Governance, Workforce, Cybersecurity, and R&D/International Cooperation. The preemption clause has sparked significant controversy, as consumer advocates argue it would strip state-level protections at a critical moment for AI regulation.

## [GitHub Copilot Moves to Token-Based AI Credits Billing for Agent Sessions](https://llm-stats.com/ai-news)
**Source:** GitHub / LLM-Stats
**Summary:** GitHub Copilot switched to a token-based AI Credits billing model (1 credit = $0.01), metering agentic task sessions while keeping traditional inline code completions free. The move marks a significant structural shift in how AI coding tools monetize agentic versus completion use cases, and signals that longer-running agent workflows are becoming a distinct and billable product category. Developers using Copilot for agent-driven tasks will now need to manage credit consumption alongside their subscriptions.

## [OpenAI to Retire GPT-4.5 on June 27, Replaced by GPT-5.3](https://llm-stats.com/ai-news)
**Source:** OpenAI / LLM-Stats
**Summary:** OpenAI announced that GPT-4.5 will be retired on June 27, 2026, after a 30-day sunset period, with GPT-5.3 taking its place across the API. This transition also reflects the broader market shift where GPT-4-class models are becoming legacy infrastructure within roughly 18 months of their launch.
