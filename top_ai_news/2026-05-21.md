# Top AI News (2026-05-21)

## [Trump Delays White House AI Executive Order on Pre-Release Model Review](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/21/trump-delays-ai-security-executive-order-i-dont-want-to-get-in-the-way-of-that-leading/)
**Source:** TechCrunch
**Summary:** The White House had prepared an executive order requiring AI companies to submit frontier models for a government security review (up to 90 days) before public release, targeting risks in cybersecurity and national security. President Trump unexpectedly delayed signing, stating he didn't like certain aspects and didn't want anything to slow America's AI leadership over China. The delay leaves a significant governance gap as AI labs race to ship increasingly powerful models.

## [Andrej Karpathy Joins Anthropic's Pretraining Team](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/19/openai-co-founder-andrej-karpathy-joins-anthropics-pre-training-team/)
**Source:** TechCrunch
**Summary:** OpenAI co-founder and former Tesla AI director Andrej Karpathy joined Anthropic on May 19 to lead a new group within the pretraining team focused on using Claude to accelerate AI research itself. Karpathy, who had most recently run AI-education startup Eureka Labs, chose Anthropic over a return to OpenAI—a signal that pretraining research is far from finished and that AI-assisted research is a genuine competitive lever.

## [Google I/O 2026: Gemini Omni, 3.5 Flash, Managed Agents, and Agent-First Platform](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/ai/google-io-2026-all-our-announcements/)
**Source:** Google Blog / The Verge
**Summary:** Google unveiled Gemini Omni (multimodal creation from any input, with video generation and editing), Gemini 3.5 Flash (frontier intelligence rivaling large models at fast-inference speeds), and Managed Agents in the Gemini API (single API call spins up a remote Linux environment for agentic reasoning, tool use, and web browsing). Google also announced a $100/month AI Ultra plan for developers and expanded SynthID content authentication to OpenAI, Kakao, and Eleven Labs. The announcements mark a decisive shift from assistant to autonomous agent as Google's primary product paradigm.

## [Federal Jury Dismisses Elon Musk's Lawsuit Against OpenAI](https://www.crescendo.ai/news/latest-ai-news-and-updates)
**Source:** Multiple outlets (Reuters / The Verge)
**Summary:** A federal jury unanimously rejected Elon Musk's lawsuit against OpenAI and CEO Sam Altman, ruling that the claims were filed outside the statute of limitations. The verdict removes a major legal overhang for OpenAI ahead of its anticipated IPO and lets the company proceed with its nonprofit-to-for-profit conversion without court interference.

## [Meta Begins 10% Workforce Reduction Tied to AI Restructuring](https://www.crescendo.ai/news/latest-ai-news-and-updates)
**Source:** VentureBeat / Bloomberg
**Summary:** Meta launched the first wave of a planned ~8,000-person layoff (roughly 10% of its workforce) as part of an efficiency push tied to heavy AI infrastructure spending. Simultaneously, approximately 7,000 employees were reassigned to new AI-focused teams. The move illustrates how large tech companies are trading headcount for AI leverage, even as critics warn of accelerating displacement across knowledge-work roles.

## [Cerebras Runs Kimi K2.6 Trillion-Parameter Model at ~1,000 Tokens/Second](https://venturebeat.com/)
**Source:** VentureBeat
**Summary:** Cerebras Systems announced it is serving Moonshot AI's Kimi K2.6—a one-trillion-parameter open-weight model—to enterprise customers at nearly 1,000 tokens per second, following the company's recent IPO. The milestone demonstrates that extreme inference speed at trillion-parameter scale is now commercially viable, dramatically changing the economics of deploying frontier open-weight models in production.

## [Google Unveils Gemini Spark: A 24/7 Personal AI Agent](https://venturebeat.com/)
**Source:** VentureBeat
**Summary:** Alongside its I/O model announcements, Google revealed Gemini Spark, a personal AI agent designed to run continuously—drafting emails, assembling documents, monitoring inboxes, and making purchases—even when the user's devices are idle. Spark represents Google's answer to the "always-on agent" category, competing directly with Microsoft Copilot and Apple Intelligence in the ambient computing space.

## [AI Search Startups Surge: Exa Labs Raises $250M at $2.2B Valuation](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/20/ai-search-startups-are-blowing-up/)
**Source:** TechCrunch
**Summary:** A16z-backed Exa Labs closed a $250 million round at a $2.2 billion valuation, joining a wave of startups targeting AI-native search as one of the hottest verticals in consumer AI. ## [Runway Valued at $5.3B, Bets That Video—Not Text—Is the Foundation of Intelligence](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/15/runway-started-by-helping-filmmakers-now-it-wants-to-beat-google-at-ai/)

**Summary:** Video-generation company Runway reached a $5.3 billion valuation and added $40 million in ARR in Q2 2026. Its founders argue that the next generation of truly intelligent AI will be trained on video and world models—not text—because video encodes how the world physically works, not just how humans describe it. The company is positioning itself to compete directly with Google and OpenAI on foundation model development.

## [Thinking Machines Lab (Mira Murati) Demos Simultaneous Listen-and-Speak AI](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/11/thinking-machines-wants-to-build-an-ai-that-actually-listens-while-it-talks/)
