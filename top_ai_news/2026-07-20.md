## [EU Forces Google to Open Android to Rival AI Assistants Under Digital Markets Act](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-july-20-2026-16-biggest-stories)
**Source:** BuildFastWithAI / Multiple outlets
**Summary:** The European Commission issued binding requirements under the Digital Markets Act ordering Google to allow rival AI assistants full voice activation and cross-app access on Android across 11 feature groups — subject to user consent and certification — effective July 2027. Google must also begin sharing anonymized search ranking, query, click, and view data with AI developers on fair and nondiscriminatory terms starting January 2027. This is the most consequential regulatory intervention in AI distribution to date and could meaningfully shift the competitive dynamics of AI assistant deployment on mobile.

**Summary:** OpenAI CEO Sam Altman proposed ceding a 5% equity stake — valued at approximately $42.6 billion based on the company's $852 billion valuation — to the US government via a sovereign wealth fund vehicle, as part of efforts to neutralize political opposition to OpenAI's for-profit conversion. ## [Etched Raises $800M and Exits Stealth with Transformer-Only AI Inference Chip](https://finance.yahoo.com/technology/ai/articles/etched-raises-800-million-ai-202858979.html)
**Source:** Yahoo Finance / TechTimes
**Summary:** Transformer-chip startup Etched exited stealth in late June with $800 million raised, over $1 billion in signed customer contracts, and a working A0 silicon chip called Sohu built on TSMC's 4nm process. Sohu is hardwired to run only transformer-based models, trading generality for dramatic inference throughput advantages over general-purpose GPUs. Previously undisclosed investors include Jane Street and TSMC's own venture arm, underscoring the financial world's bet that inference economics will favor specialized silicon.
