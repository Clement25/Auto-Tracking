## [EU AI Act Omnibus: High-Risk AI Obligations Postponed from August 2026 to December 2027](https://www.globalpolicywatch.com/2026/06/eu-ai-act-update-timeline-relief-targeted-simplification-and-new-prohibitions-2/)
**Source:** Global Policy Watch / EU AI Act tracker
**Summary:** On May 7, 2026, the EU Council, Parliament, and Commission reached a provisional agreement on the Digital Omnibus, the first set of amendments to the EU AI Act since its adoption in June 2024, postponing high-risk AI system obligations by 16 months to December 2, 2027. The amendments also introduce two new prohibited practices taking effect December 2026: using AI to generate non-consensual intimate material and child sexual abuse material. A final Code of Practice on AI-generated content labeling is expected to be published later in June 2026, giving providers standardized methods for implementing transparency obligations under Article 50.

## [Colorado Consumer Protections for AI Act Takes Effect June 30, Affecting High-Risk AI Deployments](https://www.resemble.ai/resources/the-eu-ai-act-what-generative-ai-companies-need-to-know-in-2026)
**Source:** Resemble AI / Pearl Cohen Legal
**Summary:** The Colorado Consumer Protections for Artificial Intelligence Act enters force on June 30, 2026, applying to deployers and developers of high-risk AI systems serving Colorado residents across employment, healthcare, financial services, education, housing, and legal services. Covered entities must implement a risk management program, conduct annual impact assessments, and provide disclosure when AI is used for consequential decisions. The law is being closely watched as a potential model for federal AI regulation, making it one of the most significant U.S. state AI laws to date.
