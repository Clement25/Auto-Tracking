# Top AI News (2026-07-09)

## [OpenAI Launches GPT-5.6: Sol, Terra, and Luna Now Generally Available](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-6/)
**Source:** OpenAI / 9to5Mac / Neowin
**Summary:** OpenAI made GPT-5.6 generally available on July 9, 2026, introducing three tiers: Sol ($5/$30 per Mtok) for frontier reasoning and long-horizon agentic work; Terra ($2.50/$15) as a balanced everyday model at 2× lower cost than its predecessor; and Luna ($1/$6) as the fastest, most affordable tier. Sol sets new SOTA on Terminal-Bench 2.1 and introduces "Ultra" mode — a multi-agent parallel workstream capability for complex tasks. This is the single most significant model day in OpenAI history, available globally across ChatGPT, Codex, and the API.

## [SpaceXAI Releases Grok 4.5: Opus-Class Performance at Lower Cost](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/08/spacexai-releases-grok-4-5-which-elon-describes-as-an-opus-class-model/)
**Source:** TechCrunch / Axios / SpaceXAI
**Summary:** SpaceXAI launched Grok 4.5 on July 8, its first model release since going public and acquiring coding startup Cursor. Grok 4.5 supports a 500K-token context window, function calling, web search, code execution, and configurable reasoning — priced at just $2/$6 per million tokens (input/output). ## [Google Delays Gemini 3.5 Pro to July 17 After Full Architectural Rebuild](https://finance.biggo.com/news/6f0c6bb2-795f-4c57-9d09-6db691d7638a)
**Source:** BigGo Finance / ThursdAI
**Summary:** Google DeepMind pushed Gemini 3.5 Pro's launch to July 17, 2026, after abandoning the existing Gemini 2.5 Pro architecture for a ground-up redesign. The delay — the model's second missed deadline — is attributed to excessive token consumption in extended agentic tasks and long-horizon performance gaps. The rebuilt model targets a 2M token context window, a Deep Think Reasoning Layer, and autonomous workflow capabilities to compete with GPT-5.6 and Fable 5.
