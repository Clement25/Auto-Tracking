## [Apple Sues OpenAI for Trade Secret Theft, Naming Hardware Chief Tang Tan](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/10/apple-openai-lawsuit-trade-secrets.html)
**Source:** CNBC / CNN Business / Tech-ish
**Summary:** Apple filed a federal lawsuit in Northern California on July 10, 2026 accusing OpenAI of systematically stealing hardware trade secrets, alleging that at every level—from Technical Staff to Chief Hardware Officer—OpenAI employees extracted confidential Apple data. The suit names OpenAI's hardware subsidiary io Products and two former Apple executives now at OpenAI: hardware chief Tang Tan (ex-Apple VP) and engineer Chang Liu, who allegedly downloaded dozens of confidential files before departing in January 2026. The lawsuit is a shocking reversal given the two companies' 2024 partnership integrating ChatGPT into iOS—made more pointed by Apple's decision to replace Siri's backend with Google's Gemini for its upcoming fall release.

**Source:** 9to5Mac / OpenAI Release Notes

**Summary:** South Korea's SK Hynix listed on Nasdaq on July 10, 2026 under ticker SKHY, closing up 13% at $168.01 per ADR and raising $26.5 billion—the largest US IPO ever by a foreign company, 7x oversubscribed. ## [US Eases AI Chip Export Controls to UAE, Granting License-Free Access for G42, xAI, Amazon](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/10/trump-uae-mgx-crypto-export-commerce-warren.html)
**Source:** CNBC / Khaleej Times
**Summary:** The US Department of Commerce reclassified the UAE into country group A:5 on July 10, 2026, allowing license-free exports of advanced AI chips and servers to UAE entities including G42, Core42, and US companies operating there such as Amazon, Apple, xAI, Google, Meta, Microsoft, OpenAI, and Oracle. The policy shift recognizes the UAE as a US Major Defense Partner and follows Operation Epic Fury cooperation; Senator Elizabeth Warren called a provision for UAE crypto firm MGX "corrupt." The move significantly accelerates Gulf AI infrastructure buildout and deepens US-UAE technology ties amid competition with China for regional AI influence.

## [Google Gemini 2.5 Pro Deep Think Tops Science and Reasoning Benchmarks](https://blog.google/products/gemini/gemini-2-5-deep-think/)
**Source:** Google Blog / ByteIota
**Summary:** Google's Gemini 2.5 Pro with Deep Think reasoning mode, launched in late June 2026, achieved 82.4% on GPQA Diamond, 89.8% on MMLU-Pro, and 94.1% on HumanEval+—surpassing both OpenAI's GPT-5.5 and Anthropic's Fable 5 on science and coding benchmarks. Unlike OpenAI's approach of shipping a separate reasoning model, Deep Think is a toggle on the existing Gemini 2.5 Pro that runs multiple parallel reasoning paths and selects the best answer, at roughly 4x the token cost. An advanced research version achieved gold-medal standard at the 2025 International Mathematical Olympiad.

## [Meta Lays Off 8,000 Employees in AI Restructuring; Zuckerberg Admits AI Progress Slower Than Expected](https://www.npr.org/2026/05/20/nx-s1-5826917/meta-layoffs-ai-jobs)
**Source:** NPR / Yahoo Finance / Gulf Business
**Summary:** Meta executed a wave of roughly 8,000 layoffs (≈10% of workforce) starting in May 2026, targeting integrity, cybersecurity, and Reality Labs teams while redirecting 7,000 workers into new AI-focused units including Applied AI Engineering and Agent Transformation Accelerator. ## [Qualcomm in Early Talks to Acquire AI Chip Startup Tenstorrent for Up to $10 Billion](https://www.theregister.com/systems/2026/06/16/qualcomm-said-to-be-circling-ai-chip-biz-tenstorrent-in-10b-risc-v-power-play/)
**Source:** The Register / Yahoo Finance / Data Center Dynamics
**Summary:** Qualcomm is in early-stage negotiations to acquire Tenstorrent, the RISC-V AI accelerator startup founded by legendary chip architect Jim Keller, in a deal valued between $8–10 billion, Reuters reported in mid-June 2026. Tenstorrent raised $693 million in a December 2024 Series D at a $2.6B+ valuation backed by Jeff Bezos, Samsung Securities, and others, and has since grown significantly on the strength of its open RISC-V architecture. The deal would give Qualcomm a strong foothold in data-center AI inference silicon and a credible path to challenging Nvidia's dominance using an open-architecture stack.

## [Marc Andreessen Named Co-Lead of Federal Reserve AI Task Force on Jobs and Productivity](https://aitoolsrecap.com/Blog/ai-news-july-11-2026)
**Source:** AIToolsRecap
**Summary:** The Federal Reserve appointed a16z co-founder Marc Andreessen to co-lead a new task force studying AI's impact on employment, productivity, and monetary policy—a notable move that embeds a prominent Silicon Valley venture capitalist at the heart of US macroeconomic policymaking. Andreessen's selection signals growing recognition that AI's economic disruption is now central to monetary policy considerations, though it has drawn concern from critics over potential conflicts of interest.
