## [Trump Signs Executive Order on AI Innovation and Security](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/06/promoting-advanced-artificial-intelligence-innovation-and-security/)
**Source:** The White House / MIT Technology Review
**Summary:** President Trump signed "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security" on June 2, 2026, directing national security and civilian agencies to intensify scrutiny of frontier AI models and bolster federal cybersecurity against AI-enabled threats. The order creates a voluntary regulatory regime for frontier AI developers, requiring safety evaluations before deployment of models above a compute threshold. This is the most significant federal AI action since Biden's 2023 EO and sets the stage for the Great American AI Act currently in Congressional markup.

## [The Great American AI Act of 2026 Discussion Draft Released](https://www.mcdonaldhopkins.com/insights/news/the-great-american-ai-act-what-businesses-need-to-know)
**Source:** McDonald Hopkins / Wilson Sonsini
**Summary:** Representatives Jay Obernolte (R-CA) and Lori Trahan (D-MA) released the discussion draft of the Great American Artificial Intelligence Act of 2026 (GAAIA) on June 4. The bipartisan bill would establish federal standards for high-risk AI systems, preempt a patchwork of state laws, and create a new AI Safety Institute with enforcement authority. With the Colorado AI Act taking effect June 30, businesses face a rapidly closing window to align with both state and pending federal requirements simultaneously.

## [Colorado AI Act Takes Effect June 30 — Compliance Deadline Looms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colorado_AI_Act)
**Source:** MIT Technology Review / Wilson Sonsini
**Summary:** Colorado's landmark AI Act, the first comprehensive state AI law in the U.S., comes into force on June 30, 2026, imposing substantial obligations on AI developers and deployers to avoid algorithmic discrimination and provide meaningful human oversight for consequential decisions. Enterprises that use AI in hiring, lending, housing, or healthcare in Colorado must have impact assessments and notice mechanisms in place by month-end. Legal experts are watching closely as it will set precedent for enforcement of similar bills in 10+ other states.
