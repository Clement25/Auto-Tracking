## [Anthropic's Mythos Model Finds Tens of Thousands of Software Vulnerabilities](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/05/anthropic-ceo-cyber-moment-of-danger-mythos-vulnerabilities.html)
**Source:** CNBC
**Summary:** Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei warned of a narrow "moment of danger" as the company's unreleased Mythos model has autonomously discovered tens of thousands of software vulnerabilities across legacy systems—including nearly 300 critical ones. Anthropic launched Project Glasswing, giving select approved organizations (AWS, Apple, Cisco, Google, JPMorgan, Microsoft) controlled access to Claude Mythos Preview to patch vulnerabilities before they can be exploited. This marks the first time an AI system has been described as a serious force multiplier for both offensive and defensive cybersecurity at scale.

## [Microsoft, Google DeepMind & xAI Agree to Pre-Deployment Government Testing](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/05/ai-oversight-trump-google-microsoft-xai.html)
**Source:** CNBC / CNN Business
**Summary:** The U.S. Commerce Department's Center for AI Standards and Innovation (CAISI) announced agreements with Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and xAI to provide early government access to frontier AI models before public release for national security evaluation. Anthropic, notably absent from the initial signatories, has separately briefed senior U.S. government officials on the Mythos model.

## [UIUC/Stanford Framework Lets AI Agents Share Embeddings, Cutting Training Costs 50%+](https://venturebeat.com/category/ai)
**Source:** VentureBeat
**Summary:** Researchers from UIUC and Stanford introduced a new multi-agent communication framework where agents share raw embeddings instead of natural language text, dramatically reducing inter-agent token usage and cutting training costs by more than half. The approach enables much denser information transfer between specialized agents without the verbosity overhead of language. This could significantly lower the infrastructure cost of large-scale multi-agent pipelines.
