## [Amazon Launches New $1 Billion FDE Org, Following OpenAI and Anthropic](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/30/amazon-launches-new-1-billion-fde-org-following-openai-and-anthropic/)

## [GitHub Copilot's First Full Month of Usage-Based Billing: Agentic Users Face 10x–50x Cost Surge](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/319340/20260629/github-copilot-billing-shock-confirmed-agentic-users-face-10x-cost-surge.htm)
**Source:** TechTimes / The Register / gHacks
**Summary:** June 30 marked the end of the first full billing cycle after GitHub Copilot switched from flat-rate to metered token billing on June 1. Developers in agentic coding workflows reported monthly costs jumping from $29 to $750 or from $50 to $3,000, with some extreme cases far higher. GitHub has not reversed the pricing change, instead pointing users to spending limits and usage dashboards — but the backlash is fueling accelerated adoption of alternatives.

## [Anthropic in Early-Stage Talks with Microsoft to Run Claude on Custom Maia 200 Chips](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/21/anthropic-microsoft-maia-200-ai-chip.html)
**Source:** CNBC / Techzine
**Summary:** Anthropic is negotiating to run Claude inference workloads on Microsoft's proprietary Maia 200 AI accelerator via Azure — a move that could reduce Anthropic's dependence on Nvidia GPUs and potentially lower inference costs by up to 30%. The Maia 200, built on TSMC's 3nm process, is already handling OpenAI's GPT-5.2 inference; adding Claude would make it the first major third-party frontier model on custom Microsoft silicon.

## [Colorado AI Consumer Protection Law Takes Effect — Then Is Immediately Superseded](https://www.bhfs.com/insight/colorados-landmark-ai-law-coming-online-what-developers-and-deployers-should-know/)
**Source:** Brownstein / Norton Rose Fulbright / TroutmanPrivacy
**Summary:** Colorado's original SB24-205 AI Act formally took effect on June 30, applying duty-of-care obligations to high-risk AI systems in employment, healthcare, financial services, and housing — but the law was already replaced in May 2026 by SB26-189, which shifts to a disclosure-based framework effective January 1, 2027. The episode highlights the turbulent pace of AI regulation, with states struggling to finalize rules before the technology outpaces the legislative process.
