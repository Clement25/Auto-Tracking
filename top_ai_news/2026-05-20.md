# Top AI News (2026-05-20)

## [Google I/O 2026: Gemini Omni, Gemini 3.5 Flash, Gemini Spark & Antigravity 2.0](https://www.eweek.com/news/google-io-gemini-agentic-ai-era-2026/)
**Source:** eWeek / Cybernews / Tom's Guide
**Summary:** Google's I/O 2026 keynote marked a full pivot to "agentic AI," unveiling Gemini Omni (a multimodal world model that creates video from any input), Gemini 3.5 Flash (4× faster than rival frontier models, with top coding and agentic benchmark scores), and Gemini Spark — a persistent agent running 24/7 on dedicated cloud VMs. In a live demo, Antigravity 2.0 (powered by Gemini 3.5 Flash) built a functioning OS from scratch in 12 hours using 93 subagents and ~$1,000 in API credits, signaling that multi-agent software engineering is now production-ready. The Gemini app has also crossed 900 million monthly active users, doubling year-over-year.

## [Google to Retire the Traditional Search Box — AI Overhaul Begins](https://cybernews.com/ai-news/google-io-2026-gemini-omni-antigravity-agentic-ai/)
**Source:** Cybernews / VentureBeat
**Summary:** After 25 years, Google is formally retiring its traditional search paradigm, replacing the classic search box with an AI-first interface driven by Gemini. This marks one of the most significant structural shifts in consumer internet history, as the world's dominant search engine transitions to conversational, agentic retrieval. The move is widely expected to reshape the entire search advertising market and accelerate the AI search startup wave.

## [Microsoft, Google, and xAI Will Let the US Government Test AI Models Before Launch](https://www.cnn.com/2026/05/05/tech/microsoft-google-xai-government-test-ai-models)
**Source:** CNN Business
**Summary:** Microsoft, Google DeepMind, and xAI committed to allowing the US government — through the Department of Commerce's Center for AI Standards and Innovation (CAISI) — to conduct national security assessments of their frontier models before public release. It reflects growing concern over dual-use capabilities in next-generation models.

## [An AI Agent Now Manages Other AI Agents — Intercom's New Orchestration Layer](https://venturebeat.com/)
**Source:** VentureBeat
**Summary:** The company formerly known as Intercom has deployed a production AI agent whose sole purpose is to manage and orchestrate other AI agents, marking a new milestone in agentic infrastructure. The "meta-agent" monitors subagent performance, reallocates tasks, and handles failure recovery — effectively automating the coordination layer that human engineers previously managed. This is an early signal that agentic systems are beginning to self-organize at an operational scale.
