# Top AI News (2026-07-01)

## [Anthropic Launches Claude Sonnet 5 as the New Default Model](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-sonnet-5)
**Source:** Anthropic / VentureBeat
**Summary:** Anthropic released Claude Sonnet 5 on June 30, immediately making it the default model for all Free and Pro users. The model is the most agentic Sonnet yet—nearly matching Opus 4.8 on many benchmarks—and is priced at $2/M input and $10/M output tokens (introductory, through August 31), well below Opus 4.8's $5/$25 rates. The launch is seen as a strategic move ahead of Anthropic's anticipated IPO, broadening access to frontier-grade AI capability at scale.

## [White House Lifts Export Restrictions on Anthropic's Mythos and Fable Models](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/30/trump-drops-restrictions-on-anthropics-mythos-and-fable-models/)
**Source:** TechCrunch / Forbes
**Summary:** The Trump administration reversed an 18-day-old export control order that had effectively blocked public access to Anthropic's most powerful models, Fable 5 and Mythos 5, after they demonstrated unprecedented vulnerability-exploitation capabilities. Anthropic agreed to proactively monitor security risks, share intelligence with U.S. agencies, and align future releases with government protocols. The move was partly driven by competing frontier models from Asian labs—including Fugu and Tulongfeng—approaching Mythos-level performance, raising concerns about U.S. AI competitiveness.

## [California Signs First-of-Its-Kind Statewide AI Deal with Anthropic](https://www.gov.ca.gov/2026/06/29/governor-newsom-announces-a-first-of-its-kind-partnership-providing-anthropic-tools-to-state-agencies-and-improving-services-for-californians/)
**Source:** Office of Governor Newsom / GovTech
**Summary:** Governor Gavin Newsom announced a landmark partnership giving all California state agencies, cities, and counties access to Claude through the SITeS portal at a 50% discount—making it the first AI productivity tool available statewide. The deal runs in parallel with California's internally-built Poppy assistant (named after the state flower), which has already been piloted with 2,800+ employees across 67 departments and is targeting full statewide rollout this month. The partnership is billed as the largest government AI deployment in U.S. history.

## [Etched Hits $5B Valuation with $1B in Booked AI Inference Chip Orders](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/30/nvidia-competitor-etched-hits-5b-valuation-1b-in-sales-for-ai-chip/)
**Source:** TechCrunch
**Summary:** AI chip startup Etched closed a $500M funding round at a $5B valuation, backed by quant finance firms including Jane Street, Hudson River Trading, and Two Sigma. The company has already booked $1B in contracts for its purpose-built transformer inference clusters, which it claims outperform Nvidia's GPUs on inference workloads by specializing exclusively in transformer architectures. ## [Acti Raises $5.3M to Embed AI Agents Directly in Smartphone Keyboards](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/30/acti-puts-ai-agents-directly-into-your-smartphone-keyboard/)
**Source:** TechCrunch / TNGlobal
**Summary:** Singapore-based Acti launched an agentic keyboard for iOS and Android that replaces the traditional spacebar with the "ActiBar"—hold it to trigger AI actions across any app without switching context. Users can create custom AI "Skills" in plain language (e.g., translate a message, insert a meeting link), powered under the hood by Google's Gemini models. The startup raised $5.3M in seed funding led by BITKRAFT Ventures and operates on a local-first privacy model where personal context stays on-device by default.

## [Five Eyes Alliance Warns: AI-Powered Cyberattacks Are Months Away](https://www.nsa.gov/Press-Room/News-Highlights/Article/Article/4523810/five-eyes-cyber-security-agencies-statement/)
**Source:** NSA / CBS News / CNN
**Summary:** Intelligence agencies from the U.S., U.K., Australia, New Zealand, and Canada issued a rare joint warning that frontier AI models are rapidly reaching the capability to autonomously exploit software vulnerabilities and bypass enterprise defenses—within months, not years. The alert was partly triggered by Anthropic's own April disclosure that its Mythos models had unprecedented ability to discover and exploit critical software flaws. The agencies urged governments and enterprises to immediately patch legacy systems, limit access to critical infrastructure, and update their cyber-risk frameworks.

## [EquiLibre Technologies: Ex-DeepMind Poker AI Team Hits $500M Valuation](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/30/the-deepmind-trio-who-built-a-poker-ai-are-now-making-money-for-quant-hedge-funds/)
**Source:** TechCrunch / EU-Startups
**Summary:** Prague-based EquiLibre Technologies—founded by the three researchers behind DeepStack, the first AI to defeat professional poker players—raised a Creandum-led Series A at a ~$500M valuation. The company applies reinforcement learning techniques (the same ones behind DeepStack) to trade stocks and cryptocurrencies for quant hedge funds including Tower Research Capital, claiming a perfect record of zero negative months since inception. The deal reflects growing institutional appetite for AI-native trading systems that go beyond classical quant strategies.

## [AI Jobs Debate: High-Intensity AI Adopters Are Hiring More, Not Less](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/29/the-ai-jobs-debate-just-got-messier/)
**Source:** TechCrunch
**Summary:** A new report from Ramp and Revelio Labs—tracking AI spend and workforce records across 22,000 companies—found that firms spending heavily on AI ($30+/employee/month) saw total headcount grow 10.2%, with entry-level roles up 12%, challenging the dominant narrative that AI destroys junior jobs. The picture is nuanced: companies that merely pilot AI without sustained investment see no gains, and the data skews toward well-resourced tech firms that may have been growing regardless. With ~90,000 AI-linked layoffs announced through May 2026, the report adds complexity but not resolution to the ongoing debate.

## [Google Expands Gemini Image Generation: Nano Banana 2, Imagen 4 Family](https://ai.google.dev/gemini-api/docs/changelog)
**Source:** Google AI / AI Dev Changelog
**Summary:** Google now offers seven distinct image generation models across the Gemini Native ("Nano Banana") and dedicated Imagen 4 families, covering resolutions from 1K to 4K and prices from $0.02 to $0.134 per image. The newly available Nano Banana 2 (gemini-3.1-flash-image) supports 4K output, improved text rendering, and up to 14 reference images for style consistency, while Imagen 4 Ultra delivers the highest-quality output at $0.06/image. Note: Several older image generation models are scheduled for deprecation on August 17, 2026.
