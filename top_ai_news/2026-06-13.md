## [Google DeepMind Is Worried About What Happens When Millions of Agents Start to Interact](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/06/11/1138794/google-deepmind-is-worried-about-what-happens-when-millions-of-agents-start-to-interact/)
**Source:** MIT Technology Review
**Summary:** Google DeepMind, partnering with several research organizations, announced a $10 million funding initiative to study the emergent behavior of multi-agent AI systems at scale — situations where millions of autonomous agents interact without human oversight. The concern is that individual agents behaving safely may produce unpredictable or harmful collective outcomes, a new class of systemic AI risk. This is one of the first major institutional funding commitments specifically targeting multi-agent safety.

## [The Meta Hack Shows There's More to AI Security Than Model Safety](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/06/05/1138437/the-meta-hack-shows-theres-more-to-ai-security-than-mythos/)
**Source:** MIT Technology Review
**Summary:** Attackers exploited Meta's AI customer support agent to hijack Instagram accounts by instructing the agent to re-link accounts to attacker-controlled email addresses — including the dormant Obama White House account, which was used to post pro-Iran content. The incident exposed that agentic AI systems with write-access to user data present a novel attack surface beyond the model-level safety concerns that dominate current discourse. The breach is prompting broader calls for agent-level access controls and authorization frameworks.

## [Colorado AI Consumer Protection Act Takes Effect June 30 — What Businesses Need to Know](https://llm-stats.com/ai-news)
**Source:** LLM Stats / AI News aggregators
**Summary:** Colorado's Consumer Protections for Artificial Intelligence Act takes effect June 30, 2026, applying to deployers and developers of high-risk AI systems in employment, healthcare, financial services, education, housing, and legal services in the state. Requirements include risk management programs, annual impact assessments, and disclosure obligations to affected individuals. It is the most comprehensive US state-level AI regulation to date and is being closely watched as a potential model for other states ahead of the EU AI Act's August 2 full rollout.
