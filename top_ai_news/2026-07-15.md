# Top AI News (2026-07-15)

## [Thinking Machines Releases Inkling, Its First Open-Weight AI Model](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/thinking-machines-amps-up-its-bet-against-one-size-fits-all-ai-with-its-first-open-model-inkling/)
**Source:** TechCrunch / Axios / Bloomberg
**Summary:** Mira Murati's Thinking Machines (valued at $12B, backed by Nvidia) released Inkling, a 975B-parameter mixture-of-experts model that activates ~41B parameters per task, trained on 45 trillion tokens of text, image, audio, and video. Unlike OpenAI or Anthropic flagship models, Inkling is open-weight—downloadable and fine-tunable on Hugging Face—and is designed for enterprise customization rather than raw capability. This marks a significant bet that the future of AI is specialized, not one-size-fits-all.

## [Apple Intelligence Gets China Approval via Alibaba's Qwen](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/apple-intelligence-approved-for-launch-in-china-with-alibabas-qwen-ai/)
**Source:** TechCrunch
**Summary:** Chinese regulators approved Apple Intelligence for launch in China through a partnership with Alibaba that integrates Qwen AI models into iOS, iPadOS, macOS, and visionOS. This follows months of regulatory negotiations and represents Apple's strategy of using local AI partners to satisfy government data-residency requirements. The deal gives Apple access to China's 1.4B+ user market for its AI features while giving Alibaba's Qwen a major distribution channel.

## [AI Safety Index 2026: No Lab Scores Above C+](https://futureoflife.org/ai-safety-index-summer-2026/)
**Source:** Future of Life Institute / Time / IEEE Spectrum
**Summary:** The Future of Life Institute released its Summer 2026 AI Safety Index evaluating nine labs on 37 indicators across six domains. Anthropic led with a C+, followed by OpenAI and Google DeepMind at C, Meta at D+, while xAI, DeepSeek, and Mistral effectively failed. The report's most alarming finding: multiple major labs have quietly walked back earlier safety commitments and several are pivoting toward military AI applications, raising governance alarm bells across the research community.

## [OpenAI's First Hardware Device: A Screenless AI Speaker for the Home](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/14/openais-first-hardware-device-is-reportedly-a-screenless-speaker-that-can-move/)

## [South Korea Announces $880 Billion AI and Chip Investment Plan](https://unrot.co/blogs/today-top-10-ai-news-july-15-2026)
**Source:** Multiple outlets
**Summary:** South Korea announced a ten-year national investment plan of approximately 1,350 trillion won (~$880B), including ~$518B for memory chip factories, ~$550B for AI data centers, a target of 8.4 GW of data-center power by 2029, and a push to grow its humanoid robot market share from 1% to 20% by 2028. This is one of the largest single-nation AI investment commitments ever made and reflects Seoul's strategic intent to challenge US and Chinese dominance in AI infrastructure and robotics.

## [Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella Warns Companies Are "Paying Twice" for AI](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/13/satya-nadella-has-issued-a-shocking-warning-to-companies-using-ai/)
**Source:** TechCrunch
**Summary:** Satya Nadella warned enterprises that AI adoption as currently structured means companies pay twice—once in token costs to run AI models, and again by handing proprietary data to AI providers who can learn from it. The warning is seen as strategic positioning for Microsoft's own AI products, but echoes growing enterprise concerns about data sovereignty and vendor lock-in as AI costs scale. It also puts pressure on all major AI providers to offer clearer data-use policies.

## [New York Times Files for Sanctions Against OpenAI Over Withheld Training Data](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-july-15-2026)
**Source:** Multiple outlets
**Summary:** The New York Times and allied publishers filed a motion seeking sanctions against OpenAI for allegedly withholding training-data evidence central to their copyright infringement lawsuit. The motion escalates a case that could set landmark precedent for whether training frontier AI models on copyrighted journalism without permission is lawful. A ruling favorable to publishers could force fundamental changes in how AI labs source and document training data.
