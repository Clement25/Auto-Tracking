## [Anthropic Launches Claude Tag: A Persistent AI Teammate Inside Slack](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/23/anthropics-claude-tag-is-learning-your-company-one-slack-message-at-a-time/)
**Source:** TechCrunch / VentureBeat / Fortune
**Summary:** Anthropic launched Claude Tag on June 23, a beta product that embeds an ambient AI agent directly into Slack workspaces for Enterprise and Team customers. Unlike a simple chatbot, Claude Tag breaks tasks into stages, works independently through approved tools and codebases, and proactively follows up—essentially acting as a persistent virtual employee. Notably, 65% of Anthropic's own product team's code is already being submitted by the internal version of Claude Tag, a striking proof-of-concept for autonomous AI at work.

## [SpaceX Acquires AI Coding Assistant Cursor for $60 Billion](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/16/spacex-spcx-cursor-acquisition-ipo.html)
**Source:** CNBC / CBS News / Yahoo Finance / DevOps.com
**Summary:** SpaceX announced on June 16 the acquisition of Anysphere (maker of Cursor) in a $60B all-stock deal—the largest-ever acquisition of a venture-backed startup. Cursor reached ~$4B annualised revenue in under four years, driven by explosive enterprise adoption of its AI coding assistant. The deal positions SpaceX as a major enterprise AI infrastructure player immediately after its record-breaking Nasdaq IPO, and signals that AI developer tooling has become a tier-1 strategic asset.

## [Oracle Cuts 21,000 Jobs, Explicitly Blames AI Adoption](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/23/oracle-ai-job-cuts-layoffs-21000.html)
**Source:** CNBC / Bloomberg / Tom's Hardware
**Summary:** Oracle disclosed in its FY26 annual filing that it shed approximately 21,000 employees (nearly 13% of its workforce) over the past year, spending $1.8B in restructuring costs. The company explicitly stated that "adoption and deployment of AI technologies across our operations have resulted, and may continue to result, in reductions to our workforce"—one of the most direct public attributions of mass layoffs to internal AI deployment by a major tech company to date. Sales and marketing saw the sharpest cuts (~19%).
