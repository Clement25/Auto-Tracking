# Top AI News (2026-05-24)

## [OpenAI Model Disproves Erdős 80-Year Geometry Conjecture](https://openai.com/index/model-disproves-discrete-geometry-conjecture/)
**Source:** TechCrunch / OpenAI
**Summary:** An OpenAI general-purpose reasoning model autonomously disproved the Erdős unit distance conjecture — an 80-year-old open problem in discrete geometry — by discovering an infinite family of point configurations that outperform the previously conjectured maximum. The proof was independently verified by external mathematicians including Fields medalist Tim Gowers, who called it "a milestone in AI mathematics." This is the first time an AI system has autonomously refuted a prominent open conjecture central to a field of mathematics, not just solved a competition benchmark.

## [Google Announces WebMCP: An Open Web Standard for Browser AI Agents](https://developers.googleblog.com/all-the-news-from-the-google-io-2026-developer-keynote/)
**Source:** Google Developers Blog
**Summary:** At Google I/O 2026, Google proposed WebMCP, a new open web standard that allows developers to expose structured web tools — JavaScript functions, HTML forms — to browser-based AI agents in a standardized way. Chrome DevTools for Agents was also announced, giving AI agents visibility into browser state for real-time code verification and debugging. WebMCP could become the connective layer between the open web and the emerging ecosystem of autonomous browser agents.

## [EU AI Act Transparency Rules Enter Force in August 2026](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai)
**Source:** European Union / AI Weekly
**Summary:** The European Union's AI Act — the world's first comprehensive legal framework for AI — is accelerating into enforcement, with transparency and general-purpose AI rules coming into effect in August 2026. Under Article 57, every EU Member State must establish at least one national AI regulatory sandbox by that date, and major AI providers are finalizing compliance programs. The week also saw the EU strike an AI Act compliance deal with major labs, coinciding with the first union vote at a top AI lab landing at Google DeepMind.
