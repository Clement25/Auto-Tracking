## [OpenAI and Broadcom Unveil Jalapeño, a Custom AI Inference Chip](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/24/openai-unveils-its-first-custom-chip-built-by-broadcom/)
**Source:** TechCrunch / CNBC / CNN Business
**Summary:** OpenAI and Broadcom revealed Jalapeño, OpenAI's first custom LLM-optimized intelligence processor, developed from initial design to manufacturing tape-out in just nine months — reportedly the fastest ASIC development cycle ever achieved in high-performance semiconductors. The chip is purpose-built for inference, delivering substantially better performance-per-watt than current state-of-the-art, and is targeted for initial deployment by end of 2026 at gigawatt-scale data centers. This marks OpenAI's direct strike at Nvidia's dominance in AI inference hardware.

## [Google DeepMind Releases DiffusionGemma: 26B MoE Model with 4× Faster Text Generation](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/developers-tools/diffusion-gemma-faster-text-generation/)
**Source:** Google Blog / MarkTechPost / SiliconANGLE
**Summary:** Google DeepMind released DiffusionGemma on June 10, an experimental open-weights 26B Mixture-of-Experts model that generates text via discrete diffusion rather than sequential autoregression — producing 1,000+ tokens per second on a single H100 GPU, approximately 4× faster than comparable autoregressive models. The model activates only 3.8B parameters during inference, fitting within 18GB VRAM when quantized, though it trades some benchmark quality (MMLU, coding) for speed. Released under Apache 2.0, it is being accelerated on NVIDIA RTX hardware and positions Google's open-source ecosystem as competitive on inference efficiency.
