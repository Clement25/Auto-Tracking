## [Google I/O 2026: Gemini Omni, Gemini 3.5 Flash, and Gemini Spark Unveiled](https://9to5google.com/2026/05/19/google-io-2026-news/)
**Source:** 9to5Google / CNBC

## [US Government Finalizes Pre-Deployment AI Evaluation Agreements with All Five Major Labs](https://www.crescendo.ai/news/latest-ai-news-and-updates)
**Source:** Crescendo AI News / Build Fast with AI
**Summary:** The US Commerce Department's Center for AI Safety and Innovation (CAISI) has finalized binding pre-deployment evaluation agreements with OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and xAI—meaning every major frontier model must now pass government evaluation before public release. This marks the most significant US AI governance milestone since the Biden-era voluntary safety commitments, creating a de facto regulatory checkpoint that smaller labs may struggle to meet. The move is expected to reshape the competitive landscape by raising the compliance cost of frontier model development.

## [Microsoft AI CEO Suleyman: All White-Collar Work Automated Within 18 Months](https://fortune.com/2026/02/13/when-will-ai-kill-white-collar-office-jobs-18-months-microsoft-mustafa-suleyman/)
**Source:** Fortune / eWeek
**Summary:** Microsoft AI CEO Mustafa Suleyman told the Financial Times that virtually every task performed "sitting down at a computer"—including accounting, legal, marketing, and project management—will be fully automated by AI within 12 to 18 months, predicting "human-level performance on most, if not all professional tasks." The statement has reignited debate about AI displacement timelines and corporate AI strategy.

## [EU AI Act High-Risk System Deadlines Remain on Track for August 2026](https://www.tlt.com/insights-and-events/insight/ai-brief-may-2026)
**Source:** TLT LLP AI Brief
**Summary:** Negotiations to amend the EU AI Act have stalled, meaning the August 2026 compliance deadlines for high-risk AI systems—covering areas like biometric identification, critical infrastructure, employment, and education—will proceed as scheduled. Companies deploying AI in these categories must complete conformity assessments, register in the EU database, and implement ongoing monitoring frameworks. The failed amendment effort, which sought to ease requirements for foundation model providers, leaves European AI deployment with among the most stringent regulatory obligations globally.
