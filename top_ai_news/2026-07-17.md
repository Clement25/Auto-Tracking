# Top AI News (2026-07-17)

## [Google Launches Gemini 3.5 Pro After Full Architectural Rebuild](https://centraljersey.com/2026/07/17/google-gemini-3-5-pro-launch-what-to-expect/)
**Source:** Multiple (TechTimes, Enterprise DNA, Central Jersey)
**Summary:** Google DeepMind launched Gemini 3.5 Pro on July 17, targeting general availability after a six-week delay caused by scrapping its original base model over structural failures in recursive tool-calling. Reported specs include a 2-million-token context window, a "Deep Think" extended reasoning mode (gated behind the $250/month Ultra subscription), and improved SVG generation and autonomous coding. This puts it in direct competition with GPT-5.6 and Anthropic's Fable 5 for top-tier model dominance.

## [Moonshot AI Releases Kimi K3 — Largest Open-Weight Model Ever, Rivaling US Frontier Systems](https://venturebeat.com/technology/chinas-moonshot-ai-releases-kimi-k3-the-largest-open-source-model-ever-rivaling-top-u-s-systems)
**Source:** VentureBeat / Bloomberg / Forbes
**Summary:** Chinese AI startup Moonshot AI launched Kimi K3 on July 16, a new-architecture Mixture-of-Experts model with roughly 2.8 trillion total parameters and a 1-million-token context window. Two variants shipped — K3 Max for chat/agents and K3 Swarm Max for large-scale parallel processing — with an open-weight release promised by July 27. Early benchmarks place it just behind Fable 5 and GPT-5.6, while outperforming all other models, marking the narrowest gap yet between Chinese and US frontier AI.

## [Xi Jinping Keynotes World AI Conference, China Launches Global AI Governance Body (WAICO)](https://www.npr.org/2026/07/17/nx-s1-5897285/chinas-xi-calls-for-step-up-of-global-effort-in-ai-as-us-curbs-squeeze-chinas-tech-access)
**Source:** NPR / CNBC / Ministry of Foreign Affairs China
**Summary:** President Xi Jinping delivered the opening keynote at the 2026 World AI Conference in Shanghai — his first appearance at the event since it began in 2018 — calling for "people-centered" global AI governance and warning against placing one nation's security above others. China formally launched the World Artificial Intelligence Cooperation Organization (WAICO), with 29 countries including Russia, Pakistan, and Kazakhstan signing on, positioning it as a rival governance framework to US-aligned bodies targeting the Global South.

## [Apple Sues OpenAI for Trade Secret Theft Over AI Hardware Designs](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/10/apple-sues-openai-over-alleged-trade-secret-theft/)
**Source:** TechCrunch / Bloomberg / CNBC
**Summary:** Apple filed a landmark lawsuit in Northern California federal court on July 10 accusing OpenAI of orchestrating a coordinated campaign to steal hardware trade secrets, referencing over 400 former Apple employees now at OpenAI. Key allegations target OpenAI's Chief Hardware Officer Tang Tan for using confidential Apple project codenames during recruiting and coaching departing employees to evade security. The lawsuit seeks to bar OpenAI from using Apple's confidential materials and is seen as direct fallout from the collapse of the Apple-OpenAI partnership.

## [IBM Stock Plunges 25% as Clients Shift Budgets to AI Infrastructure](https://www.axios.com/2026/07/14/ibm-stock-ai-arvind-krishna)
**Source:** Axios / Yahoo Finance / Motley Fool
**Summary:** IBM shares dropped 25.2% on July 14 — its worst single-day decline since the 1960s — after CEO Arvind Krishna warned investors that enterprise clients are redirecting technology spending toward AI infrastructure, leaving IBM's higher-margin mainframe software and consulting businesses without closed deals. Q2 revenue came in at $17.2B with only 1% growth. The selloff rippled through the software sector, dragging Microsoft, ServiceNow, Salesforce, and Intuit down 3–5%, signaling a broad spending-shift risk for traditional enterprise software vendors.

## [DeepSeek Targets $70–74 Billion Valuation in New Funding Round, Eyes IPO](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-14/deepseek-mulls-new-funding-weeks-after-7-billion-round-ft-says)
**Source:** Bloomberg / IndexBox / Tech Startups
**Summary:** Just weeks after closing a $7.4 billion Series A, DeepSeek has begun preliminary discussions for another funding round at a pre-money valuation of roughly $70–74 billion, targeting at least 10 billion yuan (~$1.5B) in fresh capital for data center construction and chip acquisition. The company is simultaneously preparing an A-share IPO filing that could debut in 2027. The accelerated capital needs are driven by DeepSeek's push into AI agents, which requires substantially more compute than its existing chat and search products.

## [OpenAI GPT-5.6 (Sol, Terra, Luna) Launches Broadly After US Commerce Department Clearance](https://llm-stats.com/llm-updates)
**Source:** LLM-Stats / ThursdAI
**Summary:** OpenAI launched GPT-5.6 as a three-model family — Sol (frontier-class), Terra (~5.5-level intelligence at roughly half the cost), and Luna (fast and lightweight) — following additional testing and meetings with US Commerce Department officials over export control compliance. A new "Ultra mode" with Max reasoning and heavier sub-agent use is also available. The launch cements OpenAI's lead on agentic and reasoning benchmarks heading into Google's Gemini 3.5 Pro release the same week.

## [OpenAI Proposes Granting U.S. Government a 5% Equity Stake Worth ~$42.6 Billion](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/02/openai-proposes-us-government-own-5percent-stake-to-address-political-blowback.html)
**Source:** CNBC / Forbes / Tom's Hardware
**Summary:** OpenAI proposed granting the US government a 5% equity stake — worth approximately $42.6 billion at its $852 billion valuation — as part of an effort to defuse regulatory and political pressure in Washington. CEO Sam Altman framed the move as a mechanism to share AI's economic upside with the public, potentially through an Alaska Permanent Fund-style vehicle, and suggested all leading US AI labs should participate. The proposal remains conceptual and would likely require an act of Congress to implement.

## [Google Renames NotebookLM to Gemini Notebook, Adds Secure Cloud Compute](https://ai2roi.substack.com/p/ai-to-roi-news-and-analysis-july-103)
**Source:** AI to ROI / Build Fast With AI
**Summary:** Google rebranded its standalone research tool NotebookLM as "Gemini Notebook" on July 16, integrating it more tightly into the Gemini ecosystem. Pro users now get access to a secure cloud compute environment that runs code directly inside notebooks, with state syncing across the Gemini app and Google Search. The rebrand follows Gemini Notebook's breakout popularity as a research and document-analysis tool, and positions it as the document-intelligence layer of Google's broader Gemini platform.

## [OpenAI Acquires Northslope — Second Applied-AI Firm Since Deployment Company Launch](https://llm-stats.com/llm-updates)
**Source:** LLM-Stats / ThursdAI

The move signals a strategy shift from pure model licensing toward deeper integration services, putting OpenAI in direct competition with established systems integrators and consulting firms.
