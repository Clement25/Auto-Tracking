## [NVIDIA Launches Cosmos 3, the Open Frontier Foundation Model for Physical AI](https://investor.nvidia.com/news/press-release-details/2026/NVIDIA-Launches-Cosmos-3-the-Open-Frontier-Foundation-Model-for-Physical-AI/default.aspx)
**Source:** NVIDIA / Axios / HPC Wire
**Summary:** On June 1, NVIDIA released Cosmos 3 — a two-tower Mixture-of-Transformers model trained on 20 trillion tokens that unifies physical reasoning, world generation, and action generation in a single open model for robots and autonomous vehicles. Available in Nano (16B) and Super (64B) variants, Cosmos 3 is the first fully open omnimodal model for Physical AI and could compress robotics training cycles from months to days, significantly lowering the barrier to developing embodied AI systems.

## [Microsoft Launches Scout at Build 2026 — An Always-On Agentic AI Assistant for Microsoft 365](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/02/microsoft-launches-scout-an-openclaw-inspired-personal-assistant/)
**Source:** TechCrunch / Redmond Magazine
**Summary:** At Build 2026, Microsoft unveiled Scout, an always-on personal AI agent built on the open-source OpenClaw framework and integrated across Microsoft 365 (Outlook, Teams, OneDrive, SharePoint). Unlike Copilot's per-app prompt model, Scout maintains a persistent understanding of the user's habits and projects and can proactively schedule meetings, prepare agendas, and block calendar time. Currently in Frontier early-access with 3,000+ internal testers, it signals Microsoft's shift toward proactive agentic AI rather than reactive chat interfaces.

## [OpenAI Upgrades ChatGPT Memory with "Dreaming V3" Architecture](https://www.ghacks.net/2026/06/05/openai-upgrades-chatgpt-memory-with-new-dreaming-architecture-for-plus-and-pro-users/)
**Source:** gHacks / Dataconomy / TechTimes
**Summary:** OpenAI rolled out Dreaming V3 on June 4 — a new background memory synthesis architecture for ChatGPT that consolidates user context during idle periods, analogous to how the human brain consolidates memories during sleep. The update improves freshness, continuity, and relevance of stored memories and initially targets Plus and Pro users in the US, with free-tier expansion enabled by a ~5x compute cost reduction. This is the most significant memory overhaul since ChatGPT's original rollout and moves AI assistants closer to truly persistent, personalized agents.

## [Bipartisan "Great American AI Act" Would Preempt State AI Laws for Three Years](https://rollcall.com/2026/06/04/bipartisan-ai-draft-proposes-three-year-preemption-of-state-laws/)
**Source:** Roll Call / Axios / Bloomberg Government
**Summary:** Reps. Jay Obernolte (R-CA) and Lori Trahan (D-MA) released a discussion draft of the Great American AI Act on June 4, a 269-page bipartisan framework that would preempt state laws specifically targeting AI model development for three years while creating federal requirements for large frontier model developers (safety plans, incident reporting, third-party audits). The bill overrides recently enacted laws in California, New York, and Illinois — a politically fraught move, given Congress voted 99-1 against a similar preemption attempt last year.

## [Hackers Tricked Meta's AI Support Bot into Hijacking High-Profile Instagram Accounts](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/01/hackers-hijacked-instagram-accounts-by-tricking-meta-ai-support-chatbot-into-granting-access/)
**Source:** TechCrunch / Krebs on Security / MIT Technology Review
**Summary:** Attackers exploited a vulnerability in Meta's AI customer support chatbot by simply asking it to add a new email address to a target account during a password reset flow — no traditional hacking required. Accounts compromised include the Barack Obama White House account and Sephora's brand account. Meta has since patched the flaw, but the incident is a high-profile demonstration of AI chatbots as a new attack surface in social engineering, with MIT Tech Review arguing it reveals AI security gaps that go far beyond theoretical prompt-injection research.

## [MIT Technology Review: The Meta Instagram Hack Reveals AI Security Blind Spots](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/06/05/1138437/the-meta-hack-shows-theres-more-to-ai-security-than-mythos/)
**Source:** MIT Technology Review
**Summary:** MIT Tech Review's analysis of the Meta AI chatbot exploit argues the industry has focused too narrowly on theoretical AI safety (misalignment, deceptive models) while underinvesting in practical AI security: authentication, authorization, and trust boundaries within AI-integrated products. As AI bots are increasingly embedded in customer-facing workflows with real account permissions, the attack surface grows beyond model-level defenses. The piece calls for security engineering disciplines — not just AI safety research — to be treated as first-class concerns in agentic product development.
