## [OpenAI Launches "Dreaming V3" — A New Persistent Memory Architecture for ChatGPT](https://openai.com/index/chatgpt-memory-dreaming/)
**Source:** OpenAI Blog / gHacks Tech News
**Summary:** OpenAI deployed Dreaming V3, a background memory synthesis architecture that continuously consolidates conversations into long-term, time-aware memory across all ChatGPT sessions. Key capabilities include temporal freshness (e.g., "You are going to Singapore" auto-updating to "You went to Singapore"), multi-session continuity, and relevance-ranked recall. A 5x reduction in compute cost enabled the rollout to Plus/Pro users on June 4, with Free-tier access in the pipeline — bringing persistent personalization to hundreds of millions of users.

## [NVIDIA Launches Nemotron 3 Ultra: 550B Open-Weights Reasoning Model for AI Agents](https://artificialanalysis.ai/articles/nvidia-nemotron-3-ultra-launch-announced)
**Source:** Artificial Analysis / Digital Applied
**Summary:** NVIDIA released Nemotron 3 Ultra on June 4 with 550B parameters (55B active in MoE architecture) under an open Linux Foundation OpenMDW-1.1 license, delivering 300+ tokens/second and topping the US open-weights intelligence leaderboard. ## ["Great American AI Act" Tables 269-Page Bipartisan Frontier AI Framework](https://fedscoop.com/bipartisan-great-american-ai-act-draft-proposes-new-federal-ai-governance-framework/)
**Source:** FedScoop / Roll Call
**Summary:** Reps. Jay Obernolte (R-CA) and Lori Trahan (D-MA) released a 269-page discussion draft on June 4 targeting frontier AI models with mandatory risk-mitigation plans, safety incident reporting to federal and state regulators, third-party audits, and civil penalties for non-compliance. The bill includes a three-year preemption of state AI laws and creates a new Center for AI Standards and Innovation (CAISI) inside the Commerce Department. This is the most substantive federal AI governance proposal in U.S. history and sets up a major battle over where AI regulation authority ultimately sits.

## [White House Signs Executive Order on AI Innovation and Security](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/06/promoting-advanced-artificial-intelligence-innovation-and-security/)
**Source:** The White House
**Summary:** President Trump signed an executive order on June 2 directing creation of an AI Cybersecurity Clearinghouse (jointly operated by Treasury, NSA, and CISA) to coordinate vulnerability scanning, validation, and patch distribution for AI-enabled threats. The order also establishes a voluntary 30-day federal review window for developers to submit frontier models for evaluation before release, and tasks NSA/CISA with classified AI benchmarking to identify models with advanced cyber capabilities. Notably, the order explicitly prohibits mandatory licensing or pre-clearance requirements for AI development.
