## Nearby Restaurant Guide

### Near Home — Margaret Drive / Queenstown

#### Margaret Drive Hawker Centre
- **Location:** 6 Margaret Drive, Singapore 149306 (2-min walk)
- **Operating hours:** Various stall hours; most open from ~7 am–9 pm
- **Recommended stalls:**
  - **Hakka Thunder Tea Rice (Lei Cha)** — ~$5.50 | ~450 kcal | 20 g protein
    Brown or white rice option with assorted vegetables (long beans, cabbage, basil, dried radish), tau kwa, dried shrimp, peanuts, and Hakka green tea broth poured over. High in fibre, moderate protein, no spice. *Closed Mondays & Tuesdays.*
  - **Yong Tau Foo (clear soup)** — ~$5–6 | ~400 kcal | 28 g protein
    Choose bee hoon or tang hoon noodles with tofu, fish balls, and an assortment of vegetables in clear soup (not the laksa/curry broth). Low fat, high protein, satisfying portion.
  - **Tanglin Halt Ruyi Vegetarian** — ~$4–5 | ~380 kcal | 15 g protein
    Vegetarian fried mee hoon or noodles; request oil-light version. Good plant-based option.

#### Seeds Cafe
- **Location:** 501 Margaret Drive, Singapore 149306 (within Rainbow Centre, ~5-min walk)
- **Operating hours:** Mon–Fri 8 am–5 pm · Sun 8 am–1 pm · **Closed Saturdays**
- **Recommended dish:**
  - **Miso Chicken Warm Bowl** — ~$10–12 | ~380 kcal | 30 g protein
    Miso-marinated grilled chicken on warm greens, edamame, and quinoa. Light dressing, low sugar. Perfect for a healthy Sunday brunch or weekday lunch if working from home.
  - **Grilled Vegetables Marinara Wrap** — ~$9 | ~320 kcal | 14 g protein
    Wholemeal wrap with grilled vegetables and marinara; lower calorie option.

## Self-Made Meal Recipes

### Breakfast Recipes

#### Egg-White Scramble on Wholemeal Toast with Avocado
**Serves 1 · Prep 10 min · ~345 kcal | P:30g C:28g F:12g**
- 4 egg whites (or 120 ml carton egg whites)
- 2 slices wholemeal bread, toasted
- ¼ ripe avocado, sliced
- Handful baby spinach
- 1 tomato, sliced
- Salt, pepper, cooking spray

Toast bread. Lightly spray a non-stick pan; scramble egg whites over medium heat with spinach until just set. Season. Top toast with egg whites, avocado, and tomato.

---

#### Greek Yogurt Parfait
**Serves 1 · Prep 5 min · ~315 kcal | P:24g C:40g F:6g**
- 200 g low-fat Greek yogurt
- 100 g mixed berries (fresh or thawed frozen)
- 30 g rolled oats (toasted dry, or use low-sugar granola)
- 10 g chia seeds
- 1 tsp honey

Layer yogurt, berries, oats/granola, chia seeds. Drizzle honey. Eat immediately or refrigerate overnight.

---

#### Overnight Oats with Peanut Butter & Banana
**Serves 1 · Prep 5 min (night before) · ~400 kcal | P:16g C:55g F:12g**
- 60 g rolled oats
- 200 ml low-fat milk
- 1 medium banana, sliced
- 1 tbsp natural peanut butter
- 10 g chia seeds
- Pinch of cinnamon

Combine oats, milk, and chia seeds in a jar. Refrigerate overnight. Top with banana slices and peanut butter in the morning.

---

#### Cottage Cheese Fruit Bowl
**Serves 1 · Prep 5 min · ~290 kcal | P:27g C:24g F:10g**
- 200 g low-fat cottage cheese
- 100 g pineapple chunks (fresh or canned in juice, drained)
- 15 g walnuts, roughly chopped
- 1 tsp honey

Combine all ingredients. Eat immediately.

---

#### Scrambled Eggs on Wholemeal Toast (Protein-Forward)
**Serves 1 · Prep 8 min · ~280 kcal | P:25g C:15g F:13g**
- 2 whole eggs
- 3 egg whites
- 1 slice wholemeal bread, toasted
- Handful baby spinach
- Salt, pepper, cooking spray

Whisk eggs and egg whites together. Cook over low heat in a lightly sprayed pan, folding gently. Add spinach. Serve on toast.

---

#### Protein Smoothie Bowl
**Serves 1 · Prep 10 min · ~420 kcal | P:40g C:45g F:8g**
- 200 g low-fat Greek yogurt
- 1 scoop vanilla protein powder (~25 g protein)
- 100 g frozen mixed berries
- ½ medium banana
- **Toppings:** 25 g low-sugar granola, 10 g chia seeds

Blend yogurt, protein powder, berries, and banana until thick. Pour into a bowl; add toppings.

---

#### Wholemeal French Toast with Berries
**Serves 1 · Prep 15 min · ~360 kcal | P:20g C:42g F:12g**
- 2 slices wholemeal bread
- 2 whole eggs
- 50 ml low-fat milk
- ½ tsp cinnamon
- 100 g strawberries, halved
- 1 tsp maple syrup
- Cooking spray

Whisk eggs, milk, and cinnamon. Dip bread slices; cook in a lightly sprayed pan over medium heat, 2–3 min per side until golden. Serve topped with strawberries and a drizzle of maple syrup.

### Lunch/Dinner Recipes

#### Baked Lemon-Herb Salmon with Roasted Broccoli & Brown Rice
**Serves 1 · Prep 5 min · Cook 25 min · ~490 kcal | P:40g C:38g F:16g**
- 150 g salmon fillet
- 300 g broccoli florets
- 150 g cooked brown rice (from ~60 g dry)
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 lemon (juice + zest)
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- Mixed dried herbs (oregano, thyme)

Preheat oven to 200 °C. Place salmon and broccoli on a lined tray. Drizzle with olive oil; squeeze lemon, scatter garlic and herbs. Bake 18–22 min until salmon is cooked through. Serve over brown rice.

---

#### Teriyaki Chicken with Quinoa & Stir-Fried Bok Choy
**Serves 1 · Prep 5 min · Cook 20 min · ~470 kcal | P:42g C:42g F:10g**
- 130 g chicken breast, sliced
- 150 g cooked quinoa (from ~60 g dry)
- 200 g bok choy, halved
- 2 tbsp low-sodium teriyaki sauce (store-bought or mix 1 tbsp low-sodium soy + 1 tsp honey + ½ tsp sesame oil)
- 1 tsp sesame seeds
- 1 tsp neutral oil

Heat oil in pan; cook chicken ~4 min each side until cooked through. Add teriyaki sauce; toss to coat. In the same pan, stir-fry bok choy with a splash of water, 2 min. Serve chicken over quinoa; top with sesame seeds.

---

#### Brown Rice Chicken Bowl (Meal-Prep / Packed Lunch)
**Serves 1 · Prep 20 min · ~445 kcal | P:42g C:44g F:8g**
- 120 g chicken breast
- 150 g cooked brown rice
- 150 g broccoli, steamed
- 2 handfuls baby spinach
- **Dressing:** 1 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce, 1 tsp sesame oil, 1 tsp grated ginger, ½ tsp rice vinegar

Grill or pan-fry chicken (seasoned with salt + pepper) until cooked. Slice. Pack into container with rice, broccoli, and spinach. Pack dressing separately; drizzle before eating. Refrigerate overnight for next day's lunch.

---

#### Tuna & Quinoa Salad (Packed Lunch)
**Serves 1 · Prep 10 min · ~420 kcal | P:42g C:38g F:12g**
- 130 g canned tuna in water, drained
- 150 g cooked quinoa
- 2 handfuls mixed salad greens
- 100 g cherry tomatoes, halved
- ½ cucumber, diced
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- Juice of ½ lemon
- Salt, pepper

Combine quinoa and salad greens in a container. Top with tuna, tomatoes, cucumber. Drizzle olive oil and lemon before eating.

---

#### Garlic-Herb Chicken with Baked Sweet Potato & Steamed Green Beans
**Serves 1 · Prep 5 min · Cook 30 min · ~470 kcal | P:42g C:42g F:10g**
- 140 g chicken breast
- 150 g sweet potato, halved lengthwise
- 150 g green beans
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- Mixed dried herbs
- Salt, pepper

Preheat oven to 200 °C. Rub chicken and sweet potato with olive oil, garlic, herbs, salt, pepper. Place on lined tray. Bake 25–30 min; steam green beans last 5 min.

---

#### Pan-Seared Tofu & Egg Stir-Fry with Brown Rice
**Serves 1 · Prep 10 min · Cook 15 min · ~480 kcal | P:32g C:45g F:16g**
- 200 g firm tofu, pressed and cubed
- 2 whole eggs
- 150 g cooked brown rice
- 200 g mixed vegetables (capsicum, snap peas, mushrooms)
- 1 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp fresh grated ginger
- 1 tsp neutral oil

Heat oil in wok; fry tofu cubes until golden on all sides (~5 min). Push to side; scramble eggs. Add vegetables and stir-fry 3 min. Add rice, soy sauce, sesame oil, ginger; toss to combine.

---

#### Miso-Glazed Salmon with Bok Choy & Soba Noodles
**Serves 1 · Prep 5 min · Cook 15 min · ~490 kcal | P:40g C:42g F:15g**
- 150 g salmon fillet
- 200 g bok choy, halved
- 90 g cooked soba noodles (from ~40 g dry)
- **Glaze:** 1 tbsp white miso paste + 1 tsp mirin + 1 tsp low-sodium soy sauce + 1 tsp honey
- 1 tsp neutral oil

Mix glaze ingredients. Brush over salmon. Pan-sear salmon (glaze-side up) on medium-high, ~4 min; flip and cook 3 min more. Steam bok choy. Cook soba per packet; rinse in cold water. Plate noodles, top with bok choy and salmon.

---

#### Baked Garlic-Herb Chicken with Cauliflower & Lentil Salad
**Serves 1 · Prep 10 min · Cook 30 min · ~460 kcal | P:40g C:35g F:14g**
- 150 g skinless chicken thigh
- 200 g cauliflower florets
- 80 g cooked green or brown lentils (from ~40 g dry)
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- Dried rosemary, thyme
- Juice of ½ lemon
- Salt, pepper
- Mixed salad greens (optional)

Preheat oven to 200 °C. Toss cauliflower and chicken thigh in olive oil, garlic, herbs, salt, pepper. Roast 25–30 min. Mix lentils with lemon juice, salt, and greens. Serve chicken and cauliflower over lentil salad.

## Practical Tips for the Week

1. **Meal prep Sunday:** Cook a big batch of brown rice and quinoa; grill 2–3 chicken breasts. Portion into containers for Tue and Thu packed lunches. This saves 30+ min on busy weekdays.
2. **Overnight oats (Tue night):** Set up the jar before bed for Wednesday's breakfast — zero morning effort.
3. **SaladStop! loyalty app:** Download the app before Monday; use the nutritional calculator to build your bowl to exact macros. Ask for "no croutons" and "dressing on the side."
4. **Thunder Tea Rice note:** The Lei Cha stall at Margaret Drive Hawker Centre is closed Mon & Tue. Choose the brown rice option for more fibre.
5. **Seeds Cafe Sunday:** Arrive before 12:30 pm as they close at 1 pm on Sundays.
6. **Hydration:** Aim for 2–2.5 L of water daily. Green tea (unsweetened) is a great afternoon option — it supports fat oxidation and has zero calories.
7. **Scaling calories up:** If you feel too hungry (especially after gym days), add 100–150 kcal via an extra hard-boiled egg or a larger portion of vegetables — not refined carbs.
