## Nearby Restaurant Guide

### Near Home — Margaret Drive, Queenstown

- **Margaret Drive Hawker Centre** (36 Margaret Drive, same street) — *Thunder Tea Rice stall*: Brown/white rice with chopped long beans, cabbage, preserved veg, peanuts, ikan bilis, served with a cup of antioxidant-rich herbal green tea soup. ~400 kcal, ~15 g protein. Ideal fat-loss choice — high fibre, low fat, rich in micronutrients. Ask for brown rice if available. Stall opens from early morning.

- **Qi Philosophy Café** (Margaret Market, 38 Margaret Drive, 1-min walk) — *Miso Chicken Warm Bowl or Salad* ($9.80): Miso-glazed chicken over basmati rice (lower GI) with seasonal vegetables. ~450 kcal, ~35 g protein. Air-conditioned food hall, opens 7 am–midnight. Good for dinner or weekend lunch. Avoid the Nasi Goreng option.

- **Kou Kou Xiang 口口香鸡汤饭** (Margaret Drive) — *Kampong Chicken Soup Rice*: Slow-simmered chicken in a clear mushroom broth, steamed rice. ~380 kcal, ~30 g protein. Light, nourishing, low-fat preparation. Request no skin on the chicken to save ~80 kcal.

- **Nine Miles Chicken Stew** (Margaret Drive Hawker Centre) — *Cantonese-style congee / steamed chicken with rice*: ~300 kcal, ~22 g protein. Low-calorie, high-satiety option. Good for lighter dinners.

### Near Office — The Metropolis / Buona Vista

- **SaladStop!** (The Metropolis, 9 North Buona Vista Rd, #01-16) — *Custom Grilled Chicken Quinoa Bowl*: Build your own with grilled chicken breast, quinoa base, leafy greens, raw veg, light dressing. ~450–500 kcal, ~35–40 g protein. Order online for 20% off. Avoid creamy dressings (Caesar, ranch); opt for lemon tahini or yuzu.

- **The Lawn Café** (31 Biopolis Way, #01-07, ~5-min walk from Metropolis) — *Olive Rice Bowl with grilled protein* (from $10.60): Choose chicken or fish, up to 5 toppings. ~500–550 kcal, ~38–42 g protein. Customizable and macro-friendly. Pick grilled over fried, skip the cheese.

- **Blessed Burrito Bowls** (The Metropolis, #01-15, ground floor) — *Brown Rice Chicken Bowl*: Grilled chicken, black beans, salsa, shredded lettuce, brown rice. ~520–560 kcal, ~35–40 g protein. Skip cheese and sour cream to save ~150 kcal. High fibre from beans.

## Self-Made Meal Recipes

### Scrambled Eggs with Wholegrain Toast
**Ingredients:** 3 large eggs, 2 slices wholegrain bread, 80 g cherry tomatoes, ½ tsp olive oil, salt, black pepper  
**Prep time:** 8 min  
**Method:** Beat eggs, season. Heat olive oil in non-stick pan over medium-low heat. Add eggs, stir gently until just set. Serve with toasted bread and halved tomatoes.  
**Calories:** ~420 kcal | P: 28 g | C: 32 g | F: 18 g

---

### Overnight Oats
**Ingredients:** 60 g rolled oats, 200 ml unsweetened soy milk, 15 g chia seeds, ½ banana (sliced), pinch of cinnamon  
**Prep time:** 5 min (+ overnight soak)  
**Method:** Combine all ingredients in a jar. Stir well. Refrigerate overnight. Eat cold or microwave 1–2 min in the morning.  
**Calories:** ~370 kcal | P: 16 g | C: 52 g | F: 10 g

---

### Greek Yogurt Parfait
**Ingredients:** 200 g plain low-fat Greek yogurt, 25 g low-sugar granola, 60 g mixed berries (fresh or frozen/thawed), 1 hard-boiled egg  
**Prep time:** 3 min  
**Method:** Layer yogurt, granola, and berries in a bowl or jar. Eat egg alongside.  
**Calories:** ~380 kcal | P: 30 g | C: 40 g | F: 7 g

---

### Avocado Toast with Poached Eggs
**Ingredients:** 2 slices wholegrain bread, ½ ripe avocado, 2 large eggs, lemon juice (½ tsp), salt, black pepper, chilli flakes (optional — skip if avoiding spicy)  
**Prep time:** 10 min  
**Method:** Toast bread. Mash avocado with lemon, salt, pepper. Poach eggs in simmering water for 3 min. Spread avocado on toast, top with eggs.  
**Calories:** ~430 kcal | P: 22 g | C: 32 g | F: 24 g

---

### Protein Smoothie
**Ingredients:** 30 g vanilla whey protein powder, 200 ml low-fat milk, 1 ripe banana, large handful (30 g) baby spinach, 4–5 ice cubes  
**Prep time:** 3 min  
**Method:** Blend all ingredients until smooth. The spinach adds fibre and micronutrients; you won't taste it.  
**Calories:** ~330 kcal | P: 33 g | C: 40 g | F: 5 g

---

### Egg-White Omelette
**Ingredients:** 4 egg whites, 1 whole egg, 60 g baby spinach, 80 g sliced mushrooms, ½ tsp olive oil, 1 slice wholegrain toast, salt, black pepper  
**Prep time:** 10 min  
**Method:** Whisk egg whites and whole egg. Heat oil in non-stick pan. Sauté mushrooms until golden. Add eggs, lay spinach on one half, fold and cook until set. Serve with toast.  
**Calories:** ~320 kcal | P: 30 g | C: 26 g | F: 8 g

---

### Oat Porridge with Almonds
**Ingredients:** 60 g rolled oats, 200 ml low-fat milk, 20 g almonds (roughly chopped), 1 tsp honey  
**Prep time:** 8 min  
**Method:** Combine oats and milk in a pot. Cook over medium heat, stirring, until creamy (~5 min). Top with almonds and honey.  
**Calories:** ~370 kcal | P: 14 g | C: 50 g | F: 13 g

---

### Baked Chicken Breast with Broccoli & Brown Rice
**Ingredients:** 150 g chicken breast, 150 g broccoli florets, 80 g brown rice (dry), 1 tsp olive oil, garlic powder, paprika (mild), salt, black pepper  
**Prep time:** 5 min | Cook time: 25 min  
**Method:** Rinse rice, cook per packet instructions. Rub chicken with olive oil and spices; bake at 200°C for 22–25 min. Steam or roast broccoli for last 10 min. Slice chicken and serve.  
**Calories:** ~460 kcal | P: 46 g | C: 38 g | F: 9 g

---

### Pan-Seared Salmon with Bok Choy & Quinoa
**Ingredients:** 150 g salmon fillet (skin-on), 150 g bok choy, 80 g quinoa (dry), 1 tsp olive oil, low-sodium soy sauce (1 tbsp), garlic (1 clove), ginger (1 tsp grated)  
**Prep time:** 5 min | Cook time: 20 min  
**Method:** Cook quinoa per instructions. Sear salmon skin-side down in hot pan with olive oil, 4 min each side. In same pan, stir-fry bok choy with garlic, ginger, and soy sauce for 3 min.  
**Calories:** ~510 kcal | P: 42 g | C: 36 g | F: 20 g

---

### Chicken & Vegetable Stir-Fry with Brown Rice
**Ingredients:** 150 g chicken breast (diced), 200 g mixed veg (capsicum, zucchini, snap peas, carrot), 80 g cooked brown rice, 1 tsp sesame oil, 1 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce, garlic (1 clove), ginger (½ tsp)  
**Prep time:** 5 min | Cook time:** 12 min  
**Method:** Heat oil, sauté garlic and ginger. Add chicken, cook 5 min. Add veg, stir-fry 4 min. Add soy sauce, toss. Serve over rice.  
**Calories:** ~480 kcal | P: 45 g | C: 38 g | F: 12 g

---

### Baked Tilapia with Steamed Vegetables
**Ingredients:** 180 g tilapia fillet, 200 g mixed veg (bok choy, broccoli), 80 g cooked brown rice, 1 tsp olive oil, garlic powder, dried herbs (oregano/thyme), lemon wedge  
**Prep time:** 5 min | Cook time:** 20 min  
**Method:** Season tilapia with olive oil, garlic, herbs. Bake at 190°C for 18–20 min. Steam vegetables 5–6 min. Serve with rice and lemon.  
**Calories:** ~460 kcal | P: 45 g | C: 40 g | F: 10 g

## Tips for Success

1. **Meal prep Sunday:** Cook a batch of brown rice and quinoa on Sunday evening — halves your weeknight cooking time.
2. **Hydration:** Aim for 2.5–3 L water/day; hunger is often thirst in disguise.
3. **At restaurants:** Always request dressings and sauces on the side; choose grilled over fried; add extra leafy greens where possible.
4. **Protein priority:** Eat your protein first at each meal to trigger satiety hormones early.
5. **Evening hunger:** If hungry after dinner, snack on cucumber slices, plain Greek yogurt, or a boiled egg rather than reaching for carbs.
