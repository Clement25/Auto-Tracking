# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-06-25)

## [Multi-Agent Transactive Memory](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19911)
**Authors:** To Eun Kim, Xuhong He, Dishank Jain, Ambuj Agrawal, Negar Arabzadeh, Fernando Diaz (CMU & UC Berkeley)
**TL;DR:** Proposes a shared retrieval infrastructure for heterogeneous LLM agent populations, extending RAG to agent-generated artifacts. Agent trajectories encoding reusable procedural knowledge are indexed and surfaced to newly instantiated agents, preventing redundant rediscovery of solutions across a population.
**Tags:** `agents`, `multi-agent`, `memory`, `RAG`

---

## [A Technical Taxonomy of LLM Agent Communication Protocols](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19135)
**Authors:** Linus Sander, Habtom Kahsay Gidey, Alexander Lenz, Alois Knoll
**TL;DR:** Introduces a five-dimensional taxonomy — counterparty, payload, interaction state, discovery mechanism, and schema flexibility — to classify emerging LLM agent communication protocols such as MCP, A2A, and ANP. Provides a principled framework for reasoning about interoperability and protocol design trade-offs in multi-agent systems.
**Tags:** `agents`, `communication-protocols`, `multi-agent`, `taxonomy`

---

## [From Task-Guided Conversational Graphs to Goal-Oriented Dialogue Runtimes](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23797)
**Authors:** Not disclosed in preprint metadata
**TL;DR:** Traces the architectural evolution of conversational AI from static dialogue trees toward stateful, tool-augmented, agent-oriented runtimes. Formalizes orchestration patterns — exposing agents, tools, and multi-agent coordination — as first-class primitives in goal-oriented dialogue infrastructure.
**Tags:** `agents`, `dialogue-systems`, `orchestration`, `LLM`

---

## [CAPRA: Scaling Feedback on Software Architecture Deliverables with a Multi-Agent LLM System](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18976)
**Authors:** Not disclosed in preprint metadata
**TL;DR:** Presents CAPRA, a multi-agent LLM framework that automates detailed, pedagogically sound feedback on software architecture deliverables in university-scale settings. Demonstrates consistent rubric alignment and coverage across diverse student submissions without human reviewer bottlenecks.
**Tags:** `agents`, `software-engineering`, `education`, `LLM`

---

## [Agentic Software: How AI Agents Are Fundamentally Restructuring the Software Paradigm](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05608)
**Authors:** Not disclosed in preprint metadata
**TL;DR:** Argues that LLM-centered agents represent a paradigm shift beyond traditional software, enabling continuous software evolution rather than discrete releases. Introduces EvoClaw, a benchmark for evaluating agent performance on long-horizon software evolution tasks including refactoring, bug repair, and feature addition.
**Tags:** `agents`, `software-engineering`, `LLM`, `benchmarks`

---

## [A Survey on Autonomy-Induced Security Risks in Large Model-Based Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.23844)
**Authors:** Not disclosed in preprint metadata
**TL;DR:** Comprehensive survey cataloging security vulnerabilities that emerge specifically from agent autonomy — tool misuse, memory poisoning, prompt injection chains, and malicious multi-agent coordination. Proposes a risk taxonomy and highlights open research directions for robust agentic deployment.
**Tags:** `agents`, `security`, `survey`, `LLM`

---

## [Embodied AI in Action: Insights from SAE World Congress 2026 on Safety, Trust, Robotics, and Real-World Deployment](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10653)
**Authors:** Jan-Mou Li et al. (7 authors)
**TL;DR:** Synthesizes key findings from the SAE World Congress 2026 panel session on Embodied AI, covering the transition of autonomous vehicles, mobile robots, and industrial machines from laboratory research to real-world deployment. Highlights safety certification, calibrated trust, and human-robot interaction as the most pressing open challenges.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `autonomous-vehicles`, `safety`, `real-world-deployment`

---

## [OpenEAI-Platform: An Open-source Embodied AI Hardware-Software Unified Platform](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03392)
**Authors:** Not disclosed in preprint metadata
**TL;DR:** Introduces a fully open-source hardware-software stack combining a low-cost 6+1 DoF robotic arm (OpenEAI-Arm) with a vision-language-action model (OpenEAI-VLA) built on Qwen3-VL-4B and a Diffusion Transformer action head. Achieves competitive manipulation performance trained exclusively on open-source datasets, lowering the barrier for embodied AI research.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `VLA`, `open-source`, `manipulation`

---

## [RoCo Challenge at AAAI 2026: Benchmarking Robotic Collaborative Manipulation for Assembly Towards Industrial Automation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.15469)
**Authors:** Not disclosed in preprint metadata
**TL;DR:** Presents the RoCo Challenge, a standardized benchmark targeting multi-robot collaborative manipulation for industrial assembly tasks. Evaluates multi-agent task planning, adaptive grasp coordination, and real-to-sim transfer, revealing significant performance gaps between current methods and industrial deployment requirements.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `benchmarks`, `manipulation`, `multi-agent`
