# Top Papers ‚Äî AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-06-28)

## [Harnessing the Collective Intelligence of AI Agents in the Wild for New Discoveries](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10402)
**Authors:** Federico Bianchi, Yongchan Kwon, Aneesh Pappu, James Zou
**TL;DR:** Introduces EinsteinArena, an agent-native platform for open distributed scientific research where AI agents collaborate via shared leaderboards and discussion forums. Unlike isolated agent systems, EinsteinArena enables agents to share partial results and build on each other's insights ‚Äî as of May 2026, agents have discovered 12 new state-of-the-art results surpassing all prior human or AI solutions.
**Tags:** `agents`, `multi-agent`, `scientific-discovery`, `collective-intelligence`

## [Agents' Last Exam](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05405)
**Authors:** Yiyou Sun, Xinyang Han, Weichen Zhang, Yuanbo Pang, Tianyu Wang, Yuhan Cao, Dawn Song, et al. (310+ authors)
**TL;DR:** Introduces ALE (Agents' Last Exam), a large-scale benchmark evaluating AI agents on 1,000+ long-horizon, economically valuable, real-world tasks with verifiable outcomes across 13 industry clusters and 55 subfields. Developed with 250+ industry experts using the U.S. O*NET occupational taxonomy, ALE exposes the gap between benchmark performance and meaningful real-world deployment.
**Tags:** `agents`, `benchmark`, `evaluation`, `long-horizon`, `agentic-workflows`

## [Benchmarking AI Agents for Addressing Scientific Challenges Across Scales](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12736)
**Authors:** (SciAgentArena team)
**TL;DR:** Presents SciAgentArena, a benchmark framework testing agents across scientific challenges at multiple scales of complexity. Finds that current agents contribute effectively to well-specified data-analysis workflows but struggle to generate genuinely novel insights, sustain self-directed exploration, or solve open-ended research questions ‚Äî providing a roadmap for next-generation scientific agents.
**Tags:** `agents`, `benchmark`, `scientific-reasoning`, `evaluation`

## [Exploring Agentic Tool-Calling Decisions via Uncertainty-Aligned Reinforcement Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06976)
**Authors:** (TRUST team)
**TL;DR:** LLM agents frequently make suboptimal tool-use decisions ‚Äî invoking unsupported tools or hallucinating direct responses ‚Äî which compound errors across multi-step interactions. The paper proposes TRUST, a method that incorporates uncertainty quantification into RL reward design as a repulsive force to maintain uncertainty separation, trained via lightweight key-turn annotations on multi-turn trajectories.
**Tags:** `agents`, `tool-use`, `reinforcement-learning`, `uncertainty`, `llm`

## [Agent Planning Benchmark: A Diagnostic Framework for Planning Capabilities in LLM Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04874)
**Authors:** (APBench team)
**TL;DR:** Proposes a fine-grained diagnostic benchmark for planning in LLM agents, decomposing the planning process into goal decomposition, tool selection, constraint reasoning, and infeasibility detection. Existing end-to-end evaluations conflate planning and execution failures; this framework isolates where agents break down, enabling targeted improvement.
**Tags:** `agents`, `planning`, `benchmark`, `llm`, `evaluation`

## [The End of Software Engineering: How AI Agents Are Fundamentally Restructuring the Software Paradigm](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05608)
**Authors:** (arXiv 2606.05608 team)
**TL;DR:** Argues that agentic AI systems ‚Äî where LLMs serve as primary reasoning engines dynamically generating and discarding code ‚Äî represent a fundamental paradigm shift away from traditional software engineering. The paper formalizes the distinction between deterministic software artifacts and fluid agentic systems, and discusses the implications for software development practices, tooling, and organizational structures.
**Tags:** `agents`, `software-engineering`, `llm`, `agentic-systems`, `paradigm-shift`

## [PhysDrift: Bridging the Embodiment Gap in Humanoid Co-Speech Motion Generation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19935)
**Authors:** (PhysDrift team)
**TL;DR:** Addresses the embodiment gap in humanoid robots where co-speech motion pipelines designed for human bodies (e.g., SMPL-X representations) fail to account for the physical constraints of robot hardware. PhysDrift introduces a method to make speech-aligned motions physically executable for humanoid embodiments, enabling more natural and physically feasible human-robot interaction.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `humanoid`, `motion-generation`, `speech`

## [Artificial Collectives of Specialists and Generalists Excel at Different Tasks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20877)
**Authors:** (arXiv 2606.20877 team)
**TL;DR:** Studies the composition of multi-agent AI collectives, finding that teams of specialist agents outperform generalist-only teams on narrow, well-defined tasks while generalist collectives adapt better to novel or hybrid tasks. Provides design principles for assembling agent teams based on task structure, with implications for enterprise automation and agentic workflow orchestration.
**Tags:** `multi-agent`, `agents`, `collective-intelligence`, `specialists`, `generalists`
