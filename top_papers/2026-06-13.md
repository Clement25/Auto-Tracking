# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-06-13)

## [Harnessing the Collective Intelligence of AI Agents in the Wild for New Discoveries](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10402)
**Authors:** (EinsteinArena team, arxiv:2606.10402)
**TL;DR:** Introduces EinsteinArena, an agent-native open platform for distributed AI-driven research with live open problems, verified leaderboards, and problem-specific discussion forums. As of May 2026, agents on the platform have discovered 12 new state-of-the-art results that surpass all prior human and AI solutions. Demonstrates that collective agent intelligence can accelerate scientific discovery at scale.
**Tags:** `agents`, `multi-agent`, `scientific-discovery`, `benchmarks`

## [Agentic Software: How AI Agents Are Restructuring the Software Paradigm](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05608)
**Authors:** (arxiv:2606.05608, submitted June 10, 2026)
**TL;DR:** Argues that AI agents—where LLMs serve as the primary reasoning engine, dynamically generating and discarding code as an instrumental resource—constitute a fundamental restructuring of what software is and how it is built. Provides a conceptual framework distinguishing "agentic software" from traditional programs and anticipates implications for software engineering practice. A foundational position paper shaping how the field thinks about LLM-driven development.
**Tags:** `agents`, `llm`, `software-engineering`, `agentic-ai`

## [Agent Planning Benchmark: A Diagnostic Framework for Planning Capabilities in LLM Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04874)
**Authors:** (arxiv:2606.04874)
**TL;DR:** Introduces a planning-specific diagnostic benchmark with 4,209 multimodal cases across 22 domains and five evaluation settings, covering holistic planning, feedback-conditioned step-wise planning, and robustness under extraneous tools, broken tools, and unsolvable tasks. Fills a critical gap in agent evaluation by isolating the planning sub-capability independently from tool execution. Results reveal significant weaknesses in current frontier LLMs on infeasibility detection and constrained sequential reasoning.
**Tags:** `agents`, `planning`, `benchmarks`, `llm`, `evaluation`

## [A Zero-Shot Multi-Agent Framework for Human-Building Interaction via Programmatic Reasoning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11354)
**Authors:** (arxiv:2606.11354)
**TL;DR:** Develops a hierarchical multi-agent framework that uses semantic routing and programmatic reasoning to decouple natural language understanding from building analytics, enabling zero-shot interaction with building management systems. Demonstrates robust performance across heterogeneous sensor data without task-specific fine-tuning. Highlights a compelling application of multi-agent LLM architectures to cyber-physical systems.
**Tags:** `multi-agent`, `agents`, `cyber-physical`, `programmatic-reasoning`, `zero-shot`

## [Dynamic Trust-Aware Sparse Communication Topology for LLM-Based Multi-Agent Consensus](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01828)
**Authors:** (arxiv:2606.01828)
**TL;DR:** Proposes a dynamic sparse communication protocol for multi-agent LLM systems that adaptively weights inter-agent trust during multi-round consensus deliberation, reducing communication overhead while maintaining reasoning reliability. Addresses the scalability bottleneck in dense all-to-all agent communication schemes. Shows improved accuracy and efficiency on complex reasoning benchmarks versus fully connected baselines.
**Tags:** `multi-agent`, `llm`, `communication`, `consensus`, `trust`

## [TempoVLA: Learning Speed-Controllable Vision-Language-Action Policies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06491)
**Authors:** (arxiv:2606.06491)
**TL;DR:** Addresses the mismatch between robot manipulation's naturally alternating fast-transit and slow-contact phases and existing VLAs that inherit a single fixed speed from training data. TempoVLA conditions execution speed explicitly, enabling a single policy to adapt its motion tempo in context. Achieves improved task success rates on manipulation benchmarks by matching speed to task phase.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `vla`, `robot-manipulation`, `vision-language-action`

## [Intercepting the Future: Latent-Space Predictive World Model for Dynamic VLA Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02486)
**Authors:** (arxiv:2606.02486)
**TL;DR:** Existing VLA models assume static scenes between observation and execution, causing failures when objects move. AHEAD augments a frozen VLA with a motion-aware latent world model that predicts future scene state before acting, enabling predict-then-act behavior. Demonstrates significant gains on dynamic manipulation tasks without retraining the base VLA policy.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `vla`, `world-models`, `robot-manipulation`

## [What Matters in Orchestrating Robot Policies: A Systematic Study of Hierarchical VLA Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10267)
**Authors:** (arxiv:2606.10267)
**TL;DR:** Conducts a systematic study of hierarchical VLA systems where high-level VLM planners decompose tasks into language subgoals executed by low-level VLA controllers, identifying which design choices—planner granularity, subgoal representation, replanning frequency—most impact downstream success. Provides actionable design principles for building reliable robot agent stacks. Fills a gap in understanding how to compose foundation models for long-horizon robot tasks.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `vla`, `agents`, `hierarchical-planning`

## [AffordanceVLA: A Vision-Language-Action Model Empowering Action Generation through Affordance-Aware Understanding](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06155)
**Authors:** (arxiv:2606.06155)
**TL;DR:** Introduces structured affordance forecasting as a task-oriented intermediate representation between perception and action in VLA models, enabling more precise and robust manipulation by explicitly reasoning about where and how objects can be interacted with. The affordance-conditioned action generation outperforms baseline VLAs on contact-rich tasks. Bridges the gap between semantic scene understanding and dexterous robot control.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `vla`, `affordance`, `robot-manipulation`

## [Robots Need More than VLA and World Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06556)
**Authors:** (arxiv:2606.06556)
**TL;DR:** A critical position paper arguing that the current community focus on VLA policies and world models is necessary but insufficient for capable real-world robots. Identifies missing components—task-level memory, failure recovery, common-sense grounding, and physical safety constraints—that must be addressed for robots to operate reliably outside labs. Proposes a broader research agenda for embodied AI beyond the current paradigm.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `vla`, `world-models`, `position-paper`
