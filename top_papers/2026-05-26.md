# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-05-26)

## [SkillOpt: Executive Strategy for Self-Evolving Agent Skills](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23904)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2605.23904 — full author list on paper page)
**TL;DR:** SkillOpt is the first systematic controllable text-space optimizer for agent skills, treating skills as external state of a frozen agent trained with weight-space optimization discipline. It achieves best or tied performance across all 52 evaluated (model, benchmark, harness) cells, outperforming human-written, one-shot LLM, Trace2Skill, TextGrad, GEPA, and EvoSkill baselines.
**Tags:** `agents`, `skill-learning`, `optimization`, `LLM`, `self-evolving`

## [ProofAgent Harness: Open Infrastructure for Adversarial Evaluation of AI Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.24134)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2605.24134 — full author list on paper page)
**TL;DR:** ProofAgent Harness provides open-source infrastructure for adversarially evaluating AI agents, covering 48 pages of benchmarks, attack surfaces, and evaluation methodology. ## [SODE: Analyzing Social Dynamics in LLM Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23949)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2605.23949 — full author list on paper page)
**TL;DR:** SODE systematically studies how social dynamics — norms, persuasion, group conformity, and inter-agent influence — emerge and propagate within populations of LLM-based agents. The work surfaces alignment implications when agents interact at scale in multi-agent environments.
**Tags:** `multi-agent`, `social-dynamics`, `LLM`, `alignment`, `agents`

## [World Action Models: The Next Frontier in Embodied AI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12090)
**Authors:** Siyin Wang et al. (14 authors)
**TL;DR:** This position paper introduces World Action Models (WAMs), embodied foundation models that unify predictive state modeling with action generation by targeting a joint distribution over future states and actions. The authors systematically analyze the data ecosystem (teleoperation, human demos, simulation, egocentric video) driving WAM development and outline open challenges.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `world-models`, `robotics`, `foundation-models`, `action-generation`

## [Embodied Multi-Agent Coordination by Aligning World Models Through Dialogue](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12920)
**Authors:** Vardhan Dongre, Dilek Hakkani-Tür
**TL;DR:** Examines whether LLM-based embodied agents can genuinely coordinate under partial observability by communicating through natural language. The work extends PARTNR (a collaborative household robotics benchmark) with a dialogue channel and shows that explicit world-model alignment via speech significantly improves joint task success.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `multi-agent`, `dialogue`, `world-models`, `robotics`, `collaboration`

## [WorldArena 2.0: Extending Embodied World Model Benchmarking on Modality, Functionality and Platform](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.17912)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2605.17912 — full author list on paper page)
**TL;DR:** WorldArena 2.0 broadens embodied world model evaluation along three axes: modality (vision-only → visuotactile), functionality (policy evaluation → planning + RL environment), and platform diversity. It provides a unified benchmark to compare models as both perception engines and interactive environments for policy optimization.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `benchmarking`, `world-models`, `multimodal`, `robotics`

## [Physically Native World Models: A Hamiltonian Perspective on Generative World Modeling](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00412)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2605.00412 — full author list on paper page)
**TL;DR:** Proposes grounding generative world models in Hamiltonian mechanics so that learned dynamics are energy-conserving and physically consistent by construction. The approach is motivated by failures of purely data-driven world models in embodied AI, robotics, and autonomous driving, where physically implausible rollouts degrade planning.
**Tags:** `world-models`, `physics`, `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `generative-models`
