# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-07-20)

## [Self-Improvements in Modern Agentic Systems: A Survey](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13104)
**Authors:** Zhe Ren et al. (12 authors)
**TL;DR:** A comprehensive survey that frames modern self-improving agents as adaptive systems coupling a foundation model with a scaffold of prompts, memory, tools, and control logic. Formalizes self-improvement as a self-induced update operator that modifies model parameters or scaffold components from experience. Organizes prior work by update target and driving signals, covering applications, evaluation challenges, and open problems.
**Tags:** `agents`, `self-improvement`, `survey`, `agentic-systems`, `LLM`

## [Agentic Data Environments](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07397)
**Authors:** (Submitted July 2026)
**TL;DR:** Examines how autonomous agents operating in data environments promise gains in speed, scale, and labor efficiency, but introduces new failure modes with abrupt and often irreversible costs. Proposes a framework for reasoning about agent-induced data risks and mitigation strategies. Relevant to the deployment of LLM agents in enterprise settings with access to live databases.
**Tags:** `agents`, `data-environments`, `risk`, `autonomous-agents`, `safety`

## [HAS-Bench: Evaluating LLM-Based Human-Agent Systems under Configurable Human Participation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04329)
**Authors:** (Submitted July 4, 2026)
**TL;DR:** Introduces HAS-Bench, a benchmark for evaluating how well LLM-based agents collaborate with humans under varying degrees of human participation and intervention. Covers diverse tasks where the human handoff threshold can be configured, revealing that current agents struggle gracefully when humans change their level of involvement mid-task. Provides insights for designing more robust human-in-the-loop systems.
**Tags:** `agents`, `benchmark`, `human-AI-collaboration`, `LLM`, `evaluation`

## [Vision Language Action (VLA) Models for Unmanned Aerial Robotics and Bimanual Manipulation: A Review](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06706)
**Authors:** (Submitted July 8, 2026)
**TL;DR:** Reviews the rapidly expanding landscape of VLA models, with focus on applications to drone robotics and dual-arm manipulation — two domains where grounding language instructions in physical action is especially challenging. Surveys training paradigms, action tokenization schemes, and sim-to-real transfer strategies. Highlights key open challenges around long-horizon planning and contact-rich interaction.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `VLA`, `robotics`, `drones`, `manipulation`, `survey`

## [Embodied.cpp: A Portable Inference Runtime of Embodied AI Models on Heterogeneous Robots](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02501)
**Authors:** (Submitted July 2, 2026)
**TL;DR:** Presents a C++ inference runtime designed for deploying VLA and world-action models (WAMs) on heterogeneous robot hardware with a latency-first, batch-1 focus for closed-loop control. Addresses the practical gap between research-grade GPU inference and real-time embedded deployment. Demonstrates portability across CPUs, edge GPUs, and custom accelerators common in robotic platforms.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `inference`, `edge-deployment`, `VLA`, `systems`

## [Vision Language Action Models in Robotic Manipulation: A Systematic Review](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.10672)
**Authors:** (Submitted July 2026)
**TL;DR:** A systematic review covering the full VLA model pipeline for robotic manipulation — from visual perception and language grounding to action generation and real-world deployment. Catalogues architecture families, benchmark datasets, and evaluation protocols. Identifies generalization across novel objects and cluttered scenes as the foremost unsolved challenge.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `VLA`, `robotic-manipulation`, `survey`, `robotics`

## [Harnessing the Collective Intelligence of AI Agents in the Wild for New Discoveries](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10402)
**Authors:** (Presented at NeurIPS 2026)
**TL;DR:** Proposes deploying ensembles of specialized AI research agents that autonomously explore hypothesis spaces, share findings through a collective memory, and iteratively refine discoveries without human intervention. Demonstrates 3–10× higher improvement rates over fixed evolutionary-search baselines on a suite of ML research tasks. Accepted at the 39th Annual Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems.
**Tags:** `agents`, `multi-agent`, `scientific-discovery`, `collective-intelligence`, `automation`

## [Tmax: A Simple Recipe for Terminal Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23321)
**Authors:** (Submitted June 2026)
**TL;DR:** Introduces a minimal recipe for building robust terminal-operating agents by combining a tight observation-action loop with a curated replay buffer of successful shell sessions. Benchmarked on OpenThoughts-TBLite, a high-signal benchmark for iterating on terminal agents in realistic CLI tasks. Achieves state-of-the-art performance with significantly less scaffolding than prior approaches.
**Tags:** `agents`, `terminal-agents`, `CLI`, `tool-use`, `benchmark`

---

## [LaWAM: Latent World Action Models for Efficient Dynamics-Aware Robot Policies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15768)
**Authors:** (Submitted June 2026)
**TL;DR:** Proposes learning compact latent world models jointly with robot action policies, enabling robots to plan and act using internal predictions rather than explicit pixel-level imagination. Achieves sample efficiency gains over prior VLA approaches on dexterous manipulation benchmarks. The latent representation captures task-relevant dynamics while filtering out perceptually distracting but irrelevant scene content.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `world-models`, `robot-learning`, `VLA`, `dexterous-manipulation`

---

## [LIBERO-Occ: Evaluating and Improving VLA Models under Scene-Induced Occlusion via Viewpoint Imagination](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10862)
**Authors:** (Submitted June 2026)
**TL;DR:** Identifies scene-induced occlusion as a major underexplored failure mode for VLA models and introduces the LIBERO-Occ benchmark to systematically evaluate robustness. Proposes a viewpoint imagination module that hallucinates unoccluded views conditioned on the partial observation to restore spatial reasoning. Substantially improves manipulation success rates in heavily cluttered tabletop scenarios.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `VLA`, `occlusion`, `benchmark`, `robotics`, `perception`

---

## [Embodied-R1.5: Evolving Physical Intelligence via Embodied Foundation Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11324)
**Authors:** (Submitted June 2026)
**TL;DR:** Presents the next iteration of the Embodied-R series, using chain-of-thought reinforcement learning to improve physical reasoning and action quality in embodied foundation models. Introduces a physical-experience replay mechanism that distills lessons from real robot interactions back into the model without catastrophic forgetting. Sets new state-of-the-art results on the RoboSuite and FurnitureBench embodied reasoning suites.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `foundation-models`, `reinforcement-learning`, `physical-intelligence`, `robotics`
