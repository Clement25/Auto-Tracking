# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-06-27)

## [The Shift to Agentic AI: Evidence from Codex](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26959)
**Authors:** OpenAI Research Team
**TL;DR:** Analyzes large-scale usage data from OpenAI's Codex to show how agentic AI is rapidly changing how people work — active agentic users grew more than fivefold in the first half of 2026, with the fastest adoption happening outside the original software-developer audience. The study uses a privacy-protecting automated pipeline to compare external personal, organizational, and internal OpenAI user populations, finding organizational adoption especially high.
**Tags:** `agents`, `agentic-ai`, `llms`, `empirical-study`, `coding-agents`

## [OPID: On-Policy Skill Distillation for Agentic Reinforcement Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26790)
**Authors:** Jinyang Wu et al.
**TL;DR:** Proposes OPID, a training method that extracts hierarchical episode- and step-level "hindsight skills" from agent rollouts and routes them via a critical-first mechanism to produce token-level self-distillation advantages for policy optimization. Evaluated on ALFWorld, WebShop, and Search-based QA, OPID outperforms outcome-only RL and existing skill-distillation baselines in performance, sample efficiency, and robustness.
**Tags:** `agents`, `reinforcement-learning`, `skill-distillation`, `training`, `policy-optimization`

## [Agents' Last Exam](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05405)
**Authors:** RDI Berkeley Team
**TL;DR:** Introduces ALE (Agents' Last Exam), a benchmark of 1,000+ long-horizon, economically valuable real-world tasks across 13 industry clusters (55 sub-fields), developed with 250+ industry experts using the O*NET/SOC 2018 occupational taxonomy. Current frontier agents achieve a full pass rate below 1% on the hardest tier, showing a stark gap between benchmark performance and economically meaningful deployment.
**Tags:** `agents`, `benchmark`, `evaluation`, `real-world-tasks`, `economics`

## [Agent Planning Benchmark: A Diagnostic Framework for Planning Capabilities in LLM Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04874)
**Authors:** Haoyu Sun, Wenxuan Wang, Mingyang Song, Jujie He, Weinan Zhang, Yang Liu, Yang Yang, Yu Cheng
**TL;DR:** Presents APB, a planning-specific diagnostic benchmark of 4,209 multimodal cases across 22 domains and five settings — covering holistic planning, feedback-conditioned step-wise planning, and robustness under extraneous/broken/unsolvable tasks. Unlike end-to-end success metrics, APB isolates whether failures stem from planning or execution, enabling targeted diagnosis of LLM agent weaknesses.
**Tags:** `agents`, `planning`, `benchmark`, `llms`, `multimodal`

## [PlanBench-XL: Evaluating Long-Horizon Planning of LLM Tool-Use Agents in Large-Scale Tool Ecosystems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.22388)
**Authors:** (Authors from arXiv:2606.22388)
**TL;DR:** Extends planning evaluation to large-scale tool ecosystems where agents must select and chain tools over long horizons, a gap not addressed by prior benchmarks. Findings reveal that even state-of-the-art agents struggle significantly as the tool space and planning depth increase, highlighting scalability as a critical open challenge.
**Tags:** `agents`, `planning`, `tool-use`, `benchmark`, `long-horizon`

## [A Survey on Autonomy-Induced Security Risks in Large Model-Based Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.23844)
**Authors:** Hang Su, Jun Luo, Chang Liu, Xiao Yang, Yichi Zhang, Yinpeng Dong, Jun Zhu
**TL;DR:** Systematically surveys novel security risks that emerge specifically from agent autonomy — memory poisoning, tool misuse, reward hacking, and emergent misalignment — that go beyond threats to standalone LLMs. Reviews defense strategies layered across the agent stack, including memory lifecycle control, constrained decision-making, and introspective reflection.
**Tags:** `agents`, `security`, `safety`, `survey`, `alignment`

## [AI Coding Agents in Social Science: Methodologically Diverse, Empirically Consistent, Interpretively Vulnerable](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11456)
**Authors:** Meysam Alizadeh, Fabrizio Gilardi, Mohsen Mosleh, Enkelejda Kasneci
**TL;DR:** Tests Claude Code and Codex on a social-science analysis task against a many-analysts human baseline, finding that AI agents actually produce more methodological diversity than humans (Claude Code 3× more specifications) while remaining empirically consistent with the human consensus. However, agents remain "interpretively vulnerable" — susceptible to motivated framing at the verdict layer where estimates are converted to substantive claims.
**Tags:** `agents`, `coding-agents`, `social-science`, `evaluation`, `methodology`

## [Holo3.1: Fast & Local Computer Use Agents](https://huggingface.co/blog/Hcompany/holo31)
**Authors:** H Company
**TL;DR:** Introduces the Holo3.1 family of Vision-Language Models (0.8B–35B-A3B parameters) for computer-use agents, extending Holo3 to mobile environments with native function-calling support and optimized quantized checkpoints (FP8, Q4 GGUF, NVFP4) for local deployment. The 35B model reaches 79.3% on AndroidWorld (up from 67%), while smaller 4B/9B variants jump from 58% to 72%, making capable computer-use agents viable at the edge.
**Tags:** `agents`, `computer-use`, `vlm`, `mobile`, `open-source`, `local-inference`

## [Embodied AI in Action: Insights from SAE World Congress 2026 on Safety, Trust, Robotics, and Real-World Deployment](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10653)
**Authors:** SAE World Congress 2026 Panel
**TL;DR:** White paper synthesizing key insights from the SAE World Congress 2026 panel on embodied AI moving from research to real-world systems (autonomous vehicles, mobile robots, industrial machines). Frames embodied AI as a systems engineering challenge requiring lifecycle governance, human-centered design, and evolving safety standards, not just a capability research problem.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `autonomous-vehicles`, `safety`, `deployment`
