# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-06-12)

## [Agents All the Way Down: A Methodology for Building Custom AI Agents from Substrate to Production](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11869)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2606.11869 — authors listed on paper page)
**TL;DR:** Presents a lifecycle methodology called "Agents All the Way Down" built on two preconditions and three iterative practices for constructing production-ready AI agents. The framework guides practitioners from foundational substrate choices all the way through deployment, covering agent design, orchestration, and ongoing maintenance.
**Tags:** `agents`, `methodology`, `production-ai`, `llm`

## [Agentic Software: How AI Agents Are Restructuring the Software Paradigm](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05608)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2606.05608 — submitted June 4, revised June 10, 2026)
**TL;DR:** Argues that AI agents—where large language models serve as the primary reasoning engine, dynamically generating and discarding code at runtime—constitute a fundamental restructuring of the software paradigm rather than a mere incremental improvement. The paper proposes a new conceptual model for agentic software distinct from traditional programming models.
**Tags:** `agents`, `software-engineering`, `llm`, `paradigm-shift`

## [Agent Planning Benchmark: A Diagnostic Framework for Planning Capabilities in LLM Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04874)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2606.04874 — June 2026)
**TL;DR:** Introduces a diagnostic benchmark covering long-horizon planning, tool-noise robustness, calibrated refusal, and inference-time refinement, evaluated across 12 multimodal LLMs. Results reveal systematic weaknesses in current frontier models, providing concrete targets for the next generation of agent planning research.
**Tags:** `agents`, `benchmarking`, `planning`, `llm`, `evaluation`

## [Future of Work with AI Agents: Auditing Automation and Augmentation Potential across the U.S. Workforce](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.06576)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2506.06576)
**TL;DR:** Proposes an auditing framework to assess which occupational tasks U.S. workers prefer AI agents to automate versus augment, and introduces a Human Agency Scale to quantify desired levels of human involvement. The study highlights significant variation across occupations and raises policy implications for workforce transitions as agentic AI deployment accelerates.
**Tags:** `agents`, `future-of-work`, `human-ai-interaction`, `policy`

## [The Evolution of Tool Use in LLM Agents: From Single-Tool Call to Multi-Tool Orchestration](https://arxiv.org/html/2603.22862v1)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2603.22862 — March 2026)
**TL;DR:** Traces the architectural evolution of tool use in LLM agents from simple single-tool invocations to complex multi-tool orchestration pipelines. The paper shows that monolithic planning is being replaced by hierarchical delegation strategies to address goal drift in long-horizon tasks, and provides a taxonomy of orchestration patterns.
**Tags:** `agents`, `tool-use`, `planning`, `multi-agent`, `llm`

## [ToolTree: Efficient LLM Agent Tool Planning via Dual-Feedback Monte Carlo Tree Search and Bidirectional Pruning](https://arxiv.org/html/2603.12740)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2603.12740 — March 2026)
**TL;DR:** Introduces ToolTree, which applies Monte Carlo Tree Search with dual-feedback signals and bidirectional pruning to solve tool selection and sequencing in LLM agents. The method achieves an average ~10% gain over state-of-the-art planning paradigms across standard tool-use benchmarks.
**Tags:** `agents`, `tool-use`, `planning`, `mcts`, `llm`
