# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-07-13)

## [When Agents Do Not Stop: Uncovering Infinite Agentic Loops in LLM Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01641)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2607.01641, submitted July 2, 2026)
**TL;DR:** Identifies and categorizes failure modes where LLM agents enter non-terminating iterative loops during planning, tool use, state updates, and multi-agent collaboration. The paper provides a taxonomy of infinite loop triggers and proposes detection and mitigation strategies critical for production deployment.
**Tags:** `agents`, `llm-agents`, `reliability`, `tool-use`, `failure-modes`

## [Beyond Attack-Success Rate: Action-Graded Severity Scale for Tool-Using AI Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07474)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2607.07474, submitted July 8, 2026)
**TL;DR:** Introduces an action-graded severity scale to evaluate adversarial attacks on tool-using AI agents, replacing coarse attack-success-rate metrics with fine-grained impact assessments. The framework enables more realistic red-teaming and better comparison of defenses across agent architectures.
**Tags:** `agents`, `security`, `tool-use`, `adversarial`, `evaluation`

## [Progressive Crystallization: Turning Agent Exploration into Deterministic, Lower-Cost Workflows in Production](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07052)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2607.07052, submitted July 8, 2026)
**TL;DR:** Presents a lifecycle methodology that uses exploratory agents for discovery and then "crystallizes" successful paths into deterministic, cheaper workflows for production IT operations. Demonstrates significant cost reduction while preserving task success rates previously achieved by full agentic runs.
**Tags:** `agents`, `production`, `workflows`, `cost-efficiency`, `it-operations`

## [Embodied.cpp: A Portable Inference Runtime of Embodied AI Models on Heterogeneous Robots](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02501)
**Authors:** Ling Xu et al. (arXiv:2607.02501, submitted July 2, 2026)
**TL;DR:** Presents a portable C++ inference runtime that unifies deployment of vision-language-action (VLA) models and world-action models (WAMs) across heterogeneous robot hardware. Achieves 91–100% task success rates in closed-loop evaluation on HY-VLA and pi0.5, significantly reducing fragmentation in robot deployment stacks.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `vla`, `inference`, `deployment`
