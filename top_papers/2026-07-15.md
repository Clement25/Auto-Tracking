# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-07-15)

## [HAS-Bench: Evaluating LLM-Based Human-Agent Systems under Configurable Human Participation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04329)
**Authors:** Yaozu Wu, Wei-Chieh Huang, Jizhou Guo, et al. (The University of Tokyo, UIUC, MBZUAI, McGill University, Zhejiang University)
**TL;DR:** Introduces HAS-Framework, a graph-based benchmark that models humans and LLM agents as first-class participants with explicit roles, permissions, and communication paths. Evaluates Human-Agent Systems across six domains, measuring both task outcomes and process-level collaboration behavior such as clarification quality, feedback utilization, and initiative. Key finding: human participation substantially improves task completion and failure recovery, but gains depend critically on when, how, and by whom input is exercised.
**Tags:** `agents`, `human-agent-collaboration`, `benchmarks`, `evaluation`, `llm`

## [Cortex: A Bidirectionally Aligned Embodied Agent Framework for Long-horizon Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05377)
**Authors:** Jiaqi Peng et al.
**TL;DR:** Proposes an asynchronous bidirectional loop architecture for embodied manipulation agents that grounds a cognitive orchestrator using curated structural metadata and multi-granular instruction priors to reduce kinematic hallucinations. Cortex resolves both semantic and temporal ambiguity to outperform monolithic Vision-Language-Action models on long-horizon simulated tasks and transfers to complex real-world environments with dynamic closed-loop physical error recovery.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `manipulation`, `vla`, `long-horizon`

## [Long-Horizon-Terminal-Bench: Testing the Limits of Agents on Long-Horizon Terminal Tasks with Dense Reward-Based Grading](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08964)
**Authors:** Anonymous (submitted 2026-07-09)
**TL;DR:** A 46-task terminal benchmark spanning nine categories (experiment reproduction, software engineering, multimodal analysis, interactive games, scientific computing) designed to stress-test agents on tasks requiring hundreds of action episodes and minutes to hours of execution. Unlike prior benchmarks scored only on final outcome, it decomposes tasks into fine-grained graded subtasks enabling dense intermediate rewards and partial credit. Exposes critical gaps in current agents' long-horizon planning, long-context management, and iterative debugging.
**Tags:** `agents`, `benchmarks`, `long-horizon`, `evaluation`, `software-engineering`

## [A Survey: Learning Embodied Intelligence from Physical Simulators and World Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.00917)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Comprehensive survey of how embodied agents learn from physical simulation environments and world models, covering agents capable of perceiving, reasoning, and acting in the physical world. Reviews the landscape of simulator-grounded training, sim-to-real transfer techniques, and the role of world models as internal predictive engines for policy learning. Identifies key open challenges in grounding language and vision to real physical dynamics.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `world-models`, `simulation`, `survey`, `robotics`

## [Robo-Cortex: A Self-Evolving Embodied Agent via Dual-Grain Cognitive Memory and Autonomous Knowledge Induction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18729)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Presents a self-evolving embodied agent that maintains dual-grain cognitive memory—fine-grained episodic traces and coarse-grained semantic knowledge—and autonomously induces generalizable knowledge from experience without human annotation. The agent iteratively improves its own task performance through cycles of exploration, reflection, and knowledge consolidation, demonstrating significant gains on robotic manipulation benchmarks over time.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `memory`, `self-evolution`, `continual-learning`

## [MECoBench: A Systematic Study of Multimodal Agent Collaboration in Embodied Environments](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31966)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Introduces a benchmark for studying how multiple multimodal agents collaborate in shared embodied environments, covering coordination, communication, and joint task execution. Finds that naive multi-agent extensions of single-agent VLMs fail to coordinate effectively, and identifies key bottlenecks in turn-taking, shared world-state representation, and role specialization. Proposes evaluation metrics for both individual and collective performance.
**Tags:** `multi-agent`, `embodied-ai`, `multimodal`, `benchmarks`, `collaboration`

## [Skill-MAS: Evolving Meta-Skill for Automatic Multi-Agent Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18837)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Proposes a framework where multi-agent systems automatically evolve reusable meta-skills through experience, enabling agents to compose known skills to handle novel tasks without retraining. Demonstrates how skill libraries grow and specialize across agent roles, achieving strong generalization on compositional task benchmarks. Reduces the need for manual prompt engineering in agentic pipelines.
**Tags:** `multi-agent`, `agents`, `skill-learning`, `automation`, `llm`

## [Harnessing the Collective Intelligence of AI Agents in the Wild for New Discoveries](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10402)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Explores how fleets of deployed AI agents accumulate diverse real-world interaction data that can be aggregated to surface novel patterns and scientific insights impossible for any single agent or human to discover alone. Proposes collective intelligence protocols for sharing, filtering, and synthesizing agent experiences into structured knowledge. Demonstrates applications in materials science hypothesis generation and codebase vulnerability discovery.

## [Embodied.cpp: A Portable Inference Runtime for Embodied AI Models on Heterogeneous Robots](https://huggingface.co/papers)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors — trending on HuggingFace)
**TL;DR:** Addresses the fragmented deployment landscape for embodied AI models (VLAs and world-action models) by providing a portable C++ inference runtime that runs across heterogeneous edge robots without Python dependencies. Unlike request-response serving runtimes, Embodied.cpp satisfies the real-time closed-loop contract required for physical robot deployment, enabling the same model binary to run on different hardware platforms.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `inference`, `deployment`, `runtime`

## [Who Grades the Grader? Co-Evolving Evaluation Metrics and Skills for Self-Improving LLM Agents](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.MA/current)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors — July 2026 submission)
**TL;DR:** Tackles the circular problem of using LLMs to evaluate LLM agents by co-evolving both the grading rubric and the agent's skills simultaneously. Demonstrates that fixed evaluation metrics become gameable as agents improve, while jointly updated metrics maintain discriminative power. Introduces a meta-evaluation framework showing the co-evolved system outperforms static judge models in detecting genuine capability gaps.
**Tags:** `agents`, `evaluation`, `self-improvement`, `llm`, `benchmarks`

## [Evidence-Grounded Verified Agentic Reasoning: Eliminating LLM Hallucination in Empirical Inference via Tool-Attested Kernel Proofs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.12650)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors — ICML 2026 TAIGR Workshop)
**TL;DR:** Proposes a reasoning framework where LLM agents ground every empirical claim in tool-verified evidence before reasoning forward, producing kernel proofs that are machine-checkable. Accepted at the ICML 2026 TAIGR workshop, the approach eliminates hallucinated intermediate steps in scientific and data-analysis agent tasks. Shows near-zero hallucination rates on chemistry and biology reasoning benchmarks at modest compute overhead.
**Tags:** `agents`, `hallucination`, `reasoning`, `verification`, `scientific-ai`
