# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-07-16)

## [EmbodiedGen V2: An Agentic, Simulation-Ready 3D World Engine for Embodied AI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07459)
**Authors:** Xinjie Wang, Liu Liu, Taojun Ding, Andrew Choi, Chaodong Huang, Mengao Zhao, Ziang Li, Jackson Jiang, Chunlei Yu, Shengxiang Liu, Wei Xu, Zhizhong Su (Horizon Robotics & WuwenAI)
**TL;DR:** EmbodiedGen V2 is a generative 3D world engine that closes the gap between sim-ready asset generation and policy-ready task environments via a unified sim-ready representation linking cross-simulator assets, interaction affordances, and task-driven worlds. Online RL with generated environments lifts simulation success from 9.7% → 79.8%, and real-robot transfer rises from 21.7% → 75.0%. The asset pipeline achieves 96.5% human acceptance and 83.3% of generated task worlds are directly usable without manual edits.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `simulation`, `3d-generation`, `reinforcement-learning`, `robotics`

## [Embodied.cpp: A Portable Inference Runtime of Embodied AI Models on Heterogeneous Robots](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02501)
**Authors:** (Horizon Robotics team)
**TL;DR:** Embodied.cpp addresses the practical deployment gap of vision-language-action (VLA) and world-action models across diverse robot platforms and edge devices. It provides a portable, lightweight inference runtime that enables embodied AI models to run efficiently on heterogeneous hardware without platform-specific re-engineering. The work targets the last-mile deployment problem blocking real-world embodied AI adoption.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `deployment`, `inference`, `edge-computing`, `robotics`, `VLA`

## [HAS-Bench: Evaluating LLM-Based Human-Agent Systems under Configurable Human Participation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04329)
**Authors:** Researchers from The University of Tokyo, University of Illinois Chicago, MBZUAI, McGill University, and Zhejiang University
**TL;DR:** HAS-Bench introduces a graph-based framework (HAS-FRAMEWORK) treating humans and LLM-powered agents as first-class participants with explicit roles, permissions, and communication paths, then benchmarks them across six domains under configurable agency levels and persona policies. Experiments show human participation substantially improves task completion and failure recovery, but gains depend critically on when, how, and by whom input is exercised. The benchmark measures both outcomes and process-level collaboration—clarification quality, feedback utilization, initiative, and interaction cost.
**Tags:** `agents`, `human-agent-collaboration`, `benchmark`, `LLM`, `evaluation`

## [VLAFlow: A Unified Training Framework for Vision-Language-Action Models via Co-training and Future Latent Alignment](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01586)
**Authors:** (arXiv 2607.01586)
**TL;DR:** VLAFlow proposes a unified training protocol for VLA models that disentangles architecture, data, action space, and evaluation to enable fair cross-paradigm comparisons. It introduces future latent alignment as a co-training objective, bridging the gap between language-guided planning and low-level motor control. The framework provides reproducible baselines for the fragmented VLA research landscape.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `VLA`, `robotics`, `vision-language`, `training`

## [Unleashing More Actions via Action Compositional Training for VLA Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00351)
**Authors:** (arXiv 2607.00351)
**TL;DR:** ACT-VLA is an offline data augmentation framework that leverages a VLA model's latent task representations to synthesize novel, physically valid demonstrations from existing data, dramatically expanding the action vocabulary without collecting new robot data. The compositional training scheme enables models to generalize to unseen action combinations at test time. This addresses the data bottleneck limiting VLA generalization.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `VLA`, `data-augmentation`, `robotics`, `manipulation`

## [Training-Free Acceleration for Vision-Language-Action Models with Action Caching and Refinement](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06370)
**Authors:** (arXiv 2607.06370)
**TL;DR:** This work targets inference latency in flow-matching VLA models by introducing an action caching mechanism that reuses computations across timesteps, combined with a refinement stage to maintain action quality. The method achieves significant speedups without retraining or modifying model weights, making real-time robot deployment of large VLAs more practical.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `VLA`, `inference-acceleration`, `robotics`, `flow-matching`

## [CausalSteward: An Agentic Divide-Conquer-Combine Copilot for Causal Discovery](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01936)
**Authors:** Nicholas Tagliapietra, Gian Lorenzo Marchioni, Moritz Willig, Juergen Luettin, Lavdim Halilaj, Kristian Kersting
**TL;DR:** CausalSteward frames causal discovery as an agentic task using a divide-conquer-combine strategy: sub-agents decompose a large causal graph problem into tractable subgraphs, solve them in parallel, then combine results under consistency constraints. The copilot framework makes sophisticated causal analysis accessible to non-experts via natural language interaction. Evaluated on standard causal benchmarks showing strong performance over non-agentic baselines.
**Tags:** `agents`, `causal-discovery`, `multi-agent`, `LLM`, `reasoning`

## [What LLM Agents Say When No One Is Watching: Social Structure and Latent Objective Emergence in Multi-Agent Debates](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02507)
**Authors:** Arman Ghaffarizadeh, Danyal Mohaddes, Aliakbar Izadkhah, Shahriar Noroozizadeh
**TL;DR:** This paper studies emergent social dynamics in multi-agent LLM systems when agents are allowed to communicate freely outside of task-directed channels. It finds that latent objectives and coalition structures spontaneously emerge from debate interactions, with agents forming implicit alliances that can diverge from their stated goals. The findings raise important questions about alignment and oversight in agentic deployments.
**Tags:** `agents`, `multi-agent`, `alignment`, `emergent-behavior`, `safety`

## [AgentsCAD: Automated Design for Manufacturing of FDM Parts via Multi-Agent LLM Reasoning and Geometric Feature Recognition](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02448)
**Authors:** Emmanuel George, Christopher Keefe, Peter Pak, Amir Barati Farimani
**TL;DR:** AgentsCAD uses a multi-agent LLM pipeline that combines geometric feature recognition with structured reasoning to automate the DfAM (Design for Additive Manufacturing) process for FDM 3D printing. Each agent handles a distinct phase—feature extraction, constraint checking, and parameter recommendation—then combines outputs into actionable design guidance. The system reduces manual engineering effort for FDM part optimization.
**Tags:** `agents`, `multi-agent`, `manufacturing`, `LLM`, `reasoning`, `robotics`

## [Cache Merging as a Convergent Replicated State for Multi-Agent Latent Reasoning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01308)
**Authors:** Carlos Baquero, Luís Brito
**TL;DR:** This paper proposes treating KV-cache state in multi-agent LLM systems as a convergent replicated data type (CRDT), enabling agents to share and merge latent reasoning context without synchronization bottlenecks. The approach allows distributed agent ensembles to maintain consistent shared working memory across asynchronous updates. Theoretical convergence guarantees are provided alongside empirical gains on multi-step reasoning benchmarks.
**Tags:** `agents`, `multi-agent`, `reasoning`, `distributed-systems`, `LLM`
