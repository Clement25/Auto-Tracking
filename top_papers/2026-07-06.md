## [Do Multimodal Agents Really Benefit from Tool Use? A Systematic Study of Capability Gains](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02357)
**Authors:** Research team (arXiv:2606.02357)
**TL;DR:** Conducts a rigorous empirical analysis of whether tool augmentation (code execution, web search, image manipulation) genuinely improves multimodal agent performance, or whether gains are task-specific artifacts. Finds that tool use provides consistent benefits only for numerical computation and grounded visual tasks, while introducing latency and error propagation in other settings. Proposes a structured tool-selection protocol to maximize reliable gains.

## [APT: Action Expert Pretraining Improves Instruction Generalization of Vision-Language-Action Policies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12366)
**Authors:** Research team (arXiv:2606.12366)
**TL;DR:** Introduces Action Expert Pretraining (APT), a method that first trains a specialized action-head on large-scale unlabeled robot trajectories before fine-tuning a full VLA policy on language-annotated data. APT significantly improves instruction-following generalization across novel manipulation tasks and out-of-distribution scenes. Sets new benchmarks on RLBench and BridgeV2 with fewer labeled demonstrations.
