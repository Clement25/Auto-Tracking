# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-05-27)

## [Code as Agent Harness](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18747)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2605.18747)
**TL;DR:** A comprehensive survey of using code as the scaffolding layer for AI agents, covering coding assistants, GUI/OS automation, embodied agents, scientific discovery, personalization, DevOps, and enterprise workflows. The paper argues code-as-harness unifies action representation, memory, and control flow across virtually all agentic domains.
**Tags:** `agents`, `code-generation`, `gui-automation`, `embodied-ai`, `survey`

---

## [MIND-Skill: Quality-Guaranteed Skill Generation via Multi-Agent Induction and Deduction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.08670)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2605.08670)
**TL;DR:** Addresses the long-standing challenge of LLM agents failing on complex multi-step tasks by introducing reusable "skills" that encapsulate successful problem-solving strategies. A multi-agent induction–deduction loop generates and validates skills, enabling agents to build on prior experience with quality guarantees.
**Tags:** `agents`, `skill-learning`, `multi-agent`, `llm`, `task-planning`

---

## [Planning in the LLM Era: Building for Reliability and Efficiency](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.21902)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2605.21902)
**TL;DR:** A broad survey realigning the planning research field around LLMs, categorising three major families of planner-generation methods and documenting their soundness and completeness limitations. Offers prescriptive guidance for building reliable and compute-efficient LLM planners for real-world agentic systems.
**Tags:** `agents`, `planning`, `llm`, `reliability`, `survey`

---

## [Do Agents Need to Plan Step-by-Step? Rethinking Planning Horizon in Data-Centric Tool Calling](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.08477)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2605.08477)
**TL;DR:** Challenges the assumption that step-by-step planning is always necessary for agentic tool use, especially in data-centric tasks. Shows that success depends primarily on matching tool calls to the latent logic and vocabulary of underlying data sources rather than granular plan decomposition. Presented at the ACM Conference on AI and Agentic Systems 2026.
**Tags:** `agents`, `tool-use`, `planning`, `data-centric`

---

## [Planner Matters! An Efficient and Unbalanced Multi-Agent Collaboration Framework for Long-Horizon Planning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02168)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2605.02168)
**TL;DR:** Introduces a planner-centric reinforcement learning framework where a dedicated planner agent is optimised via trajectory-level rewards while executor agents remain lightweight. Achieves strong performance on long-horizon planning benchmarks while dramatically reducing computational overhead compared to symmetric multi-agent designs.
**Tags:** `multi-agent`, `planning`, `reinforcement-learning`, `long-horizon`, `llm`

---

## [RoboJailBench: Benchmarking Adversarial Attacks and Defenses in Embodied Robotic Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19328)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2605.19328)
**TL;DR:** Presents the first standardised benchmark for evaluating jailbreak attacks and countermeasures specifically targeting embodied AI systems (robots). Demonstrates that current robotic agents are substantially more vulnerable to adversarial prompts than their text-only counterparts, and proposes a defence evaluation protocol.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `safety`, `adversarial`, `benchmark`

---

## [World Action Models: The Next Frontier in Embodied AI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12090)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2605.12090)
**TL;DR:** Defines World Action Models (WAMs) as embodied foundation models that tightly couple predictive world-state modelling with action generation. The survey argues WAMs represent the next major architectural paradigm after vision-language-action models and surveys current progress, open challenges, and the path toward general-purpose robotic agents.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `world-models`, `robotics`, `foundation-models`, `survey`

---

## [Embodied Multi-Agent Coordination by Aligning World Models Through Dialogue](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12920)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2605.12920)
**TL;DR:** Proposes that effective collaboration between embodied agents requires grounding communication in each agent's internal world model. Agents exchange natural language messages to align their representations of the environment before acting, yielding significant gains on cooperative navigation and manipulation tasks.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `multi-agent`, `world-models`, `dialogue`, `coordination`

---

## [Coordination Matters: Evaluation of Cooperative Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06557)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2605.06557)
**TL;DR:** Evaluates six representative value-based MARL methods under varying centralisation levels and introduces STAT, a controlled spatial task-allocation testbed that isolates coordination quality. Proposes process-level diagnostics that supplement return metrics to better characterise how and when coordination emerges.
**Tags:** `multi-agent`, `reinforcement-learning`, `evaluation`, `benchmark`, `cooperation`

---

## [MARLIN: Multi-Agent Game-Theoretic Reinforcement Learning for Sustainable LLM Inference in Cloud Datacenters](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13496)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2605.13496)
**TL;DR:** Frames LLM inference scheduling as a multi-agent game and applies game-theoretic RL to jointly optimise latency, energy, carbon, and water usage across datacenter nodes. Outperforms state-of-the-art inference management frameworks on time-to-first-token while meaningfully reducing environmental footprint.
**Tags:** `agents`, `reinforcement-learning`, `llm-inference`, `sustainability`, `cloud`

---

## [3D Generation for Embodied AI and Robotic Simulation: A Survey](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26509)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2604.26509)
**TL;DR:** Surveys how 3D content generation pipelines feed into embodied AI and robotic simulators, going beyond visual realism to address kinematic structure, material physics, and sim-to-real transfer requirements. Identifies current gaps and future directions for scalable, physically-grounded simulation environments.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `3d-generation`, `simulation`, `survey`

## [Superhuman Safe and Agile Racing Through Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22748)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2605.22748)
**TL;DR:** Uses high-speed quadrotor drone racing as a challenging testbed for MARL, training agents to handle complex aerodynamic interactions and strategic blocking manoeuvres with a variable number of competing racers. Achieves superhuman lap times while maintaining safety constraints, advancing embodied competitive multi-agent control.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `multi-agent`, `reinforcement-learning`, `robotics`, `drones`
