# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-07-10)

## [A Survey: Learning Embodied Intelligence from Physical Simulators and World Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.00917)
**Authors:** Xiaoxiao Long et al.
**TL;DR:** Comprehensive survey examining how embodied intelligence can be systematically developed through physical simulation environments and learned world models. Reviews the interplay between simulation fidelity, world-model architectures, and downstream robot-learning performance. Identifies open challenges in sim-to-real transfer and generalization.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `world-models`, `simulation`, `survey`, `robot-learning`

## [SPEAR: A Simulator for Photorealistic Embodied AI Research](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06701)
**Authors:** Mike Roberts et al.
**TL;DR:** Introduces SPEAR, a photorealistic simulation platform purpose-built for embodied AI research, featuring high-fidelity rendering, physics, and diverse indoor/outdoor environments. Demonstrates that agents trained in SPEAR exhibit significantly better sim-to-real transfer compared to prior simulators. Provides open-source tooling for scene generation, sensor emulation, and evaluation.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `simulation`, `robotics`, `benchmark`, `sim-to-real`

---

## [CORAL: Towards Autonomous Multi-Agent Evolution for Open-Ended Discovery](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24177)
**Authors:** Anonymous et al.
**TL;DR:** Proposes CORAL, a framework where long-running multi-agent systems self-evolve through shared persistent memory, asynchronous execution, and heartbeat-based intervention mechanisms. Achieves 3–10× higher improvement rates over fixed evolutionary-search baselines across 10 math, algorithmic, and systems tasks. Demonstrates open-ended capability growth without human-defined reward shaping.
**Tags:** `agents`, `multi-agent`, `self-evolution`, `open-ended-learning`, `autonomous`

## [PhysReflect-VLA: Physical Feasibility and Self-Reflective Regulation for Reliable Vision-Language-Action Policies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27146)
**Authors:** Anonymous et al.
**TL;DR:** Presents PhysReflect-VLA, a vision-language-action model augmented with a physical-feasibility checker and a self-reflection loop that rejects or revises physically implausible action sequences before execution. Significantly reduces failure rates on contact-rich manipulation tasks compared to baseline VLAs. Enables more reliable robot deployment in unstructured environments without additional hardware.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `VLA`, `robotics`, `manipulation`, `self-reflection`

---

## [DyTopo: Dynamic Topology Routing for Multi-Agent Reasoning via Semantic Matching](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20570)
**Authors:** Anonymous et al.
**TL;DR:** Introduces DyTopo, which dynamically rewires agent-to-agent communication connections at each reasoning round using semantic matching rather than fixed graph topologies. Shows that adaptive topology discovery outperforms static configurations on complex reasoning benchmarks. Provides a lightweight routing mechanism compatible with any underlying LLM backbone.
**Tags:** `agents`, `multi-agent`, `reasoning`, `graph-topology`, `LLM`

## [Embodied-R1.5: Evolving Physical Intelligence via Embodied Foundation Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11324)
**Authors:** Anonymous et al.
**TL;DR:** Proposes Embodied-R1.5, a foundation model that acquires physical intelligence by combining large-scale embodied pre-training with online reinforcement learning from environmental feedback. Achieves state-of-the-art performance across navigation, manipulation, and dexterous tasks on multiple embodied benchmarks. Demonstrates continuous self-improvement as the agent collects new interaction experience.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `foundation-models`, `reinforcement-learning`, `robotics`, `navigation`

## [ProPlay: Procedural World Models for Self-Evolving LLM Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12780)
**Authors:** Anonymous et al.
**TL;DR:** Introduces ProPlay, where LLM agents construct and continuously expand procedural world models from experience, enabling systematic exploration and planning in novel environments. The self-evolving world model serves as an internal simulator, allowing agents to mentally simulate action sequences before committing. Evaluated on long-horizon open-world tasks where it outperforms agents using static environment representations.
**Tags:** `agents`, `world-models`, `LLM`, `planning`, `self-evolution`

## [Infrastructure for the Agentic Web: Gap Analysis and Architecture from the Agentverse Platform](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20570)
**Authors:** Anonymous et al.
**TL;DR:** Performs a systematic gap analysis of current web infrastructure for hosting and orchestrating large populations of AI agents, identifying missing primitives around identity, capability discovery, and inter-agent communication. Proposes the Agentverse architecture addressing these gaps with a layered protocol stack. Discusses deployment lessons from running thousands of concurrent agents in production.
**Tags:** `agents`, `infrastructure`, `multi-agent`, `agentic-web`, `orchestration`

## [From Question Answering to Task Completion: A Survey on Agent System and Harness Design](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20683)
**Authors:** Anonymous et al.
**TL;DR:** Surveys the evolution from static QA systems to dynamic task-completion agents, cataloguing key design choices in agent harnesses including tool use, memory, planning, and feedback loops. Identifies gaps in current evaluation frameworks that conflate single-turn answering with multi-step task execution. Provides a unified taxonomy and benchmark recommendations for future agent system research.
**Tags:** `agents`, `survey`, `task-completion`, `evaluation`, `LLM`

## [A Survey on Autonomy-Induced Security Risks in Large Model-Based Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.23844)
**Authors:** Anonymous et al.
**TL;DR:** Systematically surveys security vulnerabilities that emerge specifically from the autonomy of large-model-based agents, including prompt injection, goal misgeneralization, and unintended side-effects in multi-step execution. Proposes a threat taxonomy organized by the degree of autonomy granted to the agent. Discusses mitigation strategies including sandboxing, output monitoring, and constrained action spaces.
**Tags:** `agents`, `security`, `safety`, `prompt-injection`, `survey`

## [Pelican-Unify 1.0: A Unified Embodied Intelligence Model for Understanding, Reasoning, Imagination and Action](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15153)
**Authors:** Anonymous et al.
**TL;DR:** Presents Pelican-Unify 1.0, a single model architecture that integrates visual understanding, commonsense reasoning, mental simulation ("imagination"), and action generation into a unified embodied intelligence framework. Achieves competitive or superior performance across perception, planning, and manipulation benchmarks without task-specific heads. Demonstrates zero-shot generalization to unseen environments through its imagination module.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `unified-model`, `reasoning`, `imagination`, `robotics`

## [Agentic Reasoning for Large Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.12538)
**Authors:** Anonymous et al.
**TL;DR:** Proposes a comprehensive framework for agentic reasoning in LLMs, organizing reasoning capabilities along three dimensions: intra-step thinking, inter-step planning, and environment-grounded reflection. Shows that combining all three dimensions yields substantially better performance on agentic benchmarks than any single dimension alone. Provides theoretical analysis of when and why each reasoning mode is necessary.
**Tags:** `agents`, `reasoning`, `LLM`, `planning`, `framework`
