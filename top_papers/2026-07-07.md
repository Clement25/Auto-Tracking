# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-07-07)

## [Your Agent's Memories Are Not Its Own: Forged Reasoning Attacks on LLM Agent Memory and Defenses](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05029)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2607.05029, submitted July 6, 2026)
**TL;DR:** Introduces FARMA (Forged Amplifying Rationale Memory Attack), which poisons an LLM agent's remembered reasoning traces rather than its factual knowledge — bypassing keyword-based and consensus-based defenses. The paper also proposes SENTINEL, a layered defense pipeline for detecting forged reasoning entries in agent memory.
**Tags:** `agents`, `memory`, `security`, `adversarial`, `llm`

## [Autonomous Information Seeking: A Roadmap for Agentic Recommender Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04433)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2607.04433, submitted July 5, 2026)

Proposes a roadmap covering architecture, benchmarking, and open challenges for building trustworthy agentic recommenders.
**Tags:** `agents`, `recommender-systems`, `llm`, `planning`, `information-retrieval`

## [Embodied.cpp: A Portable Inference Runtime of Embodied AI Models on Heterogeneous Robots](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02501)
**Authors:** Ling Xu, Chuyu Han, Borui Li, Hao Wu, Shiqi Jiang, Ting Cao, Chuanyou Li, Sheng Zhong, Shuai Wang
**TL;DR:** Presents a portable C++ inference runtime for embodied AI models (VLAs and world-action models) organized into five layers — input adapters, sequence builders, backbone execution, head plugins, and deployment adapters — enabling seamless deployment across heterogeneous edge devices, robots, and simulators from a single backend. Achieves latency-first fused inference without requiring model-specific Python stacks.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `deployment`, `inference`, `edge-computing`, `vla`

## [Qwen-RobotWorld Technical Report: Unifying Embodied World Modeling through Language-Conditioned Video Generation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17030)
**Authors:** Jie Zhang et al. (39 authors, Qwen Team / Alibaba)
**TL;DR:** Proposes a unified language-conditioned video world model built on a 60-layer double-stream MMDiT that couples Qwen2.5-VL semantics with video-VAE latents, trained on an 8.6M video-text corpus spanning 20+ embodiments and 500+ action categories. Achieves first place on EWM-Bench and DreamGen Bench, outperforming all open-source models on WorldModelBench and PBench.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `world-models`, `video-generation`, `robotics`, `vla`, `multimodal`

## [Multi-Agent Embodied Autonomous Driving: From V2X Information Exchange to Shared World Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13840)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2606.13840, June 2026)
**TL;DR:** Frames autonomous driving as a multi-agent embodied problem where vehicles and infrastructure share Shared World Models (SWMs) — cross-agent predictive representations maintained via V2X (vehicle-to-everything) communication. Demonstrates that multi-agent coordination through shared models significantly outperforms single-agent baselines in complex traffic scenarios.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `autonomous-driving`, `multi-agent`, `v2x`, `world-models`

**Authors:** (arXiv:2606.13672, June 2026)

## [Embodied-R1.5: Evolving Physical Intelligence via Embodied Foundation Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11324)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2606.11324, June 2026)

## [Safe Embodied AI for Long-horizon Tasks: A Cross-layer Analysis of Robotic Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05660)
