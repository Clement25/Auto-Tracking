## [Code as Agent Harness](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18747)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2605.18747, submitted May 18, 2026)
**TL;DR:** A survey centering code as the harness for agentic AI systems, providing a unified roadmap toward executable, verifiable, and stateful AI agents. Examines how harness mechanismsŌĆöplanning, memory, and tool useŌĆöenable long-horizon execution across coding assistants, GUI/OS automation, embodied agents, scientific discovery, and enterprise workflows.

## [AI Agents May Always Fall for Prompt Injections](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.17634)
**Authors:** Sahar Abdelnabi, Eugene Bagdasarian
**TL;DR:** Demonstrates that prompt injection is the most critical vulnerability in deployed AI agents, showing that prevailing defense paradigms fail to detect attacks operating through contextual manipulation rather than explicit adversarial strings. Argues that the attack surface is fundamentally unavoidable under current architectures.
**Tags:** `agents`, `security`, `prompt-injection`, `adversarial`

## [FutureSim: Replaying World Events to Evaluate Adaptive Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15188)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2605.15188, submitted May 14, 2026)
**TL;DR:** Proposes grounded simulations that replay chronological real-world news to test frontier agents on long-horizon adaptive forecasting beyond their knowledge cutoff. Benchmarking reveals a clear capability gapŌĆöthe best-performing agent achieves only 25% accuracy, with many agents performing worse than a no-prediction baseline, motivating research in long-horizon memory and uncertainty reasoning.
**Tags:** `agents`, `evaluation`, `benchmark`, `forecasting`, `world-models`

## [MIND-Skill: Quality-Guaranteed Skill Generation via Multi-Agent Induction and Deduction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.08670)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2605.08670, submitted May 9, 2026)
**TL;DR:** Addresses the failure of LLM-powered agents on complex multi-step tasks requiring domain-specific procedural knowledge by introducing a multi-agent pipeline that inductively generates and deductively validates reusable skills. The quality-guarantee mechanism sharply reduces error accumulation in long-horizon agentic tasks.
**Tags:** `agents`, `skill-learning`, `multi-agent`, `LLM`, `procedural-knowledge`

## [Planner Matters! An Efficient and Unbalanced Multi-agent Collaboration Framework for Long-horizon Planning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02168)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2605.02168, submitted May 4, 2026)
**TL;DR:** Proposes an unbalanced multi-agent framework composed of a planner, an actor, and a memory manager, and provides a systematic compute-allocation analysis showing that planning qualityŌĆönot execution or memoryŌĆöis the dominant factor in task performance. Demonstrates that investing disproportionately in the planner component yields the greatest gains on long-horizon benchmarks.
**Tags:** `agents`, `planning`, `multi-agent`, `long-horizon`, `LLM`

## [Security Risks in Tool-Enabled AI Agents: A Systematic Analysis of Privileged Execution Environments](https://arxiv.org/html/2605.09721v1)
**Authors:** Hardik Goel (accepted at IEEE COMPSAC 2026)
**TL;DR:** Presents a structured taxonomy of security risks arising when AI agents execute tools in cloud-hosted privileged environments, covering categories from credential leakage to privilege escalation. Proposes mitigation strategies and evaluation criteria for secure agent deployment at scale.
**Tags:** `agents`, `security`, `tool-use`, `cloud`, `risk-taxonomy`

## [Agentic Tool Use in Large Language Models](https://arxiv.org/html/2604.00835v1)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2604.00835, April 2026)
**TL;DR:** Organizes the tool-augmented LLM literature into three paradigms: prompting-as-plug-and-play, supervised tool learning, and reward-driven tool policy learning. Provides a unified framework to compare approaches and identifies open challenges in generalizing tool use to novel APIs and multi-step workflows.
**Tags:** `agents`, `tool-use`, `LLM`, `survey`, `reinforcement-learning`

## [From Assumptions to Actions: Turning LLM Reasoning into Uncertainty-Aware Planning for Embodied Agents](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.04326)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2602.04326, February 2026)

Shows significant improvement over baselines on manipulation and navigation benchmarks where prior knowledge is incomplete or noisy.
