# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-06-30)

## [A Survey on Autonomy-Induced Security Risks in Large Model-Based Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.23844)
**Authors:** Hang Su, Jun Luo, Chang Liu, Xiao Yang, Yichi Zhang, Yinpeng Dong, Jun Zhu
**TL;DR:** As LLM-based agents gain autonomy in open-ended environments, they introduce novel security risks beyond standalone LLMs — including memory poisoning, tool misuse, reward hacking, and emergent misalignment. The paper proposes R2A2 (Reflective Risk-Aware Agent Architecture), a unified framework grounded in Constrained Markov Decision Processes for principled, proactive safety across the agent decision-making loop.
**Tags:** `agents`, `security`, `safety`, `LLM`, `survey`

## [Agents' Last Exam (ALE)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05405)
**Authors:** UC Berkeley RDI Group (250+ industry expert collaborators)
**TL;DR:** ALE is a comprehensive benchmark for evaluating AI agents on long-horizon, economically valuable, real-world tasks with verifiable outcomes, covering 1K+ tasks across 55 sub-fields in 13 industry clusters. Current top agents achieve only a 25.2% pass rate, with the hardest tier averaging just 2.6%, revealing significant gaps between benchmark performance and real-world deployment readiness.
**Tags:** `agents`, `benchmark`, `evaluation`, `long-horizon`, `real-world`

## [OctoNav: Towards Generalist Embodied Navigation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.09839)
**Authors:** Chen Gao, Liankai Jin, Xingyu Peng, Jiazhao Zhang, Yue Deng, Annan Li, He Wang, Si Liu
**TL;DR:** OctoNav proposes a unified benchmark and model for generalist embodied navigation that handles arbitrary compounds of multi-modal, multi-capability instructions (ObjNav, ImgNav, VLN) within continuous environments. A Think-Before-Action Chain-of-Thought (TBA-CoT) dataset is introduced to ground navigation decisions in explicit reasoning, with OctoNav-R1 achieving CVPR 2026 acceptance.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `navigation`, `robotics`, `vision-language`, `benchmark`

## [Embodied AI in Action: Insights from SAE World Congress 2026 on Safety, Trust, Robotics, and Real-World Deployment](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10653)

## [IntentScore: Intent-Conditioned Action Evaluation for Computer-Use Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.05157)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors — see paper)
**TL;DR:** IntentScore introduces a new evaluation metric for computer-use agents that conditions action quality on inferred user intent rather than raw task completion, better capturing partial success and aligning with human judgment. The metric shows higher correlation with human preferences than existing step-level accuracy metrics on GUI automation benchmarks.
