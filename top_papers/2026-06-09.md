# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-06-09)

## [CORAL: Towards Autonomous Multi-Agent Evolution for Open-Ended Discovery](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01658)
**Authors:** Human-Agent-Society Team (Microsoft Research)
**TL;DR:** CORAL is the first framework for autonomous multi-agent evolution on open-ended problems, replacing rigid control flows with long-running agents that explore, reflect, and collaborate via shared persistent memory, asynchronous execution, and heartbeat-based interventions. Evaluated on 10 math/algorithmic/systems tasks, it achieves 3–10× higher improvement rates than fixed evolutionary-search baselines. On Anthropic's kernel engineering benchmark, four co-evolving agents improved the best known score from 1363 to 1103 cycles.
**Tags:** `agents`, `multi-agent`, `evolutionary-ai`, `open-ended-discovery`, `autonomous-systems`

## [DyTopo: Dynamic Topology Routing for Multi-Agent Reasoning via Semantic Matching](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.06039)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2602.06039, February 2026)
**TL;DR:** DyTopo addresses the failure of fixed communication topologies (chain, tree, fully-connected) in multi-agent reasoning by dynamically reconstructing a sparse directed communication graph at each reasoning round using semantic matching between agents' natural-language "need" and "offer" descriptors. Across code generation and math reasoning benchmarks with four LLM backbones, DyTopo consistently outperforms the strongest baseline by an average of +6.2 points while also yielding interpretable coordination traces.
**Tags:** `agents`, `multi-agent`, `reasoning`, `graph-topology`, `LLM`

## [Agentic Reasoning for Large Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.12538)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2601.12538, January 2026)
**TL;DR:** A comprehensive survey that examines how reasoning is integrated into LLM-based agents to drive planning, search, tool use, memory, and multi-agent coordination. The paper characterizes environmental dynamics through three layers: foundational agentic reasoning (planning, tool use, search), self-evolving agentic reasoning (feedback-driven memory adaptation), and collective multi-agent reasoning. It serves as a structured reference for the rapidly expanding field of agentic AI.
**Tags:** `agents`, `reasoning`, `planning`, `tool-use`, `survey`, `LLM`

## [Agentic Artificial Intelligence: Architectures, Taxonomies, and Evaluation of Large Language Model Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.12560)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2601.12560, January 2026)
**TL;DR:** This paper proposes a unified taxonomy decomposing LLM agents into six components—Perception, Brain, Planning, Action, Tool Use, and Collaboration—and investigates the architectures that let LLMs run complex workflows in software engineering, scientific discovery, and robotics. It also introduces standardized evaluation protocols across these domains, providing the field a common language for benchmarking agent capabilities.
**Tags:** `agents`, `taxonomy`, `evaluation`, `LLM`, `survey`

## [Externalization in LLM Agents: A Unified Review of Memory, Skills, Protocols and Harness Engineering](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.08224)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2604.08224, April 2026)
**TL;DR:** This review unifies the rapidly diverging literature on how LLM agents externalize cognition—storing knowledge in memory systems, encoding reusable behaviors as skills, coordinating via protocols, and orchestrating execution through harness engineering. By drawing connections across these four pillars, the paper offers a principled framework for building more capable, composable, and maintainable agentic systems.
**Tags:** `agents`, `memory`, `skills`, `harness-engineering`, `survey`, `LLM`

## [The Evolution of Tool Use in LLM Agents: From Single-Tool Call to Multi-Tool Orchestration](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2603.22862)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2603.22862, March 2026)
**TL;DR:** This paper traces the evolution of tool use in LLM agents from simple single-tool invocations to complex multi-tool orchestration pipelines, characterizing key design axes including tool selection, sequencing, error recovery, and parallelism. It synthesizes lessons from production deployments and academic systems alike, identifying open challenges around reliability and efficiency at scale.
**Tags:** `agents`, `tool-use`, `orchestration`, `LLM`

## [Embodied AI Agents: Modeling the World](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2506.22355)
**Authors:** Meta AI Research (arXiv:2506.22355, June 2026)
**TL;DR:** This Meta AI position paper argues that the next frontier of AI agents requires embodiment—grounding agents in visual, virtual, or physical forms such as virtual avatars, wearable devices, and robots that can perceive, learn, and act within their surroundings. It outlines key research challenges including world modeling, long-horizon planning, and the gap between simulated and real-world environments. The paper provides a research agenda for achieving robust embodied AI at scale.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `world-model`, `agents`, `Meta-AI`

## [Robot Learning from Human Videos: A Survey](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27621)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2604.27621, April 2026)
**TL;DR:** A comprehensive survey of methods for teaching robots manipulation skills from human video demonstrations, covering representation learning, imitation learning, video prediction, and reward shaping approaches. The survey highlights the promise of leveraging the vast quantity of existing human video data on the internet as a scalable supervision source for robot policy learning, and benchmarks key techniques on standard manipulation tasks.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `robot-learning`, `imitation-learning`, `manipulation`, `survey`

## [LAP: Language-Action Pre-Training Enables Zero-shot Cross-Embodiment Transfer](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.10556)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2602.10556, February 2026)
**TL;DR:** LAP introduces a language-action pre-training scheme that enables zero-shot transfer of manipulation policies across robot embodiments with different morphologies and action spaces. By aligning language instructions with action trajectories during pre-training on diverse robot datasets, LAP achieves strong zero-shot performance on unseen embodiments without any fine-tuning, significantly reducing the data cost of deploying new robots.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `cross-embodiment`, `zero-shot`, `manipulation`, `pre-training`

## [Being-h0.5: Scaling Human-Centric Robot Learning for Cross-Embodiment Generalization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.12993)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2601.12993, January 2026)
**TL;DR:** Being-h0.5 presents a scalable approach to human-centric robot learning that generalizes across a diverse set of robot embodiments by training on large-scale human motion data combined with robot demonstrations. The system achieves strong generalization to novel embodiments and tasks by learning shared human-robot action representations. It demonstrates that centering learning on human-like motion patterns substantially improves cross-embodiment transfer.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `cross-embodiment`, `human-robot-interaction`, `generalization`
