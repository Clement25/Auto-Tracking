# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-05-29)

## [Relevance as a Vulnerability: How Web Retrieval Degrades Safety Alignment in LLM Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.29224)
**Authors:** Aditya Nawal, Manit Baser, Mohan Gurusamy (National University of Singapore)
**TL;DR:** Web-retrieval augmentation in LLM agents paradoxically weakens safety alignment — even content from safety-oriented sources increases harmful compliance by ~25% over the no-retrieval baseline. The paper introduces AgentREVEAL, a diagnostic framework dissecting two axes of vulnerability: integration step binding and retrieved-content properties, revealing a "Safe Source Paradox." This is the most comprehensive study of retrieval-induced alignment degradation to date.
**Tags:** `agents`, `safety`, `llm`, `rag`, `alignment`

## [World Action Models: The Next Frontier in Embodied AI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12090)
**Authors:** Siyin Wang et al. (13 co-authors)
**TL;DR:** Introduces World Action Models (WAMs), a class of embodied foundation models that unify predictive world-state modeling with action generation in a single architecture. The paper proposes a taxonomy of Cascaded and Joint WAMs, analyzes data ecosystems spanning teleoperation, simulation, and internet-scale egocentric video, and synthesizes emerging evaluation protocols around visual fidelity, physical commonsense, and action plausibility. This is the first systematic survey of the WAMs landscape.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `world-models`, `robotics`, `foundation-models`, `survey`

## [RoboJailBench: Benchmarking Adversarial Attacks and Defenses in Embodied Robotic Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19328)
**Authors:** Doguhuan Yeke, Yanming Zhou, Leo Y. Lin, Hongyu Cai, Antonio Bianchi, Z. Berkay Celik
**TL;DR:** The first standardized benchmark for evaluating jailbreak attacks on VLM-powered embodied robots and autonomous vehicles, addressing the gap left by chat-model-focused security benchmarks. RoboJailBench defines adversarial input categories, physical consequence metrics, and a security–utility trade-off evaluation framework. It exposes significant weaknesses in current defenses when attackers exploit the visual-language-action pipeline.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `safety`, `adversarial`, `benchmark`, `vlm`

## [Embodied Multi-Agent Coordination by Aligning World Models Through Dialogue](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12920)
**Authors:** Vardhan Dongre, Dilek Hakkani-Tür (University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign)
**TL;DR:** Extends the PARTNR household-robotics benchmark with a natural-language dialogue channel so two partially-observing agents can share observations and align their internal world models during task execution. The key finding is that communication-grounded world-model alignment dramatically reduces coordination failures in partially observable environments, outperforming silent action-only baselines. Provides concrete evidence that dialogue is a first-class coordination primitive for embodied LLM agents.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `multi-agent`, `world-models`, `dialogue`, `robotics`, `llm`

## [A Methodology for Selecting and Composing Runtime Architecture Patterns for Production LLM Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20173)
**Authors:** Vasundra Srinivasan
**TL;DR:** Proposes a systematic decision framework for choosing and combining runtime architectural patterns (e.g., ReAct, Plan-and-Execute, reflection loops) when deploying LLM agents in production. The methodology accounts for latency, reliability, cost, and safety constraints, offering practitioners a principled alternative to ad-hoc pattern selection. Evaluated against real-world industrial deployment scenarios.
**Tags:** `agents`, `llm`, `software-engineering`, `production`, `architecture`

## [MIND-Skill: Quality-Guaranteed Skill Generation via Multi-Agent Induction and Deduction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.08670)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Addresses a core bottleneck for LLM agents: the lack of high-quality, domain-specific procedural skills for complex multi-step real-world tasks. MIND-Skill uses a multi-agent induction-deduction loop to iteratively generate, critique, and refine skills, with quality guarantees enforced at each step. Experiments show consistent outperformance over concurrent skill-generation methods across diverse task domains.
**Tags:** `agents`, `llm`, `skill-learning`, `multi-agent`, `planning`

## [Security Risks in Tool-Enabled AI Agents: A Systematic Analysis of Privileged Execution Environments](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.09721)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors; accepted at IEEE COMPSAC 2026)
**TL;DR:** Presents a structured taxonomy of security risks arising when AI agents execute tools with elevated privileges in cloud-hosted environments, covering prompt injection, tool misuse, data exfiltration, and privilege escalation. Proposes a risk classification framework and maps each category to concrete mitigation strategies. The first systematic analysis of privileged-execution security for production AI agent deployments.
**Tags:** `agents`, `security`, `tool-use`, `llm`, `cloud`

## [3D Generation for Embodied AI and Robotic Simulation: A Survey](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26509)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Surveys how recent 3D generative models (NeRF, Gaussian Splatting, diffusion-based 3D) can supply simulation-ready assets — with correct kinematic structure, material properties, and articulation — for embodied AI training environments. Identifies the gap between visual-quality generation and physically-plausible simulation assets as the critical open problem. Covers datasets, generation pipelines, and evaluation protocols for sim-to-real transfer.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `3d-generation`, `simulation`, `survey`

## [AI Agents vs. Agentic AI: A Conceptual Taxonomy, Applications and Challenges](https://huggingface.co/papers/2505.10468)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Draws a principled conceptual distinction between AI Agents (discrete, task-scoped systems) and Agentic AI (persistent, self-directed systems with long-horizon autonomy), and proposes a unified taxonomy covering architecture, memory, tool use, and goal-setting. The paper maps existing systems onto this taxonomy and highlights under-explored areas such as agent identity, accountability, and graceful degradation. Trending on Hugging Face Papers.
**Tags:** `agents`, `agentic-ai`, `taxonomy`, `llm`, `survey`

## [A Compound AI Agent for Conversational Grant Discovery](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02366)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Proposes a compound AI system for research funding discovery that autonomously aggregates ~12,000 federal and nonprofit grant opportunities from fragmented web sources using LLM-equipped browser agents, then exposes them via a ReAct-based conversational query layer. Demonstrates how compound agent architectures can replace brittle rule-based scrapers and keyword-search portals in high-stakes information retrieval domains. Provides a replicable blueprint for domain-specific agentic data pipelines.
**Tags:** `agents`, `compound-ai`, `rag`, `llm`, `information-retrieval`
