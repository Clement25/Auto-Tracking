# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-06-04)

## [CORAL: Towards Autonomous Multi-Agent Evolution for Open-Ended Discovery](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01658)
**Authors:** Human-Agent-Society Team
**TL;DR:** CORAL is the first framework for autonomous multi-agent evolution on open-ended problems, replacing rigid control with long-running agents that explore, reflect, and collaborate through shared persistent memory and asynchronous execution. Evaluated on 10 mathematical, algorithmic, and systems optimization tasks, it achieves 3–10× higher improvement rates than fixed evolutionary search baselines. The system is compatible with Claude Code, Codex, Cursor, and other modern coding agents.
**Tags:** `agents`, `multi-agent`, `autonomous-systems`, `self-evolution`, `open-ended-discovery`

**Authors:** (Multiple authors, April 2026)

## [Being-H0.5: Scaling Human-Centric Robot Learning for Cross-Embodiment Generalization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.12993)
**Authors:** BeingBeyond Team (January 2026)
**TL;DR:** Being-H0.5 is a Vision-Language-Action (VLA) foundation model that treats human interaction traces as a universal "mother tongue" for physical manipulation, enabling cross-embodiment generalization across 30 distinct robotic platforms. It introduces UniHand-2.0, the largest embodied pre-training dataset to date (35,000+ hours across egocentric human video, robot manipulation, and visual-language data), and a Mixture-of-Flow framework to decouple shared motor primitives from embodiment-specific experts. The model achieves state-of-the-art results on LIBERO (98.9%) and RoboCasa (53.9%) benchmarks.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `VLA`, `cross-embodiment`, `foundation-model`, `robot-learning`

## [HY-Embodied-0.5: Embodied Foundation Models for Real-World Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07430)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors, April 2026)
**TL;DR:** HY-Embodied-0.5 introduces an embodied foundation model family that bridges LLM agents and physical robots, featuring a 2B/4B multimodal Mixture-of-Transformers model optimized for real-time responsiveness and a 32B/407B Mixture-of-Experts model for complex reasoning tasks. The architecture unifies perception, language grounding, and low-latency motor control in a single model family deployable across diverse robotic hardware. Evaluations show strong performance on both simulated benchmarks and real-world manipulation tasks.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `foundation-model`, `VLA`, `mixture-of-experts`

## [Robot Learning from Human Videos: A Survey](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27621)

The survey includes a comprehensive benchmark comparison and identifies open problems in sim-to-real transfer and temporal generalization.

## [Embodied Robot Manipulation in the Era of Foundation Models: Planning and Learning Perspectives](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.22983)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors, December 2025 / updated 2026)
**TL;DR:** This paper surveys how foundation models are reshaping robot manipulation by separating fast reactive control from slower deliberative reasoning, with lightweight policies handling low-latency execution while larger vision-language models perform high-level planning. It provides a systematic review of recent VLA architectures and highlights the role of world models in enabling predictive planning for dexterous manipulation. The paper also discusses key challenges in grounding, long-horizon planning, and safe deployment.

## [A Survey of Robotic Navigation and Manipulation with Physics Simulators in the Era of Embodied AI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.01458)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors, May 2025)
**TL;DR:** This survey comprehensively reviews the role of physics simulators in training embodied AI agents for navigation and manipulation, covering benchmark datasets, evaluation metrics, simulation platforms, and cutting-edge methods including world models and geometric equivariance techniques. The survey serves as a resource for practitioners building embodied AI systems where real-world data collection is prohibitively expensive.

## [ARTIST: Agentic Reasoning and Tool Integration in Self-Improving Transformers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.01441)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors, May 2025)
**TL;DR:** ARTIST empowers LLMs to learn optimal strategies for leveraging external tools and interacting with complex environments via reinforcement learning, without relying on hand-crafted reward functions or supervised demonstrations. The method trains agents to decide when and how to invoke tools through trial-and-error, achieving significant gains on math reasoning, code execution, and web navigation benchmarks. It represents an important step toward self-improving agent systems that generalize tool use to novel environments.
**Tags:** `agents`, `tool-use`, `reinforcement-learning`, `llm`, `self-improvement`
