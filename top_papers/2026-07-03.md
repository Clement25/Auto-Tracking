# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-07-03)

## [Tmax: A Simple Recipe for Terminal Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23321)
**Authors:** Hamish Ivison, Junjie Oscar Yin, Rulin Shao, Teng Xiao, Nathan Lambert, Hannaneh Hajishirzi
**TL;DR:** Tmax is the strongest open RL recipe for terminal-using agents to date, achieving 27% on Terminal-Bench 2.0 with only 9B parameters — outperforming much larger prior models. The authors introduce a novel data-generation taxonomy combining difficulty control, personas, and verifier diversification to produce over 2.5× more terminal-agent training data than any previously released dataset.
**Tags:** `agents`, `terminal-agents`, `reinforcement-learning`, `open-source`, `benchmarking`

## [Harnessing the Collective Intelligence of AI Agents in the Wild for New Discoveries](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10402)
**Authors:** Federico Bianchi, Yongchan Kwon, Aneesh Pappu, James Zou (Together AI & Stanford University)
**TL;DR:** Introduces EinsteinArena, an agent-native platform for open distributed scientific research where LLM-based agents share partial results, inspect failures, and build on each other's ideas. As of May 2026, agents on the platform have discovered 12 new state-of-the-art results surpassing all prior human and AI solutions — demonstrating that collective agent intelligence can accelerate frontier scientific discovery.
**Tags:** `agents`, `scientific-discovery`, `collective-intelligence`, `multi-agent`, `open-science`

## [Act As a Real Researcher: A Suite of Benchmarks Evaluating Frontier LLMs and Agentic Harnesses in Research Lifecycle](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.07462)
**Authors:** Jiayu Wang, Weijiang Lv, Bowen Fu, Jing Fu, Jiayi Song, Lingyu Zhang, Lanxuan Xue, Luodi Chen, Zepeng Xin, Kaiyu Li, Xiangyong Cao
**TL;DR:** Proposes AARR (Act As a Real Researcher), a benchmark series probing whether agents can emulate professional research practices at a granular level — field sensitivity, research ethics, and scientific nuance — rather than just macro-level task execution. Even the best tested configuration (Mini-SWE-Agent + Claude Opus 4.7) achieves only 68.3% success, revealing systematic gaps between AI and human researchers.
**Tags:** `agents`, `benchmarking`, `research-agents`, `evaluation`, `LLM`

## [StepFinder: A Temporal Semantic Framework for Failure Attribution in Multi-Agent Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03467)
**Authors:** Taiyu Zhu, Yifan Wu, Weilin Jin, Ying Li, Gang Huang
**TL;DR:** Presents StepFinder, a temporal semantic framework that automatically identifies which agent is responsible for a failure and at which step it occurred in complex multi-agent pipelines. Accepted at KDD '26, the method uses execution trace mining and temporal semantic modeling to provide interpretable failure attribution — a critical tool for debugging production multi-agent systems.
**Tags:** `multi-agent`, `failure-attribution`, `debugging`, `temporal-semantics`, `agents`

## [How Much Coordination Gain Is Real? A Paired Noise-Floor Protocol for Multi-Agent LLM Benchmarks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20695)
**Authors:** Alibek T. Kaliyev (UT Austin), Artem Maryanskyy (Uber)
**TL;DR:** Challenges the validity of reported coordination gains in multi-agent LLM papers by establishing a paired noise-floor protocol. Testing Claude Haiku 4.5 on τ²-bench retail, the authors find that 7 out of 10 recent multi-agent coordination papers report headline performance deltas below the measured noise floor, meaning their claimed coordination improvements may be statistical artifacts rather than genuine advances.
**Tags:** `multi-agent`, `benchmarking`, `evaluation`, `methodology`, `reproducibility`

## [Embodied-R1.5: Evolving Physical Intelligence via Embodied Foundation Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11324)
**Authors:** Yifu Yuan, Yaoting Huang, Xianze Yao, Yutong Li, Shuoheng Zhang, Linqi Han, Pengyi Li, Jiangeng Sun, et al.
**TL;DR:** A unified Embodied Foundation Model that integrates embodied cognition, task planning, error correction, and spatial pointing into a single 8B-parameter architecture via a Planner-Grounder-Corrector (PGC) closed-loop framework. Built on a 15B+ token data system with a multi-task balanced RL recipe, Embodied-R1.5 achieves SOTA on 16 of 24 embodied VLM benchmarks, surpassing Gemini-Robotics-ER-1.5 and GPT-5.4.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `foundation-models`, `reinforcement-learning`, `VLA`

## [Hy-Embodied-0.5-VLA: From Vision-Language-Action Models to a Real-World Robot Learning Stack](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14409)
**Authors:** He Zhang, Lingzhu Xiang, Haitao Lin, Zeyu Huang, Minghui Wang, Dingyan Zhong, Yubo Dong, Yihao Wu, Yongming Rao, et al. (Tencent Robotics X & Tencent Hy Team)
**TL;DR:** Introduces HyVLA-0.5, an end-to-end system spanning the complete robot learning stack — from data collection and model design through supervised fine-tuning, RL post-training, and real-world deployment. The work bridges the gap between VLA research and production robotics by detailing practical engineering choices at every layer of the stack.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `VLA`, `robotics`, `real-world-deployment`, `vision-language-action`

## [Safe Embodied AI for Long-horizon Tasks: A Cross-layer Analysis of Robotic Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05660)
**Authors:** Dabin Kim, Daemin Park, Sangyub Lee, Jinsik Kim, Yeongtak Oh, Jongho Shin, Sungroh Yoon
**TL;DR:** A structured 63-page survey examining safety in long-horizon robotic manipulation across three intervention loci — planning-time, policy-time, and execution-time — analyzing formal guarantees, statistical support, and empirical safety heuristics for each. Identifies how semantic misgrounding, subtask-level error propagation, execution drift, and contact-rich physical risk can accumulate within a single closed-loop system.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `safety`, `robotic-manipulation`, `survey`, `long-horizon`

## [Embodied AI in Action: Insights from SAE World Congress 2026 on Safety, Trust, Robotics, and Real-World Deployment](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10653)
**Authors:** SAE World Congress 2026 Panel Contributors
**TL;DR:** White paper synthesizing expert perspectives from the SAE World Congress 2026 panel on embodied AI across automotive, robotics, and industrial domains. Highlights that embodied AI is rapidly transitioning from research to real-world systems such as autonomous vehicles and mobile robots, while introducing new challenges in safety, trust, governance, and operational reliability.

## [The Organizational Behavior of Agentic AI: Collective Intelligence in Human-Agent Workflows](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30986)
**Authors:** (arxiv.org/abs/2606.30986v1)
