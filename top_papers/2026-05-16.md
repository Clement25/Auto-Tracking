## [MIND-Skill: Quality-Guaranteed Skill Generation via Multi-Agent Induction and Deduction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.08670)
**Authors:** Yixuan Li, Mingshu Cai, Ziyang Xiao, Wanyuan Wang, Yanchen Deng, Bo An
**TL;DR:** MIND-Skill is a framework that automatically induces reusable agent skills from successful task trajectories using a two-agent loop: an induction agent abstracts generalizable skills and a deduction agent reconstructs the original trajectories to verify quality. Quality is enforced via reconstruction, outcome, and rubric losses, removing the need for human experts to manually curate procedural knowledge.
**Tags:** `agents`, `skill-generation`, `llm`, `multi-agent`

## [World Action Models: The Next Frontier in Embodied AI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12090)
**Authors:** Siyin Wang et al. (14 authors)
**TL;DR:** This paper introduces World Action Models (WAMs), embodied foundation models that jointly predict future environment states and actions rather than producing reactive observation-to-action mappings alone. The authors provide a unified conceptual framework synthesizing fragmented literature across architectures, learning objectives, data ecosystems (teleoperation, human demos, simulation, egocentric video), and emerging evaluation protocols.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `world-models`, `robotics`, `foundation-models`, `VLA`

## [World Model for Robot Learning: A Comprehensive Survey](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00080)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** A broad survey of world models as a core component of robot learning, covering their roles in policy learning, planning, simulation, and data generation. The paper synthesizes recent VLA models, latent-space forecasting approaches (e.g., LDA-1B, FRAPPE), and argues that actionable world models need foresight, imagination-driven planning, and data amplification capabilities.
