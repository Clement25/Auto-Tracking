# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-06-01)

## [Embodied AI Agents: Modeling the World](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.22355)
**Authors:** Pascale Fung, Yoram Bachrach, Asli Celikyilmaz, et al. (Meta AI Research)
**TL;DR:** This position paper argues that world models are central to the next generation of embodied AI agents—robots, avatars, and wearables—that must perceive, reason, and act in dynamic physical and social environments. It outlines key research directions including privacy-aware perception, social intelligence, and the ethical implications of anthropomorphic embodied systems.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `world-models`, `robotics`, `social-ai`, `ethics`

## [Qwen-VLA: Unifying Vision-Language-Action Modeling across Tasks, Environments, and Robot Embodiments](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30280)
**Authors:** QwenLM Team, Alibaba AI (40+ researchers)
**TL;DR:** Qwen-VLA extends the Qwen vision-language stack with a DiT-based continuous action decoder, unifying manipulation, navigation, and trajectory prediction across multiple robot platforms without discretizing motor commands. It achieves 97.9% on LIBERO, 76.9% OOD success on real-world ALOHA experiments, and 26.6% zero-shot success on the challenging DOMINO dynamic manipulation benchmark.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `vision-language-action`, `manipulation`, `navigation`, `foundation-models`

## [Unified Vision-Language-Action Model](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.19850)
**Authors:** (Multiple institutions, June 2026)
**TL;DR:** Proposes a unified VLA framework that casts diverse robotic control tasks—grasping, locomotion, and long-horizon planning—into a single generative model trained end-to-end. The unified approach demonstrates strong cross-task transfer and substantially outperforms task-specific baselines in low-data regimes.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `vision-language-action`, `robotics`, `generalization`

## [AndroTMem: From Interaction Trajectories to Anchored Memory in Long-Horizon GUI Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.18429)
**Authors:** CVC2233 et al. (March 2026)
**TL;DR:** Introduces AndroTMem-Bench (1,069 tasks, 34,473 steps) to diagnose why GUI agents fail on long-horizon Android tasks, finding that within-task memory loss—not perception errors—is the primary bottleneck. The proposed Anchored Structured Memory (ASM) mechanism improves Task Complete Rate by 5–30% over full-sequence replay and summary-based baselines.
**Tags:** `agents`, `gui-agents`, `memory`, `android`, `benchmark`, `long-horizon`

## [VTC-Bench: Evaluating Agentic Multimodal Models via Compositional Visual Tool Chaining](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.15030)
**Authors:** Researchers from PKU, NTU, USTC, CQU, NUDT, CASIA, Meituan (March 2026)
**TL;DR:** VTC-Bench evaluates multimodal LLMs on compositional sequences of 32 OpenCV-based visual operations organized into a 9-category cognitive hierarchy across 680 curated problems. Even the best model (Gemini-3.0-Pro) scores only 51%, revealing that multi-tool composition remains a critical and largely unsolved challenge for agentic MLLMs.
**Tags:** `agents`, `multimodal`, `benchmark`, `tool-use`, `visual-reasoning`

## [AMA: Adaptive Memory via Multi-Agent Collaboration](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.20352)
**Authors:** (January 2026)
**TL;DR:** AMA introduces a four-agent memory system—Constructor, Retriever, Judge, and Refresher—that collaboratively manages hierarchical, multi-granularity memory for long-term LLM agent interactions. Experiments show AMA significantly outperforms state-of-the-art baselines on long-context benchmarks while reducing token consumption by ~80% compared to full-context methods.
**Tags:** `agents`, `memory`, `multi-agent`, `long-context`, `rag`

## [Towards a Science of AI Agent Reliability](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.16666)
**Authors:** (February 2026)
**TL;DR:** Proposes a holistic reliability framework comprising 12 metrics across four dimensions—consistency, robustness, predictability, and safety—and evaluates 14 frontier models on two complementary benchmarks. The study finds significant reliability gaps even in top-performing agents and calls for reliability as a first-class research criterion alongside raw capability.
**Tags:** `agents`, `reliability`, `safety`, `evaluation`, `benchmark`

## [Rethinking Agentic Reinforcement Learning In Large Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27859)
**Authors:** (April 2026)
**TL;DR:** Introduces Agent-Omit, an RL-trained LLM agent that adaptively omits redundant reasoning steps and irrelevant observations, achieving efficient long-horizon task completion. The paper also presents ClawGUI, a comprehensive framework for GUI agent training with real-device deployment and rigorous online benchmarking.
**Tags:** `agents`, `reinforcement-learning`, `llm`, `efficiency`, `gui-agents`

## [AgentRxiv: Towards Collaborative Autonomous Research](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.18102)
**Authors:** Samuel Schmidgall (Johns Hopkins), Michael Moor (ETH Zurich)
**TL;DR:** AgentRxiv is a shared preprint server for LLM agent laboratories, enabling agents to upload, retrieve, and iteratively build on each other's research outputs. Agents with access to prior collaborative work achieve 13.7% relative improvement over baseline on MATH-500 versus isolated agents (11.4%), demonstrating the value of cumulative knowledge-sharing in autonomous research pipelines.
**Tags:** `agents`, `autonomous-research`, `multi-agent`, `science`, `llm`

---

## [A Survey of Robotic Navigation and Manipulation with Physics Simulators in the Era of Embodied AI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.01458)
**Authors:** (May 2025, updated 2026)
**TL;DR:** Comprehensive survey analyzing physics simulators for embodied AI training, with particular focus on the sim-to-real gap and how world models and geometric equivariance are narrowing it. Covers benchmark datasets, evaluation metrics, and the latest methods across navigation and manipulation tasks.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `simulation`, `sim-to-real`, `navigation`, `manipulation`, `survey`

## [A Vision for Auto Research with LLM Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.18765)
**Authors:** (April 2025)
**TL;DR:** Lays out a roadmap for fully automated scientific research using LLM agents, covering hypothesis generation, experimental design, code execution, result interpretation, and paper writing within closed-loop pipelines. The paper identifies key bottlenecks—verifiability, grounding, and iterative self-improvement—and proposes concrete architectural solutions for each.
**Tags:** `agents`, `autonomous-research`, `llm`, `science`, `automation`
