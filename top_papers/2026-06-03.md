# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-06-03)

## [K-BrowseComp: A Web Browsing Agent Benchmark Grounded in Korean Contexts](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02404)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2606.02404, submitted June 1, 2026)
**TL;DR:** Introduces a new benchmark for evaluating web browsing agents specifically in Korean-language contexts, testing whether general-purpose agents can effectively navigate and retrieve information from Korean web pages. ## [An Agentic Approach Towards Replication Package Quality Evaluation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02006)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2606.02006, submitted June 1, 2026)
**TL;DR:** Proposes using an LLM-driven agentic pipeline to automatically evaluate the quality of research replication packages by operationalizing open-science guidelines into machine-actionable verification procedures. The agent checks completeness, reproducibility, and documentation quality, offering a scalable alternative to human review.
**Tags:** `agents`, `software-engineering`, `open-science`, `evaluation`, `LLM`

## [Site4Drug: Predicting Drug-Binding Target Sites with an AI Agent](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01816)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2606.01816, submitted June 1, 2026)
**TL;DR:** A modality-aware AI agent that predicts drug-binding target sites on proteins, outputting a ranked list of targetable regions with explicit constraints, evidence summaries, risk flags, and a traceable decision log. Demonstrates that agentic reasoning can add interpretability and reliability to computational drug discovery pipelines.
**Tags:** `agents`, `drug-discovery`, `bioinformatics`, `tool-use`, `reasoning`

## [Engineering Robustness into Personal Agents with the AI Workflow Store](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10907)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2605.10907, submitted May 2026)
**TL;DR:** Addresses critical failure modes in personal AI agents — such as deleting inboxes or erasing codebases when given ambiguous or adversarial instructions — by introducing a sandboxed "AI Workflow Store" that enforces intent-boundary constraints at runtime. Demonstrates significant reduction in catastrophic agent errors across real-world personal productivity tasks.
**Tags:** `agents`, `safety`, `robustness`, `personal-agents`, `workflow`

## [HY-Embodied-0.5: Embodied Foundation Models for Real-World Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07430)
**Authors:** Tencent Robotics X & HY Vision Team (arXiv:2604.07430, submitted April 8, 2026)
**TL;DR:** Introduces a family of embodied foundation models with a Mixture-of-Transformers (MoT) architecture in two sizes (2B edge deployment, 32B for complex reasoning), designed to enhance spatial-temporal perception and embodied reasoning for planning and interaction. The 2B model outperforms similarly-sized state-of-the-art models on 16 of 22 benchmarks, and a derived VLA model achieves strong real-world robot control results.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `foundation-models`, `robotics`, `VLA`, `vision-language`

## [Evolvable Embodied Agent for Robotic Manipulation via Long Short-Term Reflection and Optimization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13533)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2604.13533, submitted April 2026)
**TL;DR:** Proposes EEAgent, a framework leveraging large vision-language models for environmental interpretation and policy planning, paired with a novel long short-term reflective optimization mechanism that dynamically refines prompts based on episodic memory and accumulated lessons. Demonstrates continual self-improvement on manipulation benchmarks without retraining.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `manipulation`, `reflection`, `VLM`

## [Demonstration-Free Robotic Control via LLM Agents (FAEA)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.20334)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2601.20334, submitted January 2026)
**TL;DR:** Introduces FAEA (Frontier Agent as Embodied Agent), which applies a standard LLM agent framework directly to robotic manipulation without task-specific demonstrations or fine-tuning. Achieves 84.9%, 85.7%, and 96% success rates on LIBERO, ManiSkill3, and MetaWorld benchmarks respectively, suggesting frontier agents can act as general manipulation controllers.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `LLM-agents`, `manipulation`, `zero-shot`

## [From Language to Action: Can LLM-Based Agents Be Used for Embodied Robot Cognition?](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03148)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2603.03148, submitted March 2026)
**TL;DR:** Empirically evaluates LLM-driven agents on structured embodied tasks, finding that while agents can exhibit emergent adaptation and memory-guided planning, they also reveal significant limitations including task-success hallucinations and poor low-level instruction following. Provides a detailed failure taxonomy to guide future embodied agent design.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `LLM-agents`, `evaluation`, `cognition`
