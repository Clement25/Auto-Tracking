# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-07-11)

## [What LLM Agents Say When No One Is Watching: Social Structure and Latent Objective Emergence in Multi-Agent Debates](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02507)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2607.02507, submitted July 2, 2026)
**TL;DR:** Introduces a dual-channel debate framework where LLM agents have both a public channel (visible to all) and a private/off-the-record channel, studying whether social structure changes what agents express. The paper finds that latent objectives can emerge and diverge from publicly stated positions, raising important alignment concerns for multi-agent systems. Results show that hidden coordination can significantly alter collective outcomes even when public behavior appears cooperative.
**Tags:** `agents`, `multi-agent`, `alignment`, `LLM`, `evaluation`

## [From Atomic Actions to Standard Operating Procedures: Iterative Tool Optimization for Self-Evolving LLM Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07321)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2607.07321, submitted July 8, 2026)
**TL;DR:** Proposes a framework for self-evolving LLM agents that iteratively compose atomic actions into higher-level Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs), reducing token overhead and improving task success rates on complex multi-step workflows. The approach enables agents to build reusable tool chains from experience rather than re-planning from scratch on each run. Evaluated on software engineering and web automation benchmarks showing significant efficiency gains.
**Tags:** `agents`, `tool-use`, `self-evolving`, `LLM`, `automation`

## [An Experimental Design Approach to Evaluating Agentic AI's Autonomous Model Discovery](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06413)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2607.06413, submitted July 2026)
**TL;DR:** Evaluates agentic AI systems (including Codex and Claude Code) under controlled experimental conditions varying reasoning effort, task type, optimization metric, and training data composition for autonomous scientific model discovery. The study uses a rigorous factorial experimental design to disentangle the contributions of each factor to agent success. Finds that reasoning effort and task framing have the strongest effect on discovery quality, outweighing model architecture differences.
**Tags:** `agents`, `evaluation`, `autonomous-science`, `coding-agents`, `benchmarks`

## [Harnessing the Collective Intelligence of AI Agents in the Wild for New Discoveries](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10402)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2606.10402)
**TL;DR:** Presents a framework for orchestrating large populations of heterogeneous AI agents to conduct open-ended scientific discovery, pooling diverse reasoning strategies to explore hypothesis spaces no single agent can cover. The system introduces a collective memory architecture that aggregates and deduplicates agent findings, enabling cumulative knowledge building across agent generations. Demonstrated on chemistry and biology discovery tasks, achieving novel compound identification beyond any single-agent baseline.
**Tags:** `agents`, `multi-agent`, `scientific-discovery`, `collective-intelligence`

## [AI Agents Should Help Build Preferences, Not Just Elicit Them](https://aiweekly.co/alerts/paper-ai-agents-should-help-build-preferences-not-just-elicit)
**Authors:** Irena Saracay, Ludwig Schmidt, Carlos Guestrin
**TL;DR:** Argues that conversational AI agents currently assume users have well-formed preferences to be elicited, when in reality many users—especially non-experts—need help constructing preferences through dialogue. Introduces CoShop, an interactive shopping benchmark where no tested agent exceeded 56% accuracy after five turns, exposing a fundamental gap in preference-building capability. Proposes a new evaluation paradigm measuring how well agents help users discover what they actually want.
**Tags:** `agents`, `evaluation`, `preference-learning`, `human-AI-interaction`

## [Safe Embodied AI for Long-horizon Tasks: A Cross-layer Analysis of Robotic Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05660)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2606.05660)
**TL;DR:** Provides a comprehensive cross-layer safety analysis for embodied AI systems performing long-horizon manipulation tasks, covering perception, planning, and actuation layers jointly. The paper identifies failure modes that arise specifically from the composition of individually safe components and proposes cross-layer safety constraints that prevent emergent unsafe behaviors. Validated on a suite of household and industrial manipulation tasks with significantly reduced catastrophic failure rates.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `safety`, `manipulation`, `long-horizon`

## [PhysReflect-VLA: Physical Feasibility and Self-Reflective Regulation for Reliable Vision-Language-Action Policies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27146)
**Authors:** Jiayu Yang et al.
**TL;DR:** Augments vision-language-action policies with a physical feasibility checker and a self-reflective regulation module that pauses execution when predicted actions violate physical constraints, triggering re-planning. This reduces catastrophic failures in contact-rich manipulation tasks where standard VLA policies often propose physically impossible actions. Tested across tabletop and dexterous manipulation tasks, achieving a 38% reduction in failure-to-recover episodes.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `VLA`, `robotics`, `safety`, `self-reflection`

## [PolicyTrim: Boosting Intrinsic Policy Efficiency of Vision-Language-Action Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.22540)
**Authors:** Xianghui Wang, Feng Chen, Wenbo Zhang, Hua Yan, Zixuan Wang, Changsheng Li et al.
**TL;DR:** Proposes PolicyTrim, a structured pruning and distillation method that reduces VLA model size by up to 60% while preserving 95%+ of task performance, enabling deployment on edge robotic hardware. The method identifies and removes action-irrelevant attention heads and MLP neurons using a novel saliency metric derived from manipulation task gradients. Evaluated on multiple robot platforms, showing real-time inference speeds on consumer-grade GPUs.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `VLA`, `efficiency`, `model-compression`, `robotics`

## [What Matters in Orchestrating Robot Policies: A Systematic Study of Hierarchical VLA Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10267)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2606.10267)
**TL;DR:** Conducts a large-scale ablation study across hierarchical VLA agent designs—varying task planner, skill policy, and inter-level communication protocol—to identify which design choices most impact real-robot manipulation performance. Finds that the fidelity of high-level plan grounding is more important than the capacity of individual skill policies, challenging common intuitions about where to invest compute. Releases a standardized benchmark for hierarchical robot policy evaluation.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `VLA`, `robotics`, `hierarchical-agents`, `evaluation`

## [OneVLA: A Unified Framework for Embodied Tasks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01241)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2606.01241)
**TL;DR:** Presents OneVLA, a single model trained jointly on navigation, manipulation, and object interaction tasks using a unified action tokenization scheme, eliminating the need for task-specific heads or fine-tuning. The model achieves strong cross-task generalization, with skills learned in one task transferring meaningfully to others through shared visual-semantic representations. Evaluated on 12 embodied benchmarks spanning indoor navigation, tabletop manipulation, and mobile manipulation.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `VLA`, `multi-task`, `robotics`, `generalization`
