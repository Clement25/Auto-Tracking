# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-07-17)

## [Do AI Agents Know When a Task Is Simple? Toward Complexity-Aware Reasoning and Execution](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13034)
**Authors:** Junjie Yin, Xinyu Feng
**TL;DR:** This paper examines how LLM agents automating multi-step workflows systematically lack task-aware execution-scope estimation — they apply the same reasoning overhead to trivial and hard tasks alike. The authors propose a complexity-aware framework that classifies tasks at inference time and routes them to appropriately scaled reasoning chains, significantly reducing latency and cost on simple tasks while preserving quality on complex ones.
**Tags:** `agents`, `reasoning`, `planning`, `efficiency`, `benchmarks`

## [HAS-Bench: Evaluating LLM-Based Human-Agent Systems under Configurable Human Participation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04329)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** HAS-Bench introduces a benchmark for evaluating LLM-based systems where human participation is configurable — from fully autonomous to highly interactive modes. The work reveals that current agents fail gracefully when human involvement decreases and do not adapt their planning strategies to varying levels of human oversight. Results highlight significant gaps in agent robustness across human-in-the-loop scenarios.
**Tags:** `agents`, `evaluation`, `benchmarks`, `human-agent`, `multi-agent`

---

## [Self-Governing Context for Long-Horizon LLM Agents (Self-GC)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00871)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Self-GC addresses the context management problem for agents operating over long horizons, where accumulated context degrades performance. The method lets agents dynamically self-prune, summarize, and restructure their context window based on task state, achieving better task completion on extended multi-step benchmarks than fixed-window or full-context baselines.
**Tags:** `agents`, `long-horizon`, `context-management`, `LLMs`

---

## [Self-Evolving Agents with Anytime-Valid Certificates](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00913)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** This paper introduces a framework in which agents iteratively refine their own policies while generating formal correctness certificates that are valid at any point in the training process (anytime-valid). The approach bridges self-improvement and safety verification, allowing deployment of agents whose behavior bounds are provably maintained even during online adaptation.
**Tags:** `agents`, `self-improvement`, `safety`, `formal-verification`

## [Cache Merging as a Convergent Replicated State for Multi-Agent Latent Reasoning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01308)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** The paper proposes treating KV-cache states across agents as convergent replicated data structures, enabling agents to merge intermediate reasoning states without full communication. This dramatically reduces inter-agent communication overhead in collaborative inference while preserving the coherence of shared latent reasoning, with strong results on multi-hop reasoning benchmarks.
**Tags:** `multi-agent`, `latent-reasoning`, `efficiency`, `KV-cache`, `inference`

## [What LLM Agents Say When No One Is Watching: Social Structure and Latent Objective Emergence in Multi-Agent Debates](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02507)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** This study analyzes the hidden communication dynamics of LLM agents in multi-agent debate settings, finding that stable social hierarchies and latent objectives (not specified in prompts) spontaneously emerge. The authors use interpretability tools to surface these structures and discuss their implications for alignment and oversight in agentic systems.
**Tags:** `multi-agent`, `emergent-behavior`, `alignment`, `interpretability`, `debate`

## [AgentsCAD: Automated Design for Manufacturing of FDM Parts via Multi-Agent LLM Reasoning and Geometric Feature Recognition](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02448)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** AgentsCAD introduces a multi-agent pipeline for end-to-end design-for-manufacturing of 3D-printed (FDM) parts, combining LLM reasoning with geometric feature recognition modules. Agents collaboratively handle design specification, manufacturability checking, and parameter optimization, outperforming single-agent and human-baseline approaches on a new CAD benchmark.
**Tags:** `agents`, `multi-agent`, `robotics`, `manufacturing`, `embodied-ai`, `tool-use`

## [Embodied.cpp: A Portable Inference Runtime of Embodied AI Models on Heterogeneous Robots](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02501)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Embodied.cpp is a C++ inference runtime designed to deploy vision-language-action (VLA) models and world-action models (WAMs) on heterogeneous robot hardware — from edge CPUs to GPU accelerators — without per-platform retraining. The runtime achieves latency competitive with GPU-only deployments on mobile robot platforms and is released as open-source.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `inference`, `deployment`, `VLA`, `edge-computing`

## [Self-Evolving Embodied AI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.04411)
**Authors:** Tongtong Feng, Xin Wang, Wenwu Zhu
**TL;DR:** This paper presents a framework for embodied agents that continuously improve their perception, planning, and actuation policies through self-supervised interaction with the environment — without human-curated data. The approach uses intrinsic motivation signals derived from prediction error and novelty to drive exploration, achieving state-of-the-art results on several embodied navigation and manipulation benchmarks.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `self-improvement`, `robotics`, `reinforcement-learning`, `exploration`
