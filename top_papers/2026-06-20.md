# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-06-20)

## [A Survey on Autonomy-Induced Security Risks in Large Model-Based Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.23844)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Comprehensively surveys security vulnerabilities that arise specifically from the autonomous capabilities of large model-based agents, covering threat vectors such as coordination attacks, privilege escalation via delegation, and cascade failures across multi-agent pipelines. Argues that agentic autonomy introduces a fundamentally new class of security risk beyond those of static LLMs, requiring dedicated defenses for tool-use and inter-agent communication surfaces.
**Tags:** `agents`, `security`, `safety`, `multi-agent`, `survey`

## [Survey of LLM Agent Communication with MCP](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.05364)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Surveys how the Model Context Protocol (MCP) enables structured communication between LLM agents and external tools or services, analyzing the software architecture implications of standardized agent-tool interfaces. Identifies key design patterns and open challenges in building scalable, interoperable multi-agent systems via protocol-based communication.
**Tags:** `agents`, `MCP`, `tool-use`, `multi-agent`, `survey`

## [World4omni: A Zero-Shot Framework from Image Generation World Model to Robotic Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.23919)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Proposes leveraging image-generation world models as zero-shot policy priors for robotic manipulation, enabling robots to plan and act without task-specific training data. Demonstrates that generative world models can bridge sim-to-real gaps by imagining future states and grounding action plans in visual predictions.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `manipulation`, `world-models`, `zero-shot`

---

## [Fast-in-Slow: A Dual-System Foundation Model Unifying Fast Manipulation within Slow Reasoning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.01953)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Introduces a dual-system architecture for robotic control where a fast reactive controller handles low-level manipulation while a slow deliberative reasoning module (backed by a large language/vision model) governs high-level planning. The tight integration achieves both real-time responsiveness and long-horizon semantic understanding, outperforming single-system baselines on complex manipulation benchmarks.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `manipulation`, `foundation-models`, `dual-system`

---

## [SmolVLA: A Small and Efficient Vision-Language-Action Model for Affordable and Efficient Robotics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.01844)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Presents SmolVLA, a compact vision-language-action model designed for resource-constrained robotic deployments, achieving competitive manipulation performance at a fraction of the parameter count of prior VLA models. Demonstrates that targeted architectural choices and data-efficient training can democratize capable robot policies beyond well-resourced research labs.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `VLA`, `efficiency`, `manipulation`

---

## [CEED-VLA: Consistency Vision-Language-Action Model with Early-Exit Decoding](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.13725)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Proposes an early-exit decoding strategy for vision-language-action models that allows the model to commit to an action token stream as soon as sufficient confidence is reached, reducing inference latency significantly. Maintains task success rates comparable to full-decoding baselines while enabling real-time control frequencies required for dexterous manipulation.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `VLA`, `robotics`, `efficient-inference`, `manipulation`

## [SwitchVLA: Execution-Aware Task Switching for Vision-Language-Action Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.03574)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Addresses the challenge of mid-execution task switching in VLA-controlled robots, where new instructions must be incorporated without catastrophic disruption to ongoing motor plans. Introduces an execution-aware gating mechanism that smoothly transitions between tasks by conditioning new action plans on the current execution state.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `VLA`, `robotics`, `task-switching`, `manipulation`

## [RIrc: Reinforcement Learning-Based Recovery for Compressed Vision-Language-Action Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.17639)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Studies performance degradation in quantized/pruned VLA models and proposes a reinforcement learning-based fine-tuning method to recover manipulation success rates after model compression. Shows that RL-based recovery substantially closes the gap between compressed and full-precision VLA models, enabling efficient deployment on edge robotic hardware.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `VLA`, `robotics`, `model-compression`, `reinforcement-learning`

## [The Evolution of Tool Use in LLM Agents: From Single-Tool Call to Multi-Tool Orchestration](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.22862)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Traces how tool-use in LLM agents has evolved from simple single-API calls to complex multi-tool orchestration across long trajectories, presenting a taxonomy of orchestration patterns and failure modes. Argues that the central challenge has shifted from invocation to coordination, and proposes evaluation criteria suited to multi-step tool chains.
**Tags:** `agents`, `tool-use`, `LLM`, `orchestration`, `survey`

## [AgentNoiseBench: Benchmarking Robustness of Tool-Using LLM Agents Under Noisy Conditions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.11348)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Introduces a benchmark specifically designed to evaluate how tool-using LLM agents perform when tool outputs, environmental observations, or user instructions contain realistic noise and perturbations. Reveals substantial fragility in current frontier agents under noisy conditions and proposes noise-aware training and prompting baselines.
**Tags:** `agents`, `tool-use`, `robustness`, `benchmark`, `LLM`

## [TodoEvolve: Learning to Architect Agent Planning Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.07839)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Proposes a meta-learning framework where an agent learns to design and refine its own planning architecture over time, adapting task decomposition strategies based on past successes and failures. Demonstrates that learned planning architectures outperform hand-designed decompositions on long-horizon reasoning tasks.
**Tags:** `agents`, `planning`, `meta-learning`, `LLM`, `autonomous`

## [Embodied AI in Action: Insights from SAE World Congress 2026 on Safety, Trust, and Real-World Deployment](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10653)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Synthesizes key findings from the SAE World Congress 2026 on deploying embodied AI systems—robots, autonomous vehicles, and manipulation arms—in safety-critical real-world settings. Highlights that trust, interpretability, and regulatory compliance are the primary bottlenecks to broad deployment, and outlines a research agenda to address them.
