# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-06-08)

## [CORAL: Towards Autonomous Multi-Agent Evolution for Open-Ended Discovery](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01658)
**Authors:** Ao Qu, Han Zheng, Zhou Zijian, Yan Yihao, Tang Yihong, Ong Shao Yong, Hong Fenglu, Zhou Kaichen, Jiang Chonghe, Kong Minwei, Zhu Jiacheng, Jiang Xuan, Li Sirui, Wu Cathy, Low Bryan Kian Hsiang, Zhao Jinhua, Liang Paul Pu
**TL;DR:** CORAL is the first framework for autonomous multi-agent evolution on open-ended problems, replacing rigid evolutionary control with long-running agents that explore, reflect, and collaborate through shared persistent memory and asynchronous execution. Agents use heartbeat-based interventions, isolated workspaces, and evaluator separation to remain safe and efficient. CORAL achieves 3–10× higher improvement rates with far fewer evaluations than fixed evolutionary-search baselines across 10 math, algorithmic, and systems tasks.
**Tags:** `agents`, `multi-agent`, `evolution`, `open-ended-discovery`, `LLM`

## [SocialGrid: A Benchmark for Planning and Social Reasoning in Embodied Multi-Agent Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16022)
**Authors:** Hikaru Shindo, Hanzhao Lin, Lukas Helff, Patrick Schramowski, Kristian Kersting
**TL;DR:** SocialGrid is an embodied multi-agent benchmark inspired by Among Us that evaluates LLM agents on planning, task execution, and social reasoning simultaneously. Even the strongest open model (GPT-OSS-120B) achieves below 60% task-completion accuracy, and agents fail to detect deception at near-random chance regardless of scale. The benchmark includes a Planning Oracle to isolate social reasoning bottlenecks from pure planning deficits.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `multi-agent`, `social-reasoning`, `benchmark`, `LLM`

## [Planner Matters! An Efficient and Unbalanced Multi-agent Collaboration Framework for Long-horizon Planning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02168)
**Authors:** Wenyi Wu, Sibo Zhu, Kun Zhou, Biwei Huang
**TL;DR:** This paper proposes a three-role multi-agent framework—planner, actor, and memory manager—and shows through systematic compute-allocation analysis that planning is the dominant factor driving task performance, while execution and memory management require far less capacity. Building on this insight, a planner-centric reinforcement learning approach is introduced that exclusively optimizes the planner via trajectory-level rewards from a VLM-as-judge. The framework significantly reduces total compute cost while improving long-horizon task success.
**Tags:** `agents`, `multi-agent`, `planning`, `reinforcement-learning`, `LLM`

## [SWE-Cycle: Benchmarking Code Agents across the Complete Issue Resolution Cycle](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13139)
**Authors:** Hao Guan, Lingyue Fu, Shao Zhang, Yaoming Zhu, Kangning Zhang, Lin Qiu, Xunliang Cai, Xuezhi Cao, Weiwen Liu, Weinan Zhang, Yong Yu
**TL;DR:** SWE-Cycle introduces a benchmark of 489 rigorously filtered instances that evaluates code agents across the full issue-resolution lifecycle: environment reconstruction, code implementation, and verification test generation—plus a combined FullCycle task. Unlike existing benchmarks that pre-configure environments and use static pipelines, SWE-Cycle surfaces real-world friction points for fully autonomous agents. Results reveal that current top agents still struggle significantly on end-to-end FullCycle scenarios.
**Tags:** `agents`, `code-generation`, `benchmark`, `software-engineering`, `autonomous-agents`

## [DyTopo: Dynamic Topology Routing for Multi-Agent Reasoning via Semantic Matching](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.06039)
**Authors:** Yuxing Lu, Jiuxin Cao, et al.
**TL;DR:** DyTopo replaces fixed communication topologies in multi-agent LLM systems with a manager-guided framework that reconstructs a sparse directed graph at each reasoning round via semantic matching of natural-language query/key descriptors. Only agents with relevant information exchange messages, preventing noise propagation across fixed edges. Across code generation and mathematical reasoning benchmarks with four LLM backbones, DyTopo outperforms the strongest baseline by an average of +6.2 points.
**Tags:** `agents`, `multi-agent`, `reasoning`, `communication`, `graph-routing`

## [Single-Agent LLMs Outperform Multi-Agent Systems on Multi-Hop Reasoning Under Equal Thinking Token Budgets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.02460)
**Authors:** Dat Tran, Douwe Kiela (Stanford University)
**TL;DR:** This paper argues—grounded in the Data Processing Inequality—that under a fixed reasoning-token budget and with perfect context utilization, single-agent systems are inherently more information-efficient than multi-agent systems for multi-hop reasoning. Controlled experiments across Qwen3, DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama, and Gemini 2.5 confirm that reported MAS gains often disappear when computation is normalized. The findings suggest that multi-agent overhead must be justified by tasks where parallelism or specialization genuinely adds value beyond token budget increases.
**Tags:** `agents`, `multi-agent`, `reasoning`, `benchmark`, `efficiency`

## [Externalization in LLM Agents: A Unified Review of Memory, Skills, Protocols and Harness Engineering](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08224)
**Authors:** Chenyu Zhou, Huacan Chai, Wenteng Chen, Zihan Guo, Rong Shan, Yuanyi Song, Tianyi Xu, Yingxuan Yang, Aofan Yu, Weiming Zhang, Congming Zheng, Jiachen Zhu, Zeyu Zheng, Zhuosheng Zhang, Xingyu Lou, Changwang Zhang, Zhihui Fu, Jun Wang, Weiwen Liu, Jianghao Lin, Weinan Zhang
**TL;DR:** This 54-page survey from Shanghai Jiao Tong University provides a unified taxonomy of how LLM agent capabilities are increasingly externalized at runtime rather than baked into model weights. Memory externalizes state across time, skills externalize procedural expertise, protocols externalize interaction structure, and harness engineering serves as the unification layer. The review maps the evolution path of cognitive externalization and identifies open challenges in making these modules reliable under production conditions.
**Tags:** `agents`, `memory`, `survey`, `harness-engineering`, `LLM`

## [AtomVLA: Scalable Post-Training for Robotic Manipulation via Predictive Latent World Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.08519)
**Authors:** Xiaoquan Sun, Zetian Xu, Chen Cao, Zonghe Liu, Yihan Sun, Jingrui Pang, Ruijian Zhang, Zhen Yang, Kang Pang, Dingxin He, Mingqi Yuan, Jiayu Chen
**TL;DR:** AtomVLA addresses a critical "instruction grounding gap" in Vision-Language-Action models, where coarse high-level task instructions during SFT lead to compounding errors in long-horizon manipulation. The framework uses a large language model to decompose demonstrations into fine-grained atomic subtasks, then trains a predictive latent world model to provide intermediate guidance at each step. Evaluated on standard manipulation benchmarks, AtomVLA achieves significantly improved performance on multi-step tasks compared to VLA baselines.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `VLA`, `manipulation`, `world-models`

## [Embodied AI in Action: Insights from SAE World Congress 2026 on Safety, Trust, Robotics, and Real-World Deployment](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10653)
**Authors:** Jan-Mou Li et al.
**TL;DR:** This white paper summarizes key insights from the SAE World Congress 2026 panel on embodied AI, documenting the rapid transition from research to real-world systems including autonomous vehicles, mobile robots, and industrial machines. Major themes include trust calibration between humans and autonomous systems, safety certification for dynamic environments, and the engineering challenges of deploying foundation models on physical hardware. The paper outlines a research roadmap for bridging the gap between lab demonstrations and reliable field deployment.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `safety`, `autonomous-vehicles`, `deployment`

## [EmbodiedGovBench: A Benchmark for Governance, Recovery, and Upgrade Safety in Embodied Agent Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11174)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2604.11174)
**TL;DR:** EmbodiedGovBench introduces a governance-focused benchmark for physical AI agents, specifically targeting three underexplored axes: graceful recovery from unexpected failures, safe in-field model upgrades, and policy-level governance interventions. The benchmark simulates household and industrial robot scenarios where agents must recognize when to defer to human oversight versus act autonomously. Findings reveal that current embodied agents are significantly overconfident in self-recovery capability and underperform on safe upgrade transitions.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `safety`, `governance`, `benchmark`

## [FM-Planner: Foundation Model Guided Path Planning for Autonomous Drone Navigation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.20783)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2505.20783)
**TL;DR:** FM-Planner integrates foundation models into UAV path planning, enabling drones to interpret natural language mission descriptions and reason about obstacle-rich environments without task-specific training. The system uses a hierarchical architecture where the foundation model generates high-level waypoint plans that are refined by a local reactive controller. Experiments in cluttered indoor and outdoor simulation environments show substantial improvements in mission completion rate over classical path planners.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `drones`, `navigation`, `foundation-models`
