# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-06-22)

## [Embodied AI Agents: Modeling the World](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.22355)
**Authors:** Meta AI Research Team
**TL;DR:** This Meta AI Research paper presents a comprehensive framework for AI agents embodied in visual, virtual, or physical forms — including virtual avatars, wearable devices, and robots — designed to perceive, learn, and act within their surroundings. It emphasizes world modeling as a foundational capability for embodied agents, arguing that agents must build internal representations of their environment to plan and act effectively. The paper surveys progress across robot manipulation, navigation, and social interaction with embodied systems.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `world-models`, `robotics`, `agents`, `perception`

## [Embodied AI in Action: Insights from SAE World Congress 2026 on Safety, Trust, Robotics, and Real-World Deployment](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10653)
**Authors:** SAE World Congress 2026 Panel Contributors
**TL;DR:** This white paper synthesizes insights from the SAE World Congress 2026 panel session on embodied AI deployment in autonomous vehicles, mobile robots, and industrial machines. It argues that embodied AI must be treated as a systems-level engineering challenge requiring lifecycle governance, human-centered design, and evolving safety standards. Key themes include trust calibration, fail-safe design, and the gap between controlled benchmarks and real-world operational reliability.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `safety`, `autonomous-vehicles`, `robotics`, `deployment`

## [SmoLVLA: A Vision-Language-Action Model for Affordable and Efficient Robotics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.01844)
**Authors:** Hugging Face Robotics Team
**TL;DR:** SmoLVLA introduces a compact yet highly capable vision-language-action model targeting affordable robotic platforms that cannot run large frontier models on-device. By distilling from larger VLA models and applying aggressive quantization, SmoLVLA achieves competitive task success rates on standard manipulation benchmarks while running at low latency on consumer-grade hardware. This work lowers the barrier for robotics researchers who lack access to large compute clusters.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `vision-language-action`, `efficiency`, `robot-manipulation`

## [RationalVLA: A Rational Vision-Language-Action Model with Dual System](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.10826)
**Authors:** arXiv preprint contributors
**TL;DR:** RationalVLA proposes a dual-system architecture for robot control that combines a fast reactive controller (System 1) with a slower deliberative reasoning module (System 2), inspired by dual-process cognitive theory. The model uses a vision-language backbone for high-level task understanding and a lightweight policy network for low-level motor control, with the two systems arbitrating dynamically during execution. Experiments on real-robot manipulation tasks show improved performance on long-horizon, multi-step tasks compared to single-system baselines.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `vision-language-action`, `dual-system`, `robot-manipulation`, `reasoning`

## [CyberGym: A Benchmark for Evaluating AI Agents on Real-World Security Vulnerabilities](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.02548)
**Authors:** UC Berkeley Security Research Group
**TL;DR:** CyberGym is a cybersecurity benchmark that evaluates AI agents on their ability to discover real memory-safety bugs in real C/C++ open-source code from Google's OSS-Fuzz corpus, spanning 1,507 task instances across 188 projects. Unlike synthetic CTF challenges, CyberGym tasks require agents to reason about complex codebases with minimal scaffolding, making it a robust test of autonomous code analysis. Frontier models show strong performance on straightforward bugs but struggle with cross-function vulnerabilities requiring multi-hop reasoning.
**Tags:** `agents`, `security`, `benchmarks`, `code-agents`, `evaluation`

## [OS-Harm / τ2-Bench: Measuring Safety of Computer Use Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.14866)
**Authors:** Sierra Research Team
**TL;DR:** This paper introduces OS-Harm, a benchmark for quantifying safety risks posed by autonomous computer-use agents performing real desktop tasks, and τ2-Bench, a dual-control conversational agent evaluation framework. OS-Harm covers scenarios where agents might inadvertently or intentionally cause harm through file system operations, network actions, or process management. The work provides a structured taxonomy of computer-use harms and a corresponding evaluation suite to guide safe deployment of agentic systems.
**Tags:** `agents`, `safety`, `computer-use`, `benchmarks`, `evaluation`

## [TrajAD: Trajectory Anomaly Detection for Trustworthy LLM Agents](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2506.01438)
**Authors:** arXiv preprint contributors
**TL;DR:** TrajAD proposes a runtime verifier that monitors LLM agent execution trajectories and detects errors or unsafe action sequences before they cause downstream harm. By modeling expected agent behavior as a sequence distribution, TrajAD can localize the exact step in a trajectory where the agent deviates from correct behavior. Evaluations across web-navigation and API-call agents show high detection rates with minimal false positives, making it practical for production agentic systems.
**Tags:** `agents`, `safety`, `monitoring`, `trajectory`, `trustworthy-ai`

## [ProvenanceGuard: Source-Aware Factuality Verification for MCP-Based LLM Agents](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2506.01438)
**Authors:** IEEE/RSJ IROS 2026 Contributors
**TL;DR:** ProvenanceGuard addresses factual hallucination in LLM agents that retrieve information through Model Context Protocol (MCP) tool calls by tracking provenance of each retrieved fact and verifying claims against their original sources. The system intercepts agent tool outputs and attaches source metadata, which a lightweight verifier then uses to flag responses that cannot be grounded. Accepted at IROS 2026, this work is among the first to tackle hallucination specifically in the context of agentic tool-augmented generation.
**Tags:** `agents`, `mcp`, `factuality`, `hallucination`, `tool-use`

## [Agentic Tool Use in Large Language Models](https://arxiv.org/html/2604.00835v1)
**Authors:** arXiv preprint contributors
**TL;DR:** This survey comprehensively reviews how large language models are trained and prompted to invoke external tools, APIs, and retrieval systems as part of multi-step agentic pipelines. It covers neural long-term memory modules, role-aware reasoning for persona-consistent agents, and emerging patterns for composing tool-use with planning. The authors identify key gaps in current evaluation methodology and propose a taxonomy for classifying tool-use settings by autonomy level and tool complexity.
**Tags:** `agents`, `tool-use`, `llm`, `survey`, `planning`

## [Anticipatory Planning for Multimodal AI Agents](https://arxiv.org/html/2603.16777v1)
**Authors:** arXiv preprint contributors
**TL;DR:** This paper introduces anticipatory planning, a framework that allows multimodal agents to reason forward in time about likely future states before committing to an action, reducing costly backtracking in long-horizon tasks. The approach augments standard chain-of-thought planning with a learned world model that predicts the consequences of candidate action sequences across vision and language modalities. On benchmark environments including web navigation and embodied instruction following, anticipatory agents achieve higher task completion rates with fewer total steps.
**Tags:** `agents`, `planning`, `multimodal`, `long-horizon`, `world-models`

## [Efficient Benchmarking of AI Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23749)
**Authors:** arXiv preprint contributors
**TL;DR:** Current AI agent benchmarks are expensive to run and often require thousands of LLM API calls per evaluation, making iterative research cycles slow and costly. This paper proposes a suite of techniques for efficient benchmark evaluation — including early stopping, stratified task sampling, and surrogate model scoring — that reduces evaluation cost by up to 70% while preserving ranking fidelity. The authors validate their approach across five popular agent benchmarks and release tooling to help the community adopt efficient evaluation practices.
**Tags:** `agents`, `benchmarks`, `evaluation`, `efficiency`, `methodology`
