## [Agents All the Way Down: A Methodology for Building Custom AI Agents from Substrate to Production](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11869)
**Authors:** Marc Alier Forment et al.
**TL;DR:** Presents a bottom-up methodology for constructing custom AI agents that live within their own application boundary, manage their own data and tools, enforce security perimeters, and carry auditable brand identities. The framework spans from substrate-level infrastructure choices up to production deployment, addressing gaps left by general-purpose agent platforms. Particular emphasis is placed on security, observability, and maintainability across the full agent lifecycle.

## [Harnessing the Collective Intelligence of AI Agents in the Wild for New Discoveries](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10402)
**Authors:** (EinsteinArena team)
**TL;DR:** Introduces EinsteinArena, an agent-native open platform for distributed scientific discovery that provides agents with live open problems, automated verifiers, public leaderboards, and problem-specific discussion forums. As of May 2026, agents on EinsteinArena have discovered 12 new state-of-the-art results that outperform all prior human and AI solutions. The work demonstrates that structured competitive environments can harness collective agent intelligence for accelerating scientific progress.

## [SABER: Benchmarking Operational Safety of LLM Coding Agents in Stateful Project Workspaces](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01317)
**Authors:** (SABER team)
**TL;DR:** Places LLM coding agents in realistic, stateful project environments and evaluates safety based on the final environment state after a sequence of agentic actions rather than per-turn outputs. Evaluations reveal that even the best-performing model exhibits a harmful safety-violation rate above 54%, indicating that current alignment techniques are insufficient for realistic project-scale deployments. The benchmark is designed to be extensible to new agent frameworks and model families.
**Tags:** `agents`, `safety`, `benchmarks`, `coding-agents`, `alignment`

## [Agents' Last Exam (ALE)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05405)
**Authors:** (ALE Consortium â€” 250+ industry experts)
**TL;DR:** ALE is a rigorous benchmark for evaluating AI agents on long-horizon, economically valuable, real-world tasks with verifiable outcomes, co-developed with over 250 domain experts spanning 13 industry clusters and more than 1,000 tasks. The design prioritizes tasks where success or failure can be objectively verified, avoiding the ambiguity of human-judge-dependent evaluations. Results reveal a significant capability gap between current frontier agents and expert human performance on high-value professional tasks.

## [Agent Skill Evaluation and Evolution: Frameworks and Benchmarks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11435)
**Authors:** (Survey team)
**TL;DR:** Systematically examines how AI agents acquire and refine skills over time, categorizing skill evolution into four paradigms: execution feedback, trajectory distillation, compression, and reinforcement learning. Provides a unified framework for comparing evaluation methodologies and identifies key gaps, particularly in cross-task generalization and continual learning settings. Includes a meta-benchmark comparing existing evaluation suites across dimensions of coverage, cost, and reliability.
**Tags:** `agents`, `evaluation`, `skill-learning`, `reinforcement-learning`, `survey`

## [AffordanceVLA: A Vision-Language-Action Model Empowering Action Generation through Affordance-Aware Understanding](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06155)
**Authors:** (AffordanceVLA team)
**TL;DR:** Proposes a VLA model that explicitly grounds robot action generation in affordance-aware visual understanding, enabling the model to reason about where and how objects can be interacted with before committing to an action sequence. Leverages pretrained vision-language models and fine-tunes them with affordance annotations to improve generalization to novel objects and scenes in manipulation tasks. Demonstrated strong performance on tabletop manipulation benchmarks without requiring dense per-step demonstrations.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `vla`, `manipulation`, `affordance`, `vision-language`

## [TempoVLA: Learning Speed-Controllable Vision-Language-Action Policies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06491)
**Authors:** (TempoVLA team)
**TL;DR:** Introduces TempoVLA, a single VLA model whose execution speed is conditioned on an explicit temporal control signal, allowing the robot to dynamically adapt motion speed based on task context (e.g., slow for precise grasps, fast for transport). Training combines standard behavior cloning with a speed-conditioning objective derived from demonstration timing annotations. Achieves better task success rates than fixed-speed baselines while reducing collision rates in dynamic environments.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `vla`, `manipulation`, `motion-control`

## [Robots Need More than VLA and World Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06556)
**Authors:** (Critical review team)
**TL;DR:** Offers a critical perspective on the prevailing assumption that combining Vision-Language-Action models with world models is sufficient for general-purpose robot autonomy, arguing that key missing ingredients include reliable grounding, uncertainty quantification, and principled failure recovery. The paper identifies specific failure modes observed when VLA+world-model pipelines encounter distribution shift, sensor noise, and long-horizon dependency. Proposes a research agenda targeting the gaps between current capabilities and truly robust embodied AI systems.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `vla`, `world-models`, `critical-analysis`

## [What Matters in Orchestrating Robot Policies: A Systematic Study of Hierarchical VLA Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10267)
**Authors:** (Hierarchical VLA team)
**TL;DR:** Conducts a systematic empirical study of hierarchical robot policy systems in which a high-level VLM planner decomposes instructions into sub-goals that are passed to low-level VLA executors. The study isolates the contribution of planner model size, sub-goal granularity, replanning frequency, and executor fine-tuning strategy across a diverse set of manipulation and navigation tasks. Finds that replanning frequency and sub-goal granularity interact strongly, with optimal settings being highly task-dependent.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `vla`, `hierarchical-planning`, `multi-agent`
