## [HAS-Bench: Evaluating LLM-Based Human-Agent Systems under Configurable Human Participation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04329)
**Authors:** Yaozu Wu, Wei-Chieh Huang, Jizhou Guo, Dongyuan Li, Renhe Jiang, Henry Peng Zou, Chunyu Miao, Shanghao Li, Weizhi Zhang, WeiWei Ye, Yankai Chen, Meng Zhang, Xue Liu, Philip S. Yu
**TL;DR:** Introduces HAS-Framework, a graph-based framework treating humans and LLM-powered agents as first-class participants in collaborative task-solving. HAS-Bench evaluates Human-Agent Systems across configurable agency levels, interaction channels, and persona policies, measuring task outcomes and process-level behavior (clarification quality, feedback utilization, safety, initiative, and interaction cost). Experiments across six domains show human participation substantially improves task completion and failure recovery.
**Tags:** `agents`, `human-agent-interaction`, `benchmark`, `evaluation`, `llm`

## [EmbodiedGen V2: An Agentic, Simulation-Ready 3D World Engine for Embodied AI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07459)
**Authors:** Xinjie Wang et al. (12 authors)
**TL;DR:** A generative 3D world engine for building executable, simulation-ready environments for embodied intelligence, bridging the gap in policy-ready task environments via a unified sim-ready representation connecting cross-simulator assets, interaction affordances, task-driven worlds, and large-scale multi-room scenes. Online reinforcement learning with generated environments improves simulation task success from 9.7% to 79.8%, and real-robot transfer improves from 21.7% to 75.0%. A major advance for scalable embodied AI training.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `simulation`, `3d-worlds`, `reinforcement-learning`, `robotics`

## [Embodied.cpp: A Portable Inference Runtime of Embodied AI Models on Heterogeneous Robots](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02501)
**Authors:** Ling Xu, Chuyu Han, Borui Li, Hao Wu, Shiqi Jiang, Ting Cao, Chuanyou Li, Sheng Zhong, Shuai Wang
**TL;DR:** Addresses fragmented deployment of embodied AI models (VLAs and World-Action Models) across heterogeneous edge devices with a portable C++ inference runtime organized into five execution layers: input adapters, sequence builders, backbone execution, head plugins, and deployment adapters. Targets latency-first batch-1 inference inside closed-loop robot control. Opens a path to standardized, hardware-agnostic deployment of embodied AI at the edge.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `inference`, `robotics`, `edge-computing`, `vla`, `runtime`

## [Cache Merging as a Convergent Replicated State for Multi-Agent Latent Reasoning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01367)
**Authors:** Carlos Baquero, Luís Brito
**TL;DR:** Proposes using cache merging as a convergent replicated data structure to enable efficient multi-agent latent reasoning, drawing on distributed systems concepts (CRDTs) to coordinate agent inference without explicit communication overhead. The approach allows multiple agents to independently compute while converging to consistent shared reasoning states. A novel intersection of distributed systems theory and multi-agent AI.
**Tags:** `agents`, `multi-agent`, `latent-reasoning`, `distributed-systems`, `efficiency`

## [TimeLens2: Generalist Video Temporal Grounding with Multimodal LLMs](https://huggingface.co/papers)
**Authors:** (Authors pending full indexing)
**TL;DR:** Tackles the gap that video MLLMs can describe what happens in video but rarely identify *when* — introduces a generalist video temporal grounding framework where one model predicts variable-cardinality evidence intervals across diverse video lengths, domains, query forms, and viewpoints. TimeLens2-2B outperforms all size-matched baselines on every benchmark; 4B and 8B variants surpass open-source models with up to 397B parameters. Enables embodied AI agents to ground actions and events in time.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `video-understanding`, `temporal-grounding`, `multimodal`, `llm`
