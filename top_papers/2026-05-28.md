## [AI Research Agents Narrow Scientific Exploration](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.27905)
**Authors:** Yixuan Tang, et al.
**TL;DR:** Using four AI research-agent frameworks and six LLMs, the authors generated 37,802 scientific ideas from shared seed literature across AI/ML research areas. Comparing AI-generated ideas against human-authored papers reveals that AI agents tend to concentrate exploration around existing work rather than expanding the frontier, raising concerns about homogenization of AI-assisted science.
**Tags:** `agents`, `scientific-discovery`, `multi-agent`, `LLM`, `evaluation`

## [Agentic CLEAR: Automating Multi-Level Evaluation of LLM Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22608)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** Agentic CLEAR is an automatic, dynamic evaluation framework that produces textual insights into agent behavior at three levels of granularity: system, trace, and node. It enables fine-grained diagnosis of where and why LLM agents fail, going beyond aggregate pass/fail metrics to identify systematic behavioral patterns.
**Tags:** `agents`, `evaluation`, `LLM`, `benchmarking`, `multi-agent`

## [MARLIN: Multi-Agent Game-Theoretic Reinforcement Learning for Sustainable LLM Inference in Cloud Datacenters](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13496)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** MARLIN introduces a multi-agent game-theoretic RL framework to co-optimize time-to-first-token latency, carbon emissions, water usage, and energy costs associated with LLM inference at scale. The approach models competing datacenter operators as strategic agents and finds Nash equilibria that balance performance against environmental sustainability targets.
**Tags:** `agents`, `reinforcement-learning`, `LLM-inference`, `green-AI`, `cloud`

## [MIND-Skill: Quality-Guaranteed Skill Generation via Multi-Agent Induction and Deduction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.08670)
**Authors:** (Multiple authors)
**TL;DR:** MIND-Skill tackles how LLM-powered agents struggle with complex, multi-step real-world tasks by using a multi-agent induction-deduction loop to automatically generate and verify reusable skill libraries. Quality guarantees are enforced through formal deductive checks, significantly reducing the failure rate of skill-based agent execution on long-horizon tasks.
