# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-06-02)

## [Agentic Plan Caching: Test-Time Memory for Fast and Cost-Efficient LLM Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.14852)
**Authors:** et al.
**TL;DR:** Proposes a test-time memory mechanism that extracts, stores, adapts, and reuses structured plan templates from prior agent runs, significantly cutting the planning latency and API cost of LLM-based agents without sacrificing task performance. The approach is orthogonal to model improvements and can be layered on top of any planning framework.
**Tags:** `agents`, `planning`, `efficiency`, `llm`, `test-time-compute`

## [Levels of Autonomy for AI Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.12469)
**Authors:** et al.
**TL;DR:** Introduces a five-level taxonomy of AI agent autonomy—operator, collaborator, consultant, approver, and observer—that characterizes the role a human takes when interacting with an agent. The framework provides a principled vocabulary for discussing safety, oversight, and deployment readiness of agentic systems.
**Tags:** `agents`, `autonomy`, `safety`, `human-ai-interaction`, `taxonomy`

---

## [Efficient LLM Collaboration via Planning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.11578)
**Authors:** et al.
**TL;DR:** A lightweight planner model generates a structured plan that guides a larger executor model, enabling small and large models to take turns as planner and executor in a multi-stage cascade. The resulting framework matches the performance of large proprietary models while drastically reducing inference API cost.
**Tags:** `agents`, `planning`, `llm`, `efficiency`, `multi-agent`

---

## [Autonomous Computer Vision Development with Agentic AI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.11140)
**Authors:** et al.
**TL;DR:** Demonstrates that a specialized computer vision pipeline—including data preparation, model selection, training, and evaluation—can be built end-to-end from a natural language prompt using agentic AI methods. Closes the loop between requirement specification and deployable vision models without human-in-the-loop intervention.
**Tags:** `agents`, `computer-vision`, `agentic-ai`, `automation`, `code-generation`

## [Foundation Models in Autonomous Driving: A Survey on Scenario Generation and Scenario Analysis](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.11526)
**Authors:** et al.
**TL;DR:** Comprehensive survey of how foundation models are being used to generate and analyze diverse driving scenarios for testing and validating autonomous vehicles. Covers simulation-based testing, rare-event synthesis, and the emerging use of generative models to stress-test autonomous driving stacks.
**Tags:** `autonomous-driving`, `embodied-ai`, `foundation-models`, `scenario-generation`, `safety`

## [Improving LLM Agent Planning with In-Context Learning via Atomic Fact Augmentation and Lookahead Search](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.09171)
**Authors:** et al.
**TL;DR:** Introduces an LLM agent architecture that extracts and stores "atomic facts" from prior interactions, then uses lookahead search at planning time to select context that improves action accuracy. Achieves consistent gains on multi-step planning benchmarks without fine-tuning.
**Tags:** `agents`, `planning`, `in-context-learning`, `lookahead-search`, `llm`

## [Agentomics-ML: Autonomous Machine Learning Experimentation Agent for Genomic and Transcriptomic Data](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.05542)
**Authors:** et al.
**TL;DR:** Presents a fully autonomous agentic system that takes a genomics or transcriptomics dataset and a natural language objective, then designs, trains, and evaluates a classification model—outputting reproducible code and results. Demonstrates that agentic ML pipelines can accelerate bioinformatics research with minimal human supervision.
**Tags:** `agents`, `bioinformatics`, `genomics`, `autonomous-ml`, `science-agents`

## [STRATUS: A Multi-agent System for Autonomous Reliability Engineering of Modern Clouds](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.02009)
**Authors:** et al.
**TL;DR:** STRATUS deploys a team of specialized LLM agents—detector, diagnostician, remediator, and auditor—to perform site-reliability engineering tasks on cloud infrastructure autonomously. Evaluated on real cloud failure scenarios, it matches the incident-resolution rate of experienced SRE teams at a fraction of the response time.
**Tags:** `multi-agent`, `cloud-computing`, `reliability-engineering`, `sre`, `agents`

---

## [Embodied AI Agents: Modeling the World](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.22355)
**Authors:** Meta AI Research (et al.)
**TL;DR:** Meta AI explores how AI agents embodied in virtual avatars, wearable devices, and physical robots can build and exploit world models for perception and action. The paper argues that world-model learning—rather than purely reactive policies—is the key architectural ingredient for robust embodied intelligence.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `world-models`, `agents`, `robotics`, `perception`

## [Future of Work with AI Agents: Auditing Automation and Augmentation Potential across the U.S. Workforce](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.06576)
**Authors:** et al.
**TL;DR:** First large-scale empirical audit of both worker desire for and AI capability to automate or augment occupational tasks, covering thousands of job categories. Finds that augmentation—AI assisting rather than replacing humans—dominates in most knowledge-work roles, with full automation concentrated in structured, repetitive tasks.
**Tags:** `agents`, `future-of-work`, `automation`, `augmentation`, `labor-economics`

---

## [World Model for Robot Learning: A Comprehensive Survey](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00080)
**Authors:** et al.
**TL;DR:** Surveys the use of world models—learned predictive representations of environment dynamics—across robot learning sub-fields including policy learning, planning, simulation, data generation, and evaluation. Highlights how foundation models and large-scale video generation are accelerating world-model quality and generalization for robotic systems.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `world-models`, `robotics`, `survey`, `foundation-models`
