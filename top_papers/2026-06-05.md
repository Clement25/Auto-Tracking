# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-06-05)

## [The Meta-Agent Challenge: Are Current Agents Capable of Autonomous Agent Development?](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04455)
**Authors:** Xinyu Lu et al. (Chinese Information Processing Laboratory, Ant Group)
**TL;DR:** Introduces MAC, a framework testing whether frontier models can autonomously develop agent systems — not just execute tasks in human-designed workflows. Meta-agents are evaluated in a sandboxed environment with an API and time budget; results show they rarely match human-engineered baselines, with the few successes dominated by proprietary frontier models.
**Tags:** `agents`, `meta-agent`, `evaluation`, `autonomous-development`, `benchmarks`

## [Cosmos 3: Omnimodal World Models for Physical AI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02800)
**Authors:** NVIDIA Research (Cosmos Lab)
**TL;DR:** NVIDIA releases Cosmos 3, an open family of omnimodal world models based on a two-tower Mixture-of-Transformers architecture that jointly processes and generates language, image, video, audio, and action sequences. Trained on 20 trillion tokens across diverse modalities, it unifies VLMs, video generators, and action models into one framework and achieves new SOTA across understanding and generation benchmarks. Available in Nano (16B) and Super (64B) variants.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `world-models`, `robotics`, `autonomous-vehicles`, `physical-ai`, `multimodal`

## [Embodied AI in Action: Insights from SAE World Congress 2026 on Safety, Trust, Robotics, and Real-World Deployment](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10653)
**Authors:** Jan-Mou Li, Paul Schmitt, Wei Tong, Majed Mohammed, Akshay Chalana, Arpan Kusari, Edward Griffor
**TL;DR:** A white paper synthesizing key takeaways from the SAE World Congress 2026 expert panel on embodied AI as it transitions from research to real-world deployment in autonomous vehicles, mobile robots, and industrial machines. The paper argues embodied AI must be treated as a systems engineering challenge requiring lifecycle governance, human-centered design, and evolving safety standards — not just model performance.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `safety`, `robotics`, `autonomous-vehicles`, `governance`, `real-world-deployment`

## [World Model for Robot Learning: A Comprehensive Survey](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00080)
**Authors:** 18 authors from Nanyang Technological University, UC Berkeley, Stanford, University of Tokyo, University of Oxford, and others
**TL;DR:** A comprehensive survey examining world models — predictive representations of how environments evolve under actions — as central components of modern robot learning. Covers how world models serve as learned simulators for RL and evaluation, how robotic video world models have evolved from imagination-based to foundation-scale formulations, and connections to navigation and autonomous driving.
**Tags:** `robotics`, `world-models`, `reinforcement-learning`, `embodied-ai`, `survey`, `robot-learning`

## [Memanto: Typed Semantic Memory with Information-Theoretic Retrieval for Long-Horizon Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22085)
**Authors:** Seyed Moein Abtahi et al.
**TL;DR:** Proposes Memanto, a universal memory layer for agentic AI that challenges the assumption that knowledge graph complexity is needed for high-fidelity agent memory. Using a typed semantic schema, built-in conflict resolution, and an information-theoretic retrieval engine (Moorcheh), it achieves 89.8% on LongMemEval and 87.1% on LoCoMo — SOTA among vector-only systems — with zero ingestion cost, single-query retrieval, and sub-90ms latency.
**Tags:** `agents`, `memory`, `long-horizon`, `retrieval`, `agentic-ai`

## [Agentic Tool Use in Large Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.00835)
**Authors:** (Multiple)
**TL;DR:** A structured survey of how LLMs perform tool use in agentic settings. Traces the evolution from the ReAct Thought-Action-Observation loop through modern frameworks, covering how models decide when and how to invoke external tools, update steps based on observations, and compose multi-step tool chains for complex tasks.
**Tags:** `agents`, `tool-use`, `LLM`, `ReAct`, `reasoning`

## [Agentic Reasoning for Large Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.12538)
**Authors:** (Multiple)
**TL;DR:** Characterizes agentic reasoning along three axes — foundational (planning, tool use, search), self-evolving (feedback loops, memory, adaptation), and collective (multi-agent coordination and knowledge sharing). Provides a unified taxonomy for the rapidly expanding literature on LLM agents that plan, act, and learn in dynamic environments.
**Tags:** `agents`, `reasoning`, `planning`, `multi-agent`, `LLM`, `survey`

## [Towards Embodied Agentic AI: Review and Classification of LLM- and VLM-Driven Robot Autonomy and Interaction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.05294)
**Authors:** (Multiple)
**TL;DR:** Reviews and classifies the emerging field of agentic AI in robotics, where large language and vision-language models serve as intelligent intermediaries between users and robotic systems. Proposes a structured classification covering autonomy levels, interaction modalities, and task domains, identifying key open challenges in grounding, safety, and real-world generalization.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `LLM`, `VLM`, `agentic-AI`, `survey`

## [AI Agent Systems: Architectures, Applications, and Evaluation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.01743)
**Authors:** (Multiple)
**TL;DR:** A survey synthesizing emerging agent architectures across deliberation and reasoning (chain-of-thought decomposition, self-reflection, constraint-aware decision making), planning and control (reactive policies to hierarchical planners), and tool calling and environment interaction (retrieval, code execution, APIs, multimodal perception). Includes evaluation frameworks and discusses open research challenges.
**Tags:** `agents`, `architectures`, `evaluation`, `planning`, `tool-use`, `survey`

---

## [A Survey of Robotic Navigation and Manipulation with Physics Simulators in the Era of Embodied AI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.01458)
**Authors:** (Multiple)
**TL;DR:** Systematically reviews how physics simulators are used for training and evaluating embodied agents on navigation and manipulation tasks, covering PointNav, ObjectNav, VLN, and complex manipulation benchmarks. Highlights the sim-to-real gap and how recent foundation-model-based approaches are narrowing it.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `robotics`, `navigation`, `manipulation`, `simulation`, `survey`
