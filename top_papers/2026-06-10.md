# Top Papers — AI Agents & Embodied AI (2026-06-10)

## [The Cost of Dynamic Reasoning: Demystifying AI Agents and Test-Time Scaling from an AI Infrastructure Perspective](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.04301)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2506.04301 — authors not retrieved)
**TL;DR:** Presents the first comprehensive system-level analysis of LLM-based AI agents that employ dynamic, multi-step reasoning coordinated with external tools. Quantifies resource usage, latency, energy consumption, and datacenter-wide power demands across diverse agent designs and test-time scaling strategies. Key finding: agents improve accuracy with more compute but suffer rapidly diminishing returns, widening latency variance, and unsustainable infrastructure costs.
**Tags:** `agents`, `test-time-scaling`, `infrastructure`, `llm`, `systems`

## [A Survey of LLM-Driven AI Agent Communication: Protocols, Security Risks, and Defense Countermeasures](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.19676)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2506.19676)
**TL;DR:** Surveys how LLM-driven AI agents communicate across protocols including Anthropic's Model Context Protocol (MCP) and Google's Agent-to-Agent (A2A) standard. Analyzes security risks unique to agentic communication pipelines and proposes defense countermeasures. Timely given the rapid industry-wide adoption of MCP as an interoperability standard.
**Tags:** `agents`, `mcp`, `security`, `communication-protocols`, `multi-agent`

## [Survey of LLM Agent Communication with MCP: A Software Design Pattern Centric Review](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.05364)

## [Octonav: Towards Generalist Embodied Navigation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.09839)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2506.09839)
**TL;DR:** Proposes Octonav, a generalist navigation framework for embodied agents that handles diverse indoor and outdoor environments without task-specific fine-tuning. Leverages vision-language models for semantic understanding of navigation goals expressed in natural language, achieving strong zero-shot transfer across benchmarks.

## [Rapid Hand: A Robust, Affordable, Perception-Integrated, Dexterous Manipulation Platform for Generalist Robot Autonomy](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.07490)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2506.07490)
**TL;DR:** Introduces a low-cost yet high-capability dexterous robotic hand that integrates tactile and visual perception directly into the hardware design. ## [Robot Learning from Human Videos: A Survey](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27621)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2604.27621)

## [Per-Group Error, Not Total MSE: Fine-Tuning Vision-Language-Action Models for 11-DoF Mobile Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/search/?searchtype=all&query=per-group+error+VLA+mobile+manipulation)
**Authors:** (ICRA 2026 Workshop — VLA Pipelines for Real Robots)
**TL;DR:** Challenges the standard mean-squared-error objective for fine-tuning Vision-Language-Action models by showing that per-group error metrics better capture failure modes in high-DoF mobile manipulation tasks. Accepted at the ICRA 2026 Workshop "From Data to Decisions: VLA Pipelines for Real Robots," with demonstrated gains on 11-DoF loco-manipulation benchmarks.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `vla`, `mobile-manipulation`, `fine-tuning`, `robotics`

## [Externalization in LLM Agents: A Unified Review of Memory, Skills, Protocols and Harness Engineering](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08224)
**Authors:** (arXiv:2604.08224)
**TL;DR:** Frames LLM agent design around the concept of "externalization" — offloading cognitive functions (memory, skills, communication) into structured external systems rather than relying on in-context representation alone.
