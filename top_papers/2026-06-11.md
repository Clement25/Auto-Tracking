**Authors:** (Authors not fully indexed yet — submitted June 10, 2026)

## [Silent Failure in LLM Agent Systems: The Entropy Principle and the Inevitable Disorder of Autonomous Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.08162)
**Authors:** (Authors not fully indexed — submitted June 6, 2026)
**TL;DR:** Analyzes how LLM agent systems suffer from "silent failures" that occur without external triggers, drawing on over 40,000 controlled trials and 100,000+ agent interactions. Introduces the Entropy Principle — the tendency of autonomous agent state to degrade silently over time — and proposes detection and mitigation strategies.
**Tags:** `agents`, `reliability`, `failure-analysis`, `autonomous-systems`, `LLM`

## [Agentic Software: How AI Agents Are Restructuring the Software Paradigm](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05608)
**Authors:** (Submitted June 4, 2026; revised June 10, 2026)
**TL;DR:** Argues that AI agents represent a fundamental restructuring of software engineering, where LLMs serve as the primary reasoning engine and decision logic is generated at runtime rather than hardcoded. Surveys the shift to "agentic engineering" as the dominant paradigm while noting fully autonomous software engineering remains a multi-year research challenge.
**Tags:** `agents`, `agentic-software`, `software-engineering`, `LLM`, `survey`

## [Declarative Skills for AI Agents in Knowledge-Grounded Tool-Use Workflows](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06923)
**Authors:** M. Danish Lim, I. Danial Bin Sharudin, Wen Han Chen, Cedric Lim, Laura Wynter (Singapore Management University)
**TL;DR:** Studies orchestration mechanisms for tool-using AI agents in realistic customer-service workflows over unstructured knowledge bases. Shows that declarative agents — equipped with natural-language skill files appended to the system prompt — outperform both programmatic state-machine agents and unscaffolded baselines, offering a practical and interpretable orchestration paradigm.
**Tags:** `agents`, `tool-use`, `declarative-skills`, `knowledge-grounding`, `customer-service`

## [Future of Work with AI Agents: Auditing Automation and Augmentation Potential across the U.S. Workforce](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.06576)

## [MemoryVLA++: Temporal Modeling via Memory and Imagination in Vision-Language-Action Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09827)
**Authors:** Hao Shi, Weiye Li, Bin Xie, Yulin Wang, Renping Zhou, Tiancai Wang, Xiangyu Zhang, Ping Luo, Gao Huang (Submitted June 3, 2026)
**TL;DR:** Addresses temporal modeling limitations in Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models by combining episodic memory with imagination-based planning. Demonstrates improved robot task performance on long-horizon manipulation benchmarks by allowing the model to recall past interactions and anticipate future states before acting.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `VLA`, `robotics`, `memory`, `temporal-modeling`, `robot-manipulation`

## [iMaC: Translating Actions into Motion and Contact Images for Embodied World Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09813)
**Authors:** Zhenyu Wu, Xiuwei Xu, Yukun Zhou, Yifan Li, Qiuping Deng, Xiaofeng Wang, Zheng Zhu, Bingyao Yu, Ziwei Wang, Jiwen Lu, Haibin Yan
**TL;DR:** Proposes representing robot actions as "Motion and Contact Images" to enable embodied world models to predict physically plausible future states. This image-based action representation bridges the gap between language instructions and low-level physics, improving sample efficiency and generalization across manipulation tasks.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `world-models`, `robotics`, `manipulation`, `action-representation`

## [Robot-R1: Reinforcement Learning for Enhanced Embodied Reasoning in Robotics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.00070)
**Authors:** (Submitted June 2026)
**TL;DR:** Proposes Robot-R1, a reinforcement learning framework that enhances embodied reasoning in robotic systems tailored for low-level action control. Models trained with Robot-R1 achieve over a 28% improvement in embodied reasoning benchmarks, demonstrating that RL-based training signals can substantially improve spatial and causal reasoning in robotic agents.
**Tags:** `embodied-ai`, `reinforcement-learning`, `robotics`, `reasoning`, `action-control`
