## Highlights

### US
Ella Langley is the undisputed queen of the Hot 100 this week, holding the top two spots simultaneously with "Choosin' Texas" (now at its ninth non-consecutive week at #1, the longest-running chart-topper of 2026 so far) and "Be Her." Olivia Dean makes a transatlantic breakthrough with "Man I Need" debuting at #4 — a notable crossover from the UK scene. Meanwhile, the indie-meets-K-pop collab "Dracula" by Tame Impala & JENNIE vaulted into the top 10 at #10, marking historic firsts for both acts on the all-genre chart; JENNIE becomes only the second BLACKPINK member to crack the Hot 100 top ten as a solo artist.

### EU
The UK chart is crowned by the emotional folk-pop duet "Rein Me In" from hometown hero Sam Fender and rising British soul star Olivia Dean, continuing a strong run at #1. Olivia Rodrigo's punk-pop scorcher "Drop Dead" holds firm at #2, showing consistent transatlantic strength. Justin Bieber's surprise double-entry (both a Nicki Minaj collab and a solo track "Daisies" at #6) signals a major commercial comeback in the European market. Tame Impala & JENNIE's "Dracula" lands at #4, buoyed by a viral TikTok trend that has swept across the continent.

### CN
Jay Chou's comeback album *Children of the Sun* (太阳之子), released March 25, 2026 — his first in four years — continues to dominate China's streaming landscape in May, having sold over two million copies on QQ Music alone. The rap-influenced "Sicily" and the romantic ballad "The Day It Rained" are the album's standout streaming hits. Yu Shuxin's surprise drop "人间最值得" (released without advance promotion on April 21) hit #1 on the Global Chinese Music Chart in under 30 minutes after release, reflecting the growing power of C-drama stars in music. Drama OST specialist Liu Yu Ning rounds out the top five with his charismatic "装模作样."
