## Highlights

### US

The biggest story on the Billboard Hot 100 this week is Ella Langley's historic dominance. "Choosin' Texas" holds at #1 for a ninth week — the year's longest-running chart-topper — while "Be Her" climbs to a new #2 high, making Langley the first woman known primarily for country music ever to claim the Hot 100's top two slots simultaneously in the chart's 67-year history. Olivia Rodrigo's gothic-pop banger "Drop Dead" continues its strong run at #3 after debuting at #1 on May 2, a track that gave Rodrigo her fourth career number one. Bruno Mars' "I Just Might," a disco-pop funk anthem that debuted at #1 back in January, remains a fixture in the top five months after release — a testament to its evergreen streaming appeal.

### EU

The UK chart is equally story-rich. Sam Fender and Olivia Dean's indie-folk duet "Rein Me In" enters its tenth consecutive week at #1, tying Rihanna and Jay-Z's "Umbrella" for the longest-running UK #1 by a mixed-gender duo. The song, originally from Fender's 2025 album *People Watching*, picked up a BRIT Award for Song of the Year in 2026. Tame Impala's brooding, synth-laden "Dracula" — bolstered by the band's current European tour and a collaboration with K-pop star JENNIE — holds firm at #2, their highest-ever UK chart position. A streaming revival for Justin Bieber and Nicki Minaj's 2012 hit "Beauty and a Beat" at #4 underscores how catalog tracks continue to punch above their weight in the streaming era.

### CN

Yu Shuxin (Esther Yu), better known as an actress, stunned the Chinese music world when her surprise drop "世界上最短暂的事" (The Most Fleeting Thing In The World) — released on April 21 with zero promotional lead-up — reached #1 on QQ Music in under 30 minutes, amassing over 41,000 simultaneous listeners on release. The emotional ballad's lyrical reflection on impermanence struck a deep chord with young Chinese audiences and spread virally across Douyin. Zi Yu and Jolin Tsai continue to anchor the upper chart with their respective anthems, while Jay Chou's streaming catalog remains a perennial force, consistently driving millions of plays per week across QQ Music and NetEase Cloud Music.
