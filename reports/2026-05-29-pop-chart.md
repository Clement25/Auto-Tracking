## Highlights

### US
Drake's *ICEMAN* trilogy drop turned the Billboard Hot 100 into a personal takeover this week: he placed 42 songs on the chart simultaneously — a new all-time record — and "Janice STFU" logged its 10th week at #1 (non-consecutive), giving him his 14th solo Hot 100 chart-topper and surpassing Michael Jackson's record for the most solo male #1s. The trap-heavy banger pulled 40.7 million streams, while "Ran to Atlanta" (feat. Future & Molly Santana) trailed closely at 38.5 million. The only non-Drake entry in the top 5 is Ella Langley's country-pop crossover "Choosin' Texas," which has spent eight weeks in the top 5 and remains a remarkable outlier in an otherwise Drake-dominated chart cycle.

### EU
The UK chart this week showcases indie-pop and alternative guitar acts defying the usual streaming-pop monoculture. Sam Fender & Olivia Dean's delicate indie-folk collaboration "Rein Me In" holds the top spot, blending Fender's anthemic Springsteen-influenced sound with Dean's jazz-tinged vocals. Olivia Rodrigo storms back with "The Cure" debuting at #2, signalling another strong album campaign. Michael Jackson's 1983 disco-funk classic "Billie Jean" re-entered the top 3, likely boosted by renewed interest from the Michael Jackson biopic in theaters. Veteran British act The Chemical Brothers and cult psychedelic outfit Tame Impala round out the top 5, reflecting the UK's enduring appetite for alternative and electronic sounds.

### CN
Jay Chou (周杰伦) continues to dominate Chinese streaming following the March 2026 release of his album *太阳之子 (Son of the Sun)*. "那天下雨了" (It Rained That Day), a melancholic R&B ballad, sits at #1 on QQ Music's Popularity Index with a 138% popularity score, while charting #5 on the overall 热歌榜. The top of the 热歌榜 is led by Ren Jun's melodic "同谋者的默契," which blends neo-soul with Mandopop production, and 刘珂矣's traditional-influenced "半壶纱," a slow-burn folk piece that has been steadily climbing for weeks. 宝石Gem's rap-driven "枪火" (#4) reflects the continued Douyin-fueled virality of rap-flavored pop in mainland China.
