## Highlights

### US
Ella Langley is the defining artist of 2026 on the US charts, simultaneously holding **#1 with "Choosin' Texas"** (its 12th week at the top — breaking Morgan Wallen's 2023 record for most #1 stints by a single title) and **#3 with "Be Her,"** while also charting 15 songs simultaneously on the Hot 100. Taylor Swift's **"I Knew It, I Knew You"** — the lead single from the *Toy Story 5* soundtrack, giving her a record-tying 15th career Hot 100 #1 — holds at #2 after debuting at the summit. Drake's **"Janice STFU"** (from his ICEMAN trilogy released May 15) rests at #4 weeks after debuting with a record 42 simultaneous Hot 100 entries and 40.7M first-week streams, while British breakout Olivia Dean rounds out the top 5 at #5 with **"Man I Need,"** the first song by a woman to top both Pop and Adult R&B radio simultaneously since Adele's "Hello."

### EU
**"Rein Me In"** by Sam Fender & Olivia Dean continues its historic run at the top of the UK Official Singles Chart — approximately its 15th non-consecutive week at #1, tying Wet Wet Wet's "Love Is All Around" (1994) for the longest UK chart reign by a British act. The indie-rock/soul duet won Song of the Year at the 2026 BRIT Awards. Michael Jackson's **"Billie Jean"** (1982) roars back at #2, its resurgence fueled by the global box-office hit *Michael* biopic, which recently pushed it to #1 on the Billboard Global 200 — shattering records for longest rise to a Global chart summit. Harry Styles' disco-pop **"American Girls"** (from his fourth album, March 2026) holds at #3, while American country's EU crossover continues with Ella Langley's "Choosin' Texas" at #4. Olivia Rodrigo's sharp breakup anthem **"Stupid Song"** (from her third album, June 2026, featuring Robert Smith of The Cure on a separate track) debuts in the top 5 at #5.

### CN
Wang Junkai (王俊凯, former TF Boys member) delivered one of the most dominant album launches in recent Chinese streaming history: his debut solo album **WJK** (released July 8) swept the entire top 3 of QQ Music's 热歌榜 the very next day. **"破晓 (Breaking Dawn)"** leads at #1 with a 192% play-count surge — an atmospheric ballad that sparked viral Weibo debate after fans noticed a co-writer credit sharing a name with celebrity Wang Yibo. **"Badi Badi"** (#2, +181%) is an infectious dance-pop track praised for its live-show potential, while **"下一天 (Next Day)"** rounds out a historic top-3 clean sweep (#3). The K-pop/hip-hop influence remains strong: Korean royalty Yoon Mirae, Tiger JK, and Bizzy chart at #4 with the soulful bilingual **"ANGEL (天使)."** Completing the top 5, Hong Kong legend Karen Mok's (莫文蔚) 1999 classic **"阴天 (Cloudy Day)"** — a Golden Melody Award winner — stages a nostalgia-driven comeback at #5 after going viral on Douyin.

## Cross-Regional Notes
- **Ella Langley** appears on both the US chart (#1 + #3) and UK chart (#4) — country's most remarkable crossover moment since 2023.
- **Olivia Dean** holds #5 in the US ("Man I Need") and #1 in the UK (with Sam Fender), making her the week's most cross-market artist.
- **Michael Jackson's catalog** continues to benefit from the *Michael* biopic, proving a prestige film can revive 40-year-old songs to #2 in the UK.
- **Wang Junkai's album release** (July 8) drove a top-3 sweep on China's biggest streaming chart within 24 hours — a pattern typical of coordinated idol fandom campaigns.
