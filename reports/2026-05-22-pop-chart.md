## Highlights

### US
Ella Langley is dominating the US charts in a historic fashion this week. "Choosin' Texas" notched its **10th week at #1** on the Billboard Hot 100, making it the longest-running chart-topper by a female country artist in the chart's history. The twang-heavy anthem — co-written with Miranda Lambert and produced with Ben West — rode a viral TikTok wave of misheard lyrics to explosive mainstream popularity after its October 2025 release. Langley simultaneously holds both the #1 and #2 spots (with "Be Her"), joining Taylor Swift as the only solo female acts to achieve that feat. British singer Olivia Dean makes a strong US crossover showing with two entries in the top 5, while Bruno Mars' "I Just Might" continues its chart run, boosted further by a Latin version.

### EU
The UK chart this week blends contemporary British indie, K-pop crossover, and a massive nostalgia wave driven by the **Michael** biopic. Sam Fender and Olivia Dean's heartfelt duet "Rein Me In" holds the top spot, cementing both artists as dominant forces in British indie-pop. The Tame Impala x JENNIE (BLACKPINK) "Dracula" remix, released in February 2026, continues to climb after going viral on TikTok and becoming Tame Impala's first and JENNIE's fifth global top-ten hit. Michael Jackson's "Billie Jean" (#3) and "Beat It" (#5) have surged back into the chart following the release of the biographical film *Michael*, introducing his catalogue to new audiences and bringing legacy fans back to streaming.

### CN
China's chart this week is led by actress-turned-pop-star Yu Shuxin, whose surprise drop "The Most Fleeting Thing In The World" (世界上最短暂的事) hit #1 on the Global Chinese Music Chart in under 30 minutes of release, amassing over 41,000 listeners on day one with zero advance promotion. Drama OSTs continue to dominate the Chinese market, with Liu Yuning and Jackson Wang both charting via their contributions to popular period dramas. Folk-influenced singer-songwriter Mao Buyi rounds out the top 5 with "Dai Wo Ru Chu," a deeply emotional track that resonated widely on short-form platforms, while Suki's "Shi Mian" re-emerged through Douyin edits and cover trends, highlighting how short-form video continues to shape streaming patterns in China.
