## Highlights

### US
Taylor Swift continues her dominance with "I Knew It, I Knew You" holding the #1 spot for a second consecutive week, cementing her status as the summer's biggest act. Ella Langley makes a strong country-pop crossover showing with two entries in the top five — "Choosin' Texas" (#2) and "Be Her" (#5). Olivia Rodrigo dominates the rest of the chart with a remarkable three tracks in the Hot 100 top ten simultaneously ("Stupid Song," "Drop Dead," "Honeybee"), signaling a powerful album cycle moment for the pop-rock artist.

### EU
The UK chart tells a cross-genre story this week. Indie-folk duo Sam Fender & Olivia Dean land the top spot with the emotionally charged "Rein Me In," reflecting European audiences' appetite for guitar-driven storytelling. The surprise highlight is Michael Jackson's classic "Billie Jean" climbing back to #3, likely driven by a viral social media moment or sync placement. Olivia Rodrigo also crosses the Atlantic successfully, placing three songs in the UK top five and demonstrating her global commercial reach.

### CN
The Chinese charts reflect the country's distinct taste for melodic, emotionally resonant domestic pop. NetEase chart-topper "海屿你" (Sea and Island, You) by 马也_Crabbit is a soft indie-pop track with nostalgic marine imagery that resonated widely on social platforms. Gareth.T's English-language "glass" shows rising appetite for bilingual or crossover artists among younger Chinese listeners. Meanwhile, Douyin continues to be a powerful hitmaker: "趁早(忘了就好)" by 小仓鼠要早睡 and "爱的回归线" by 陈开心 both broke through via short-video virality, a pattern that increasingly defines Chinese pop chart behavior in 2026.
