## Highlights

### US
Taylor Swift debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard Hot 100 (week of June 20) with "I Knew It, I Knew You," a heartfelt ballad from the Toy Story 5 soundtrack — her 15th career chart-topper. The song ended Ella Langley's historic run: her country crossover hit "Choosin' Texas" had dominated for 10 weeks at No. 1, and she remains the only artist in Hot 100 history to hold the top two spots simultaneously as a primarily country act, with "Be Her" still parked at #4. Drake's "Janice STFU" (a viral diss-adjacent rap track) holds firm at #3, and Ariana Grande's glossy electro-pop anthem "Hate That I Made You Love Me" rounds out the top five.

### EU
Ariana Grande's "Hate That I Made You Love Me" topped the UK Official Singles Chart (week ending June 12, 2026), her eighth UK No. 1 — placing her level with Kylie Minogue, Oasis, and The Rolling Stones on the all-time British chart. The rest of the top five reflects the UK's eclectic tastes: Ellie Goulding returns with the cinematic synth-pop of "Black Prada Dress" (#2), while newcomer DARA makes a statement with the Afrobeats-inflected "Bangaranga" (#3). Zara Larsson's atmospheric "Midnight Sun" (#4) continues a strong Swedish pop showing, and Sam Fender & Olivia Dean's beloved indie-soul duet "Rein Me In" — which logged 13 non-consecutive weeks at #1 earlier in 2026 — keeps its grip at #5.

### CN
China's charts this week showcase a split between international cross-genre collaborations and viral domestic hits. QQ Music's 流行指数榜 (Popularity Index Chart) is led by "92% ANGEL (天使)" — a bilingual banger from Korean-American rapper Yoon Mirae (尹美莱) alongside Tiger JK and Bizzy, blending hip-hop and R&B — with the emotional ballad "相遇" by rising C-pop singer 卓沅 close behind at #2. On NetEase Cloud Music's Hot Chart, the folk-tinged "海屿你" by 马也_Crabbit & Cole先生 sits at #1, driven by massive Douyin virality after the track soundtracked a wave of nostalgic travel videos. Veteran Mandopop star 李荣浩 (Li Ronghao) sustains his perennial chart presence with the smooth ballad "恋人," underscoring the enduring commercial power of polished, emotionally resonant C-pop.
