## Highlights

### US
The Billboard Hot 100 (chart dated July 4, 2026) is dominated by country-pop crossover star Ella Langley, who claims three of the top ten positions — #1 "Choosin' Texas" (11th non-consecutive week at #1, the year's longest run so far), #3 "Be Her," and #9 "I Can't Love You Anymore" (feat. Morgan Wallen). Olivia Rodrigo is the other major force: her album *You Seem Pretty Sad for a Girl So in Love* (released June 12) placed lead single "Drop Dead" (her fourth #1 debut) and third single "Stupid Song" firmly in the top five. Taylor Swift's country-pop gem "I Knew It, I Knew You" — written for *Toy Story 5* in a single eight-hour session with Jack Antonoff — holds at #2 and marks her 15th career Hot 100 #1.

### EU
The UK Official Singles Chart (week ending July 2, 2026) is headlined by the slow-burn phenomenon "Rein Me In" by Sam Fender & Olivia Dean, now in its 15th non-consecutive week at #1 — tying Wet Wet Wet's 1994 record as the longest-running #1 by a British act. The duet made chart history earlier this year by spending 35 consecutive weeks in the top 40 before finally hitting the summit in February, the longest climb to #1 ever recorded. Olivia Rodrigo's new album has made an immediate impact in the UK as well, with three tracks in the top five simultaneously. Ariana Grande's moody mid-tempo debut "Hate That I Made You Love Me" (her 10th US #1, released May 29) and Harry Styles' disco-pop "American Girls" continue to chart strongly across European markets.

### CN
China's streaming landscape this week reflects a mix of viral indie pop and major Mandopop. On NetEase Cloud Music (206 million MAU), "海屿你" (Haiyu Ni) by 马也_Crabbit holds the top hot-songs position — a nostalgic C-pop ballad that sparked a massive cover trend on Douyin. Jay Chou's 16th studio album *Children of the Sun* (太阳之子, released March 26) has been the blockbuster of 2026, selling two million digital copies on QQ Music within two days of release and generating over 100 million RMB in cross-platform revenue; the title track continues to appear prominently across TME chart surfaces. Indie singer Gareth.T's ethereal "glass" and rising star 郑润泽's reflective "于是" round out the NetEase top three, pointing to a growing appetite for introspective indie-pop among younger listeners. Meanwhile, Zi Yu — China's 2025 Billboard Global No. 1 representative — remains a consistent TME UNI Chart performer, sustaining international visibility for Mandopop.
