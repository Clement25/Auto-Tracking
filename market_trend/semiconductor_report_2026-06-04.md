## Analysis & Outlook

### 1. Broadcom's Miss Sets a Dangerous Precedent for Valuation

Today's dominant story — AVGO down nearly 13% — is not a fundamental breakdown but a valuation recalibration. Broadcom's Q2 numbers were strong by any historical standard: 48% revenue growth, 143% AI chip growth, $10.8B in AI revenue. The problem is that at a $2T+ valuation, investors priced in a raised full-year guidance that didn't come. This creates a meaningful risk for every AI semiconductor company trading at elevated multiples: the market increasingly requires not just strong results but accelerating upside surprises. With AVGO reaffirming rather than raising its $100B+ AI semiconductor revenue target for FY2026, the sector faces a moment of recalibration. Investors would do well to distinguish between companies with true earnings acceleration (NVDA, MU, MRVL) and those with rich valuations priced for guidance upgrades.

### 2. TSMC Is the Indispensable Infrastructure — and Knows It

Taiwan Semiconductor's 4.8% surge today is a reflection of its unparalleled strategic position. TSMC is not merely a manufacturing vendor; it is the chokepoint through which nearly every cutting-edge AI chip must pass. The planned 15% 3nm price hike in H2 2026 (with further increases through 2027) signals extraordinary pricing power — TSMC can raise prices precisely because its customers (NVIDIA, AMD, Apple, Google, AWS) have no viable alternative for leading-edge logic. The integration of NVIDIA's cuLitho tool into TSMC's manufacturing process is particularly significant: it deepens the operational co-dependency between the world's most valuable company and its sole leading-edge foundry partner. TSM at $436.69 (market cap ~$1.96T) may still be undervalued relative to the structural moat it possesses.

### 3. Intel's Comeback Is Real, But the Easy Gains Are Behind

Intel's 250% YTD gain is the rotation story of 2026. Real catalysts underpin it: CHIPS Act funding, Apple foundry win, 18A yield progress ahead of schedule, and now the Foxconn rackscale AI infrastructure partnership. At $110.06 and $566B market cap, INTC commands a premium that increasingly prices in a best-case 18A yield recovery and foundry profitability. Morningstar's senior analyst warns that investors may be pricing best-case scenarios even as Intel faces fierce competition from AMD and Arm-based alternatives. The stock is up 445% over the trailing 12 months — the risk/reward is less compelling here than 90 days ago, and any foundry yield miss in 2H 2026 could trigger a sharp correction.

### 4. Micron and the Memory Supercycle — $1 Trillion and Growing

Micron's entry into the $1 trillion market cap club (52-week low: $103.38; current: $1,022.37) represents perhaps the most dramatic fundamental re-rating in semiconductor history. The catalyst is HBM3e: high-bandwidth memory has become as critical to AI GPU shipments as the logic die itself, and Micron alongside SK Hynix are the only suppliers. DRAM revenues industry-wide are projected to hit $418.6B in 2026 (+177% YoY per IDC). Today's -7.21% pullback is healthy consolidation after MU set an all-time closing high of $1,089.29 just one day prior. The supply constraint on HBM and advanced packaging remains the binding constraint on how many AI accelerators ship globally — Micron's pricing power is structurally intact.

### 5. Stocks to Watch and Key Risks Going Forward

**Stocks to watch:** MRVL ($317.66) remains the most compelling risk/reward in custom ASIC silicon — the NVIDIA endorsement and $10B+ pipeline through 2029 are not yet fully priced in at a $192B market cap. CDNS hitting all-time highs on autonomous AI design capabilities signals that EDA software companies are a beneficiary of the AI design cycle, not just a commodity vendor. KLAC's upcoming 10-for-1 split (June 12) will increase retail accessibility; the underlying business — process control equipment — is a must-have for every leading-edge fab running.

**Key risks:** (1) AVGO's miss creates near-term overhang for the entire sector; any further guidance disappointment from hyperscalers could trigger a sector-wide multiple compression. (2) TSMC's pricing hikes will eventually flow through to NVIDIA, AMD, and Apple's cost structures — margin pressure downstream is underappreciated. (3) Intel's valuation requires near-flawless execution on 18A through 2027; execution risk remains elevated. (4) Geopolitical risk around Taiwan remains the sector's existential tail risk, particularly as TSMC's strategic importance to global AI infrastructure deepens. (5) The semiconductor market's projected $1.5T size in 2026 assumes AI capital expenditure continues at pace — any macro shock or hyperscaler spending pause would have an outsized impact.
